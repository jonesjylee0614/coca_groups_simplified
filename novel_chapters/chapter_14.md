Walking back with his groceries, Alex decided to explore a **bit** more before heading home. "—and that's why we need to organize," the speaker was saying. "They want to **talk** about **crime** and **weapon**s in this neighborhood, but that isn't the **real** problem. A woman **notice**d him standing at the edge of the crowd. "You **here** for the meeting?"

"I was just passing by," Alex admitted. "What's the organization?"

"Just moved to the area. Few blocks that **way**."

"You should **join** us. We meet every week. Here, **take** a flyer." She handed him a sheet explaining their mission and meeting schedule.

"I'm actually a journalist," Alex said. "Or trying to be. Would it be okay if I **attend**ed to **maybe** write a story **about** what you're doing?"

"Tell you what—come to a meeting **first** just as a community member, not as a journalist. **See** what we're **about**. **If** you still want to **write** **something** after that, we can **talk**. **Anyway**, it's good to have new people interested."

Alex agreed and pocketed the flyer. This was exactly the kind of **story** he wanted to tell—communities organizing for **change**, voices that mainstream media often ignored.

He continued walking, his **brain** processing everything he'd observed. The city was **various** layers of **reality** stacked on **top** of each other. "Sorry!" they both said simultaneously, then **laugh**ed.

"Fine. In a hurry?"

"Always. I'm trying to **catch** the **light** before it gets too **dark**. Shooting a documentary **about** street art in the neighborhood. You seen the **new** mural on Seventh Avenue?"

"Oh, you should definitely check it **out**. Artist goes by the **name** 'Shadow'—does incredible work. Very political, lots to say **about** gentrification and community **memory**. I'm Zara, by the **way**."

"Journalist? We should **talk** sometime. I'm always **look**ing for people to collaborate with. Documentary and written journalism can complement each other. Here's my card—**shoot** me an email if you want to **discuss** working together **sometime**."

She rushed off, leaving Alex holding another unexpected connection. The city, he was discovering, wasn't just **various** **people** and places—it was also **various** networks and possibilities, connections waiting to be made.

Back at the apartment, Priya was in the kitchen preparing dinner. "**How** was your **day** of exploring?"

"Overwhelming in the **best** **way**," Alex said, setting his groceries down. "I met a woman who's lived **here** forty years, found a community organizing group, and ran into a documentary filmmaker. All in about **three** hours."
