## ğŸ“– Reading Passage: The Wooden Portrait of Grandfather

Emily walked through the **wooden** door of the old house, carefully examining each **thread** of memories woven into the walls. She had come to her **grandfather**'s home for a special **matter** - to find his **portrait** that had disappeared during the **final** days before he passed away.

The **subject** of her search was **frankly** important. Her **grandfather** had been a **fellow** artist who painted with such skill that his work was displayed at many **exhibition**s. However, the **legacy** he left behind wasn't just art, but a **wish** to see his family united, not divided by the **wound**s of old arguments.

"I **wish** I could find it," Emily said to herself, feeling the **painful** weight of loss. The **portrait** showed her **grandfather** in his **uniform** from when he served in the **legislature**, but it also showed something more - the **calm** wisdom in his eyes, despite the **stress** of his responsibilities.

She checked the **margin** of each painting on the wall, hoping to **derive** some clue. "Don't be a **fool**," she told herself. "Think like **grandfather** would think. Where would he hide something precious?"

As she searched, the room seemed to speak in a **loud** voice of memories. There was the old chair where he would sit, the **mask** he wore at the **catholic** festival, and the books about **coal** mining - his first **profession** before politics. Each item had its own story, each telling her: Don't **confuse** the past with the present.

**Hence**, she decided to check the basement. The stairs were dark, and the atmosphere felt almost **dumb** with silence. But as she descended, she noticed something strange - a **wooden** box marked with **zero** dust, as if someone had recently touched it.

Inside the box was not just the **portrait**, but a letter. Her **grandfather** wrote: "My dear **fellow** family members, I know there has been a **decrease** in our love for each other. The **formation** of new **wound**s has been **painful** to watch. **Frankly**, I **wish** you could see that these arguments are a **tactic** that only brings us to **zero** happiness."

The letter continued: "I'm not trying to **characterize** anyone as wrong. Each of you has the **privilege** to your own opinions. But **furthermore**, remember that family is the **uniform** that binds us together, not divides us. The **scope** of our differences should never **exceed** the **scope** of our love."

Emily read about how her **grandfather** had tried to **sustain** family unity despite the **inflation** of conflicts. He wrote about a **hunting** trip where all the siblings went together, before the **wound** of their **father**'s **legacy** created division. That was the **final** happy moment he wanted them to remember.

"The **harm** we do to each other is **temporary** if we choose to heal," the letter said. "Don't let your **flesh** and blood become strangers. The **nerve** it takes to forgive is the same **nerve** it takes to love. And that, my dear ones, is no **myth** - it's the **miracle** we need."

Emily felt her **stress** begin to **decrease**. She understood now that finding the **portrait** wasn't the real **subject** of her **grandfather**'s **wish**. It was about finding the **calm** to forgive, the courage to **sustain** relationships, and the wisdom not to **confuse** pride with principle.

She decided to **cancel** her plans to **resort** to **legal** actions against her cousins over the estate. **Hence**, she would invite everyone to a family **exhibition** of her **grandfather**'s art. **Physically**, the **portrait** showed an old man in a **uniform**. But **frankly**, it showed much more - it showed that the **margin** between love and hate is as thin as a **thread**, and we must choose carefully which side we **derive** our strength from.

That evening, as the sun cast **loud** shadows through the **wooden** windows, Emily made a **mask** of **calm** for herself. The **painful** memories were still there, but she chose not to let them **characterize** her family's future. She understood that her **grandfather**'s **legacy** wasn't the paintings or the house, but the **wish** for unity he held until his **final** breath.

She called her siblings and cousins, **frankly** telling them about the letter. "**Grandfather** left us more than **property**," she said. "He left us a **tactic** for happiness - forgive, love, and **sustain** each other. **Hence**, I'm inviting you all to come home. Not to fight over what's left, but to celebrate what we still have - each other."

The **silence** on the phone was like **zero** sound, but then, one by one, they agreed. The **formation** of old **wound**s could finally begin to heal. The **painful** **divorce** from family values could be reversed. And the **nerve** to forgive, which her **grandfather** had called a **miracle**, began to show itself.

As Emily hung the **portrait** back on its place above the **wooden** fireplace, she noticed something she hadn't seen before. In the **margin** of the painting, in tiny letters, her **grandfather** had written: "Love is the **uniform** we all wear. Don't **confuse** anything else **for** what **matters**."

She smiled, feeling the **stress** of years **decrease** into **calm**. Her **grandfather**'s **wish** was being fulfilled. **Frankly**, it wasn't about the **portrait** at all. It was about the **thread** that connects us, the **legacy** we choose to **sustain**, and the **miracle** of family that, despite all **harm** and **wound**s, can always be healed if we have the **nerve** to try.

The **wooden** house, which had seemed so full of **painful** memories, now felt like a **resort** of peace. The **exhibition** of love her **grandfather** planned had finally opened, and every family member was invited. **Furthermore**, they would make sure this **legacy** of love would not **fade** with time, but would **sustain** through generations.
