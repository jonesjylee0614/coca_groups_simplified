## ğŸ“– Reading Passage: Believe in Yourself

Nina **used** to **believe** that **success** **came** **from** **luck**. She'd **see** **others** **succeed** and **think**, "They're **just** **lucky**. I **could** **never** **do** that."

One day, her **coach** **asked** her: "Do you **believe** in **yourself**?"

Nina **hesitated**. **Did** she? She'd **never** **really** **thought** **about** it. She'd **seen** so **many** **people** **more** **talented** than her. She'd **heard** so **many** **voices** **saying** she **wasn't** **good** **enough**.

"I **used** to **believe** I **could** **succeed**," Nina **admitted**. "But **after** **seeing** **how** **many** **people** are **better** than **me**, I'm not **sure** **anymore**."

Her **coach** **shook** his **head**. "You're **comparing** **yourself** to **what** you **see** in **others**. But you **can't** **see** **their** **struggles**, their **doubts**, their **failures**. You **only** **see** the **final** **result**."

Nina **thought** **about** this. She'd **seen** her **classmate** **Li** **win** a **competition**. But she **hadn't** **seen** the **hundreds** of **hours** **Li** **spent** **practicing**. She'd **seen** her **friend** **Maria** **get** **accepted** to a **top** **university**. But she **hadn't** **seen** the **years** of **hard** **work** **Maria** had **put** in.

"I **see** **what** you **mean**," Nina **said**. "I **only** **see** the **success**, not the **process** **behind** it."

"**Exactly**," her **coach** **replied**. "And **because** you **only** **see** the **success**, you **believe** it **came** **easily** to **them**. You **used** to **believe** you **could** **succeed**. Don't **lose** that **belief**."

Nina **decided** to **change** **how** she **saw** **things**. Instead of **seeing** **others**' **success** and **feeling** **inadequate**, she **started** **seeing** it as **proof** that **success** was **possible**. If **they** **could** **do** it, **maybe** she **could** too.

"I **believe** I can **do** this," she **told** **herself** **every** **morning**. **At** **first**, the **words** **felt** **empty**. But **slowly**, **something** **changed**. She **used** to **doubt** **herself** **constantly**. Now she was **beginning** to **believe**.

She **used** this **new** **belief** to **push** **herself** **harder**. When **she** **failed**, instead of **seeing** it as **proof** she **wasn't** **good** **enough**, she **saw** it as **part** of the **learning** **process**.

**Months** **passed**. Nina **didn't** **become** the **best** **overnight**. But she **did** **improve**. And **more** **importantly**, she **started** to **truly** **believe** in **herself**.

"I **used** to **think** **belief** **didn't** **matter**," Nina **told** her **coach** one **day**. "I **thought** **only** **talent** **mattered**. But I **see** now that **believing** in **yourself** **makes** **everything** **possible**."

Her **coach** **smiled**. "**Belief** **alone** **isn't** **enough**. You **also** **need** **hard** **work**. But **without** **belief**, even **hard** **work** **can't** **help** you **reach** your **potential**."

Nina **nodded**. She'd **seen** this **truth** in her **own** **journey**. She **used** to **see** **limitations**. Now she **saw** **possibilities**. She **used** to **believe** she **couldn't**. Now she **believed** she **could**.

And that **belief** **made** **all** the **difference**.
