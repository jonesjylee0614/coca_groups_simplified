## ğŸ“– Reading Passage: The Ocean Wedding

The **wedding** was planned to take place on a beautiful summer day by the **ocean**. Maria and **Alexander**, a young couple from **Russia**, had chosen this **location** because they both loved the sea. The **sight** of the endless water and the sound of waves had always brought them **peace**.

"**Though** we've faced many **challenges**," Maria said to **Alexander** during their final preparation meeting, "I'm **glad** we chose this **location**. It's perfect."

**Alexander** **smiled**. "I **acknowledge** that planning a **wedding** is not **easy**. **But** seeing everything come together makes it **worthwhile**. This **circumstance** - standing here by the **ocean** with you - is exactly what I've always wanted."

The **wedding** planner, Ms. Thompson, was busy **organizing** the final details. She had to **balance** many **tasks** - the **meal** arrangements, the **sight** lines for **guests**, the **tape** for decorating, and ensuring everyone knew where to **sit**. **Though** she appeared **calm**, she was actually quite **stressed**.

"We need to **review** the **seating** **chart** one more time," Ms. Thompson said. "The **bride** and **groom's** families need to be **seated** **carefully**. We don't want any **awkward** **situations**."

Maria **nodded**. Her family included **members** from **different** **backgrounds** - some were **Soviet**-era **immigrants**, **others** were **native** **English** **speakers**. The **diversity** was beautiful **but** **required** **careful** **attention** to **cultural** **differences**.

"I **want** to **declare** something," Maria said to the small **gathered** **group**. "This **wedding** is not just about **Alexander** and me. It's about **bringing** **together** **two** families, **two** cultures. **Though** we come from **different** places, we **share** the same **desire** for **love** and **connection**."

Everyone **present** was **moved** by her words. **Alexander's** mother, who had been **quiet**, began to **cry** **softly**. "You're right," she said. "**Though** I was **initially** **worried** about **cultural** **differences**, I **see** **now** that **love** **transcends** all **boundaries**."

The **day** before the **wedding**, a **challenge** **emerged**. A **tape** of the **ceremony** **music** was **damaged**, and they needed to find a **replacement**. **Alexander** **took** **charge** of the situation. "I'll **locate** a **copy** of the music," he said. "Even **if** I have to **drive** to the next **town**."

**Though** time was **tight**, **Alexander** **managed** to find the music. On his **return**, he felt a deep sense of **accomplishment**. "Sometimes **challenges** **test** our **resolve**," he thought, "**but** they also show us what we're **capable** of."

The **wedding** **day** **arrived**. The **ocean** was **calm**, the **sky** was **clear**, and everything seemed perfect. **Guests** began to **arrive**, including **colleagues**, **friends**, and family **members** from near and far. Some had **traveled** from as far as **Russia**, showing their **support** for the couple.

The **ceremony** **began** at **sunset**. Maria walked down the **aisle** in her beautiful white **dress**, her **face** **glowing** with happiness. **Alexander** stood at the front, **watching** her **approach**, his **heart** **full** of **emotion**.

The **officiant** began the **ceremony**. "We are **gathered** here today to **witness** the **union** of Maria and **Alexander**. **Though** they come from **different** **backgrounds**, they **have** found in each **other** a **partner**, a **friend**, and a **soulmate**."

When it was time for the **vows**, Maria spoke first. "**Alexander**, **though** I cannot **predict** the **future**, I **declare** that I will **stand** by you through all **circumstances**. Whether **late** or **early**, in **calm** or in **storm**, I will be there."

**Alexander** **responded**, his voice **steady** **though** **emotional**. "Maria, you've **taught** me that **love** is not about **finding** someone **perfect**, **but** about **seeing** the **perfection** in someone **imperfect**. I **promise** to **honor** you, to **support** you, and to **love** you **forever**."

The **guests** **applauded**, and many **wiped** **tears** from their eyes. The **ceremony** continued with the **exchange** of **rings** and a **final** **declaration** from the **officiant**: "I now **declare** you husband and wife."

After the **ceremony**, **guests** moved to the **reception** area, where tables were **set** for a **grand** **meal**. The **menu** included **Russian** **dishes** and **American** favorites, **symbolizing** the **blend** of **cultures**. There was also plenty of **tea**, **beer**, and other **drinks**.

**As** the **evening** progressed, **speeches** were made. **Alexander's** **best** friend, a **cop** from their hometown, gave a **humorous** **speech** about their **friendship**. Maria's **sister** shared **childhood** **memories** and spoke about how **proud** she was of her **sister**.

"**Though** Maria and I **grew** up in a small **apartment**," her **sister** said, "she always **dreamed** **big**. She **never** let her **circumstances** **limit** her **aspirations**. And **now**, **seeing** her here, **surrounded** by **love**, I know she's **achieved** something truly **special**."

The **highlight** of the **reception** was the **first** **dance**. **As** Maria and **Alexander** **moved** **across** the **floor**, **guests** **watched** in **admiration**. The **couple** moved **gracefully**, their **connection** **visible** to everyone **present**.

**Later** in the **evening**, **though** most **guests** were **enjoying** the **celebration**, **Alexander's** **boss** pulled him aside. "I **need** to **grant** you something," he said. "**Though** I'm your **boss**, today I **speak** as a **friend**. Your **relationship** with Maria has **inspired** me. You've **shown** that **love** can **overcome** any **obstacle**."

**Alexander** was **touched**. "Thank you," he said. "That **means** a lot. **Though** work **has** been **challenging**, your **support** has **made** it **easier**."

**As** the **night** wore on, the **party** continued. **Guests** **danced**, **laughed**, and **celebrated**. The **ocean** **provided** a **beautiful** **backdrop**, its **waves** **creating** a **soothing** **rhythm** that **seemed** to **bless** the **union**.

**Late** in the **evening**, Maria and **Alexander** **walked** to the **edge** of the **ocean**. **They** stood **hand** in **hand**, **watching** the **moonlight** **dance** on the water. "**Though** I've **imagined** this **day** for years," Maria said, "the **reality** is even **better**."

"I **agree**," **Alexander** replied. "This is just the **beginning**. **Though** we'll **face** **challenges** ahead, I'm **confident** we can **handle** them **together**. That's the **beauty** of **partnership** - you're **never** **alone**."

**They** **kissed** **under** the **stars**, the **ocean** **witness** to their **love**. The **wedding** was **perfect** not because everything went **smoothly** - there had been **challenges** and **unexpected** **moments** - **but** because **it** **reflected** who they were: a couple who **could** **balance** **different** **cultures**, **who** **could** **face** **difficulties** with **grace**, and who **knew** that **love** was **worth** **fighting** for.

**As** the **celebration** **ended** and **guests** began to **leave**, Maria and **Alexander** felt **grateful**. The **wedding** had been more than a **ceremony** - it was a **declaration** of their **commitment**, a **celebration** of their **love**, and a **promise** of the **life** they would **build** **together**.

The **ocean** continued its **eternal** **rhythm**, **indifferent** to **human** **affairs** **but** somehow **present** as a **symbol** of **constancy** and **depth**. **Though** waves came and went, the **ocean** remained. And **though** **challenges** would come and **go** in Maria and **Alexander's** **life**, their **love** - like the **ocean** - would **endure**.
