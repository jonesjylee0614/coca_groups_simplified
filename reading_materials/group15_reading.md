"We need to **act** quickly," said Dr. Chen, a **medical** professional who had moved here recently. "The **population** is growing, and we must provide better care for **everybody**."

**Each** person in the meeting had a different **answer** to the problem. Some suggested building a new facility, while others wanted to improve the existing one. But the **common** **response** was that we needed something now.

"I have a **simple** idea," said Maria, a **worker** from the local clinic. "Why don't we start with a mobile health unit? It would be less **difficult** **than** building a new structure, and we could **act** immediately."

The **nature** of the problem was clear: the **poor** people in our town couldn't afford **medical** care. The **cause** was not just money, but also distance. Many lived far from the nearest hospital.

"We need to **act** quickly," Dr. Chen said. "The **population** is growing, and **each** day we wait means more people without proper care."
