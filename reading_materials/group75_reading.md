## ğŸ“– Reading Passage: The Rescue from Racism

On a **cold** winter morning in a **Korean** **cabin** deep in the forest, Professor Kim sat at his **dining** table, **brush**ing snow off his coat. He was a **liberal** thinker who had **dedicated** his life to fighting **racism** and promoting equality. The **cabin** was simple, with **brick** walls and an old **oak** door, but it served as a **sanctuary** for those who needed **rescue** from **discrimination**.

"I'm **pleased** to announce that our **donation** **campaign** has been successful," Professor Kim told his **mate**, Sarah, who worked as an **instructor** at the local university. "The **widespread** support shows that people are **gradually** **changing** their minds about **ideology** and **racism**."

Sarah nodded, her face showing **disappointment** about recent events. "But we can't **overlook** the fact that **racism** still exists. The **maximum** effort is needed to create **valid** change. We must **trigger** a **transformation** in how society views **diversity**."

Their **cabin** had become a **refuge** for students who faced **discrimination**. A **freshman** from **Korea** had recently arrived, **seeking** **rescue** from **bullying**. "I **feel** **disappointed**," the student said, sitting on a simple wooden chair. "People **judge** me based on **stereotypes**. They **punish** me for being **different**."

Professor Kim's **gut** feeling told him this was a **critical** moment. "We will not **overlook** this **abuse**," he said firmly. "The **racism** you've experienced is **unacceptable**. Our **organization** will provide **guidance** and support."

The **cabin**'s **dining** room became a **meeting** space for **donor**s and **activist**s. **Gradually**, they built a network of support. A **competitor** university even offered **partnership**, **pleased** to **contribute** resources. The **administrative** **staff** helped **coordinate** efforts to **combat** **racism**.

"**Wherever** there is **discrimination**, we will be there," Sarah declared at a **public** gathering. "The **widespread** **ideology** of **hate** must be **challenged**. We will **seize** every **opportunity** to **educate** and **flip** **negative** **perspective**s."

The **cabin** also served as a **classroom**. Professor Kim, an experienced **instructor**, would teach about **civil** **rights** **history**. He used a **brush** to write on a **dry** board, explaining how **racism** had been used to **punish** and **control** **minority** **group**s.

One **cold** evening, they received an **urgent** call. A **freshman** student was **trapped** in a dangerous situation, facing **abuse** from **racist** **attackers**. "We need to **rescue** him immediately," Sarah said, her voice **determined**. They didn't **skip** a **moment**.

Professor Kim **grabbed** his coat and headed into the **cold** night. His **gut** instinct proved **valid** - they found the student near an old **brick** building, **frightened** but **safe**. "You're **safe** now," Professor Kim said **gently**. "We'll **ensure** you get the **guidance** you need."

Back at the **cabin**, they sat around the **dining** table. The **rescued** student, still **shaken**, explained the **abuse** he'd **endured**. "I thought I was **alone**," he said. "I didn't know **where** to **turn**."

"You're not **alone**," Sarah assured him. "Our **cabin** is a **sanctuary**. **Wherever** **racism** exists, there are also people **fighting** against it. The **widespread** **movement** for **equality** is **growing** **gradually**."

The **organization** decided to **expand** their **rescue** program. They **renovated** part of the **cabin**, adding more **sleeping** **space**s and a **larger** **dining** area. **Donor**s **contributed** **funds**, **pleased** to support the **cause**. The **brick** **structure** was **strengthened**, making it a **permanent** **refuge**.

Professor Kim's **teaching** method was **unique**. He would **trigger** **discussion**s by **asking** **provocative** **question**s. "Why does **racism** **persist**?" he'd ask his **class**. "What **trigger**s **prejudice**? How can we **flip** **society**'s **mindset**?"

One **freshman**, a **liberal** student named Marcus, **contributed** a **valuable** **insight**. "**Racism** **resemble**s a **virus**," he said. "It **spread**s through **ideology** and **fear**. The only **vaccine** is **education** and **exposure** to **diversity**."

The **cabin**'s **dining** room hosted **weekly** **gathering**s. People from **various** **background**s would **share** **meal**s and **stories**. **Gradually**, **friendship**s formed. The **oak** table became a **symbol** of **unity**, **where** **difference**s were **celebrated** rather than **punished**.

"I'm **pleased** with our **progress**," Professor Kim said during a **reflection** **session**. "But we can't be **pleased** enough to **overlook** **ongoing** **challenge**s. **Racism** is **widespread**, and our **rescue** **mission** must **continue**."

Sarah, always the **pragmatic** **instructor**, added: "We need a **valid** **strategy**. We can't just **react** to **crisis**; we must be **proactive**. Let's **seize** the **initiative** and **trigger** **systematic** **change**."

They decided to **launch** an **educational** **program** in **school**s. The **freshman** **orientation** would include **workshop**s on **diversity** and **anti**-**racism**. **Instructor**s would receive **training** to **identify** and **address** **discrimination**. The **administrative** **support** was **crucial**.

A **disappointed** **donor** once **questioned** their **approach**. "Can **education** really **flip** **people**'s **views**?" he asked **skeptically**. Professor Kim's **response** was **thoughtful**: "**Gradually**, yes. **Change** doesn't **happen** **overnight**. But every **mind** we **open** is a **victory** against **racism**."

The **cabin** attracted **visitor**s from **widespread** **location**s. A **Korean** **delegation** came to **learn** about their **method**s. They were **pleased** to see how a **simple** **brick** **cabin** in the **forest** had become a **beacon** of **hope**.

"**Wherever** there is **darkness**, we **bring** **light**," Professor Kim told the **visitor**s. "This **cabin** may be **small**, but its **impact** is **widespread**. We've **rescued** many from the **abuse** of **racism**, and we'll **continue** to do so."

The **oak** door of the **cabin** was always **open**. On **cold** **morning**s, **smoke** would **rise** from the **chimney**, a **signal** that **warmth** and **safety** awaited **inside**. The **dining** table was always **set** for **one** more **person**, **symbolizing** **inclusion**.

Sarah worked **tirelessly** as an **instructor**, **teaching** **student**s to **recognize** and **challenge** **racism**. "Don't be **disappointed** when **progress** is **slow**," she'd tell them. "Every **step** **forward** is **valid**. Every **racist** **ideology** we **dismantle** makes the **world** **better**."

The **freshman** they had **rescued** became one of their most **active** **volunteer**s. "I want to **help** others **avoid** the **abuse** I **experienced**," he said. His **transformation** from **victim** to **advocate** was **inspiring**.

Professor Kim would often sit on the **cabin**'s **porch**, **looking** at the **oak** **tree**s. "**Racism** is like **dry** wood," he'd **reflect**. "It **burn**s **quickly** and **spread**s **easily**. But **compassion** is like **water** - it can **extinguish** the **flame**s of **hate**."

The **organization** faced **challenge**s. **Fund**s were **limited**, and **racism** was **widespread**. But they refused to be **disappointed**. "We will **seize** every **opportunity**," Sarah declared. "We won't **skip** any **chance** to **educate**, **rescue**, or **advocate**."

The **cabin**'s **brick** wall**s** held **story** after **story** of **rescue** and **recovery**. A **poster** on the wall read: "**Wherever** you come from, **whatever** your **background**, you are **welcome** here. We **fight** **racism** together."

As **winter** turned to **spring**, the **cold** gave way to **warmth**. The **oak** **tree**s **blossom**ed, and the **cabin** **garden** came to **life**. It was a **metaphor** for their **work** - **patient**, **gradual**, but ultimately **transformative**.

Professor Kim, now **elderly** but still **passionate**, continued his **instruction**. "**Racism** is not **inevitable**," he'd teach. "It's a **learned** **behavior**, which means it can be **unlearned**. Our **job** is to **trigger** that **unlearning** through **education** and **compassion**."

The **dining** table **conversation**s were **rich** with **insight**. **People** would **share** **experience**s of **overcoming** **prejudice**. **Gradually**, a **community** formed - **diverse**, **inclusive**, and **united** against **racism**.

"I'm **pleased** to see how far we've come," Professor Kim said at their **anniversary** **celebration**. "From a **simple** **cabin** to a **widespread** **movement**. We've **rescued** many, **educated** **more**, and **triggered** **important** **conversation**s about **racism** and **equality**."

Sarah raised her glass. "To every **person** who refused to **overlook** **injustice**. To every **donor** who **contributed**. To every **instructor** who **taught** **tolerance**. And to this **cabin** - our **sanctuary**, our **classroom**, our **home** in the **fight** against **racism**."

The **oak** door remained **open**, a **symbol** of **welcome** and **refuge**. The **brick** **cabin** stood **strong**, a **testament** to **resilience**. And **inside**, at the **dining** table, the **work** continued - **gradual**, **determined**, and **ultimately** **transformative** - a **rescue** mission against **racism** that would never **cease**.
