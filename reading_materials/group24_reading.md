Dr. Martinez **asked** her **students** a **simple** **question**: "**Why** do **people** **ask** **questions**?"

The **students** **looked** at **each** **other**. It **seemed** like an **easy** **question**, but the **more** they **thought** **about** it, the **more** **complex** it **became**.

"To **get** **answers**?" one **student** **suggested**.

"To **learn**?" **another** **added**.

Dr. Martinez **smiled**. "Those are **good** **reasons**. But **let** me **ask** you **this**: **Do** all **questions** **need** **answers**?"

This **question** **surprised** **them**. **Why** **would** you **ask** a **question** if you **didn't** **want** an **answer**?

"**Some** **questions**," Dr. Martinez **explained**, "are **asked** not to **get** **answers**, but to **make** us **think**. When I **ask** '**Why** do we **exist**?', I'm not **expecting** a **simple** **answer**. I'm **inviting** **deeper** **thought**."

One **student** **raised** her **hand**. "So **some** **questions** are **more** **important** than their **answers**?"

"**Exactly**," Dr. Martinez **nodded**. "The **right** **question** can **open** your **mind**. It can **make** you **see** **things** **differently**. **That**'s **why** **asking** **good** **questions** is an **art**."

She **asked** **them** to **practice**: "**Think** of a **question** that has no **easy** **answer**, but **makes** you **think** **deeply**."

The **students** **thought** **carefully**. **Finally**, one **asked**: "**Why** do we **fear** what we don't **understand**?"

"That's a **great** **question**," Dr. Martinez **said**. "**Notice** how it **opens** up **many** **directions** for **thought**. **That**'s the **power** of a **good** **question**."

By the **end** of **class**, **students** **understood**: **asking** **questions** wasn't **just** **about** **getting** **answers**. It was **about** **opening** **doors** to **understanding**.

"**Keep** **asking** **questions**," Dr. Martinez **told** them. "Even **when** you don't **expect** **perfect** **answers**. **Especially** **then**. **Because** those are the **questions** **worth** **asking**."
