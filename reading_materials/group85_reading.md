## Reading Passage: The Pottery Studio **Above** the City

Emma looked at the **calendar** on the wall. It was **noon**, and she needed to finish one **last** pottery piece before the **Olympics** art exhibition. The **continuous** rain outside made the studio feel cozy, but she had to **worry** about the **landing** on the old wooden stairs—they needed **repair** after years of use.

Her pottery studio sat **above** a busy street in the old district, where **historically** many artists had worked. The windows were covered with thin **curtain**s that filtered the soft light. Emma's collection of clay pieces lined the shelves, some **identical** to ancient designs, others uniquely **hers**.

"This **ritual** is **delicious** in its own way," Emma thought as she sat at her wheel. She would take a morning **bath**, then **sneak** into her studio before most people woke up. The **mud**-colored **clay** felt cool in her hands as she shaped it. Her **supervisor** at the gallery had said they needed a certain **quantity** of pieces, but Emma wanted quality more than numbers.

The **reminder** note stuck to her workbench mentioned the exhibition's theme: **unity** through art. The **declaration** from the organizing committee was clear—artists should show how creativity can **switch** perspectives and build connections. Emma felt the weight of this **quest** to create something **outstanding**.

As she worked, her **elbow** bumped the table, nearly knocking over a piece. "**Uh-huh**, that was close," she whispered. The design she was making had a **flat** base with a curved body, decorated with a **strip** of raised patterns. The **slope** of the sides was **pleasant** to touch, and she added a small **seal** with her initials at the bottom.

Her business partner, Marcus, was a young **entrepreneur** who handled sales. He had helped her **specify** which pieces to prioritize. "Don't **stumble** over perfection," he would say. "Focus on your vision." His advice was wise—she used to **regret** spending too much time on small details.

Emma's phone rang from the **outlet** where it was charging. It was Dr. Chen, an art history professor who studied **slavery** and freedom expressions in pottery. "Your work shows people are **immune** to giving up their dreams," she once told Emma. "Each piece in your **portfolio** tells a **mysterious** story."

As Emma finished the rim, carefully smoothing it with wet fingers, she felt satisfied. The piece wasn't perfect, but it was honest. She stood up, brushing her hands on her **skirt**, and placed the pottery on the drying **shelf**. Rain continued to fall outside, but inside, something beautiful had taken shape.

Tomorrow, she would **rent** a van to transport everything to the gallery. For now, she could rest, knowing she had created something meaningful. She wasn't trying to **manipulate** anyone's emotions—just share her truth through clay. Looking out the window at an **airplane** passing **above** the clouds, she smiled. The exhibition would be her **declaration** of artistic independence, a **reminder** that creativity and persistence create **unity** in unexpected ways.

## Story Summary

**Story Theme**:  
This story follows Emma, a pottery artist, as she completes her final piece for an important Olympics art exhibition. The narrative explores her creative process, the physical space of her studio, and her journey toward artistic confidence.

**Story Structure**:
1. **Opening**: Emma checks her calendar at noon, worrying about repairs needed in her studio above the city while racing against a deadline.
2. **Development**: We learn about Emma's daily ritual, her working process with clay, and her relationship with her supervisor and business partner Marcus.
3. **Climax**: Emma creates her final pottery piece, nearly making a mistake but recovering, while reflecting on advice from mentors and her own artistic vision.
4. **Conclusion**: Emma completes her work with satisfaction, planning to transport pieces to the gallery and feeling confident about her artistic declaration.

**Learning Objectives**:
1. Master descriptive vocabulary related to physical spaces (above, landing, slope, flat)
2. Learn temporal and habitual expressions (continuous, noon, last, calendar, ritual)
3. Understand business and creative vocabulary (entrepreneur, portfolio, quantity, supervisor)
4. Practice abstract concepts (unity, declaration, quest, regret, immune)

**Vocabulary Distribution**:
- **Location/Space words**: above, landing, slope, flat, outlet, curtain, skirt, strip, elbow
- **Time/Process words**: continuous, noon, last, calendar, historically, reminder
- **Art/Creation words**: clay, pottery, seal, portfolio, entrepreneur, quest
- **Emotion/Thought words**: worry, regret, pleasant, mysterious, delicious
- **Action words**: repair, sneak, scan, stumble, switch, manipulate, specify, rent

## Chinese Translation (中文翻译)

### 陶艺工作室在城市上空

艾玛看了看墙上的日历。现在是中午,她需要在奥运会艺术展览前完成最后一件陶艺作品。外面连续不断的雨让工作室感觉很舒适,但她不得不担心老木楼梯的平台——使用多年后需要修理了。

她的陶艺工作室位于老城区一条繁忙街道的上方,历史上许多艺术家都在这里工作过。窗户上挂着薄薄的窗帘,过滤着柔和的光线。艾玛的陶器作品收藏摆满了架子,有些与古代设计相同,有些则是她独特的创作。

"这种仪式感本身就很美妙,"艾玛坐在陶轮前想道。她会在早上洗个澡,然后在大多数人醒来之前偷偷溜进工作室。泥土色的陶土在她手中感觉很凉爽。画廊的主管说他们需要一定数量的作品,但艾玛更看重质量而不是数量。

贴在工作台上的提醒便条提到了展览的主题:通过艺术实现团结。组委会的声明很明确——艺术家应该展示创造力如何转换视角并建立联系。艾玛感受到了创作出色作品这一追求的重量。

在工作时,她的肘部撞到了桌子,差点打翻一件作品。"嗯哼,好险,"她低声说。她正在制作的设计有一个平坦的底座和弧形的器身,装饰着一条凸起的图案带。边缘的斜度摸起来很舒服,她在底部加了一个带有姓名首字母的小印章。

她的商业合作伙伴马库斯是一位年轻的企业家,负责销售。他帮她明确了哪些作品应该优先制作。"不要为完美而犯错,"他会说。"专注于你的愿景。"他的建议很明智——她以前常常后悔在小细节上花太多时间。

艾玛的手机在充电的插座旁响了。是陈博士打来的,她是一位研究陶艺中奴隶制和自由表达的艺术史教授。"你的作品表明人们不会放弃梦想,"她曾告诉艾玛。"你作品集中的每一件作品都讲述着一个神秘的故事。"

当艾玛完成边缘,用湿手指小心地抚平它时,她感到很满意。这件作品并不完美,但它是真诚的。她站起来,在裙子上擦了擦手,把陶器放在干燥架上。外面雨继续下着,但里面,美丽的东西已经成形。

明天,她会租一辆货车把所有东西运到画廊。现在,她可以休息了,知道自己创作了有意义的东西。她不是想操纵任何人的情绪——只是通过陶土分享她的真实。看着窗外一架飞机在云层上方飞过,她笑了。这次展览将是她艺术独立的宣言,提醒人们创造力和坚持以意想不到的方式创造团结。

## Key Vocabulary Annotations (重点词汇注释)

### Core Spatial & Temporal Words (核心空间和时间词汇)

1. **above** /əˈbʌv/
   - **词性**: prep./adv.
   - **用法**: 表示在...上方,高于
   - **记忆技巧**: a-bove(在上面升起),想象东西往上飘
   - **例句**: The studio sits above the street. (工作室在街道上方。)

2. **continuous** /kənˈtɪnjuəs/
   - **词性**: adj.
   - **用法**: 连续不断的,持续的
   - **记忆技巧**: con-tin-uous(一直延续的),想象连续剧
   - **例句**: The continuous rain lasted all day. (连续的雨下了一整天。)

3. **noon** /nuːn/
   - **词性**: n.
   - **用法**: 中午,正午
   - **记忆技巧**: noon看起来像12:00的两个0
   - **例句**: We'll meet at noon for lunch. (我们中午见面吃午饭。)

4. **last** /læst/
   - **词性**: adj./v.
   - **用法**: 最后的;持续
   - **记忆技巧**: 想象"拉住最后"一个机会
   - **例句**: This is my last chance to finish. (这是我完成的最后机会。)

5. **historically** /hɪˈstɒrɪkli/
   - **词性**: adv.
   - **用法**: 历史上,从历史角度
   - **记忆技巧**: history + ically = 历史地
   - **例句**: This area was historically important. (这个地区在历史上很重要。)

## Key Sentence Analysis (重点句子分析)

### Sentence 1
**"Her pottery studio sat above a busy street in the old district, where historically many artists had worked."**

**结构分析**:
- 主句: Her pottery studio sat above a busy street
- 地点状语: in the old district
- 定语从句: where historically many artists had worked

**语法点**:
- where引导定语从句修饰district
- 副词historically放在从句中间
- 过去完成时had worked表示过去的过去

**词汇搭配**:
- sat above: 位于...上方
- busy street: 繁忙的街道
- old district: 老城区

**学习重点**: 学习使用where引导地点定语从句,以及如何描述建筑物的位置关系

### Sentence 2
**"The reminder note stuck to her workbench mentioned the exhibition's theme: unity through art."**

**结构分析**:
- 主语: The reminder note (stuck to her workbench作定语)
- 谓语: mentioned
- 宾语: the exhibition's theme
- 同位语: unity through art

**语法点**:
- 过去分词stuck作后置定语修饰note
- 冒号引出同位语解释theme
- through表示方式"通过"

**词汇搭配**:
- reminder note: 提醒便条
- stuck to: 贴在
- through art: 通过艺术

**学习重点**: 学习使用过去分词作后置定语,以及冒号引出解释说明的用法

### Sentence 3
**"Emma's phone rang from the outlet where it was charging."**

**结构分析**:
- 主语: Emma's phone
- 谓语: rang
- 地点状语: from the outlet
- 定语从句: where it was charging

**语法点**:
- where引导定语从句修饰outlet
- 过去进行时was charging表示正在充电
- from表示声音来源

**词汇搭配**:
- rang from: 从...响起
- was charging: 正在充电

**学习重点**: 学习描述物体位置和状态的表达方式

### Sentence 4
**"Don't stumble over perfection; focus on your vision."**

**结构分析**:
- 第一部分: 否定祈使句 Don't stumble over perfection
- 第二部分: 肯定祈使句 focus on your vision
- 连接: 分号表示转折和强调

**语法点**:
- 祈使句表示建议或命令
- stumble over: 在...上犯错/绊倒
- focus on: 专注于

**词汇搭配**:
- stumble over: 在...上犯错
- focus on: 专注于
- your vision: 你的愿景

**学习重点**: 学习使用祈使句给出建议,以及分号连接对比的两个观点

### Sentence 5
**"The exhibition would be her declaration of artistic independence, a reminder that creativity and persistence create unity in unexpected ways."**

**结构分析**:
- 主句: The exhibition would be her declaration
- 同位语: a reminder (that从句作同位语说明reminder的内容)
- that从句: creativity and persistence create unity in unexpected ways

**语法点**:
- would be表示将来的预期
- of artistic independence作后置定语
- that引导同位语从句
- in unexpected ways作方式状语

**词汇搭配**:
- declaration of independence: 独立宣言
- a reminder that: 提醒...
- in unexpected ways: 以意想不到的方式

**学习重点**: 学习使用同位语从句解释抽象名词,以及如何表达深层含义

## Memory Techniques Summary (记忆技巧总结)

### 1. 空间位置词记忆法
- **above**: 想象"a在bove上面"—— 物体在上方
- **landing**: land(陆地) + ing = 降落的平台
- **slope**: slow(慢) + up = 慢慢上坡
- **flat**: 想象"拍平flat"的动作

### 2. 时间过程词记忆法
- **continuous**: continue(继续) + ous = 持续不断的
- **noon**: 看起来像12:00的两个0
- **last**: 拉住"last"最后的机会
- **calendar**: 召唤"call"日期的东西

### 3. 动作词记忆法
- **repair**: re(再) + pair(配对) = 重新配好
- **sneak**: 像蛇"snake"一样偷偷爬行
- **stumble**: stone(石头) + tumble = 被石头绊倒
- **switch**: 开关"嗖地"一下切换

### 4. 材料物品词记忆法
- **clay**: 谐音"克雷" = 捏黏土
- **curtain**: certain(确定)遮住窗户

## Practice Suggestions (练习建议)

### 1. 朗读练习 (Reading Aloud Practice)
- **每日朗读**: 每天朗读全文3遍,注意语音语调
- **重点句子**: 特别练习含有多个目标词汇的句子
- **录音对比**: 录下自己的朗读,与标准发音对比
- **角色扮演**: 想象自己是Emma,带着情感朗读

### 2. 词汇替换练习 (Vocabulary Substitution)
- 将"above"替换为"over"或"higher than"
- 将"continuous"替换为"constant"或"ongoing"
- 将"pleasant"替换为"nice"或"agreeable"
- 尝试用同义词重写关键句子

### 3. 造句练习 (Sentence Creation)
- 用**above**造句: The birds fly above the trees.
- 用**repair**造句: I need to repair my bicycle this weekend.
- 用**entrepreneur**造句: She became a successful entrepreneur at age 25.
- 每个词汇至少造2-3个不同情境的句子

### 4. 情景对话练习 (Situational Dialogue)
**场景1: 在艺术工作室**
- A: "Where is your studio located?"
- B: "It's above a busy street in the old district."

**场景2: 讨论作品**
- A: "Do you worry about the deadline?"
- B: "Yes, but I won't stumble over perfection."

**场景3: 参观展览**
- A: "What quantity of pieces will you show?"
- B: "About twenty pieces from my portfolio."

### 5. 写作练习 (Writing Practice)
- **短文创作**: 用20个以上目标词汇写一个200词的故事
- **日记写作**: 用目标词汇描述你的一天
- **描述练习**: 描述你的工作空间,使用above, flat, outlet等词
- **创意写作**: 想象你是一位艺术家,写出你的创作过程

### 6. 分类复习练习 (Categorical Review)
- **空间类**: above, landing, slope, flat, elbow
- **时间类**: noon, last, continuous, calendar, historically
- **动作类**: repair, sneak, stumble, switch, manipulate
- **情感类**: worry, regret, pleasant, mysterious, unity

### 7. 联想记忆练习 (Association Practice)
- 看到"clay"就想到"pottery"和"artist"
- 看到"calendar"就想到"deadline"和"schedule"
- 看到"entrepreneur"就想到"business"和"innovation"
- 建立词汇之间的关联网络

### 8. 实用应用练习 (Practical Application)
- **购物场景**: 使用rent, quantity, outlet等词
- **工作场景**: 使用supervisor, specify, portfolio等词
- **日常生活**: 使用bath, curtain, skirt, calendar等词
- **艺术讨论**: 使用clay, seal, unity, declaration等词
