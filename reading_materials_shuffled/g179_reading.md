## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Professor James stood **below** /bɪˈloʊ/ (prep./adv. 在...下面) the ancient **oak** /oʊk/ (n. 橡树) tree on campus, his **iPad** /ˈaɪpæd/ (n. 平板电脑) in hand. He had been invited to give a **debate** /dɪˈbeɪt/ (n./v. 辩论) on whether **revolution** /ˌrevəˈluːʃn/ (n. 革命；旋转) in education was necessary or dangerous. The question made him think of his grandfather, who had survived a real **revolution** and kept telling him: "Stay **alive** /əˈlaɪv/ (adj. 活着的) by finding your **purpose** /ˈpɜːrpəs/ (n. 目的；用途)."

James opened his notes, written in a **formula** /ˈfɔːrmjulə/ (n. 公式；配方) he'd developed over years: observe, question, adapt. His teaching approach didn't **distinguish** /dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ/ (v. 区分；辨别) between old and new methods rigidly. Instead, he believed in **picking** /ˈpɪkɪŋ/ (v. 挑选；采摘) the best from both. But lately, he felt **tired** /ˈtaɪərd/ (adj. 疲倦的), like a **pitcher** /ˈpɪtʃər/ (n. 投手；水壶) that had poured out too much.

At the **debate**, James sat with **five** /faɪv/ (adj./n. 五) other professors, plus **one** more joining via video—**six** /sɪks/ (adj./n. 六) total. His opponent, Professor Lee, argued that traditional education provided **comfort** /ˈkʌmfərt/ (n./v. 舒适；安慰) and stability. "Students need **reference** /ˈrefrəns/ (n. 参考；提及) points," Lee said. "Too much change creates confusion."

James disagreed. "What's **interesting** /ˈɪntrəstɪŋ/ (adj. 有趣的) is that you assume comfort equals learning," he replied. "But real growth comes from **violation** /ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃn/ (n. 违反；侵犯) of our comfort zones. My grandfather escaped a **revolution** with nothing but **survived** because he kept learning, kept adapting."

He described his teaching **workout** /ˈwɜːrkaʊt/ (n. 锻炼；练习): students didn't just memorize; they created. Last semester, a student who was quite **fat** /fæt/ (adj. 肥胖的) and struggled with confidence had written a brilliant essay on **capital** /ˈkæpɪtl/ (n. 首都；资本；大写字母). James had helped her **pick** the right words to express complex ideas. Her essay won an **exclusive** /ɪkˈskluːsɪv/ (adj. 独有的；专用的) scholarship.

"Education should **kick** /kɪk/ (v./n. 踢；激动) us into action," James continued, "not **lull** us into complacency. We need to **distinguish** what truly matters: not grades, but growth. Not memorization, but meaning."

Professor Lee responded with an **explanation** /ˌekspləˈneɪʃn/ (n. 解释；说明) about tradition's value. "Without **reference** to the past, we lose our foundation," he said. James nodded but added, "True. But without innovation, we lose our future."

After the **debate**, a young man approached James. "I'm a **pitcher** for the baseball team," he said, "and I do my philosophy readings during my morning **workout**. Your **debate** helped me understand my **purpose** better—to think while I train, to stay mentally **alive** while I stay physically strong." James smiled. The **oak** tree above cast shadows that moved with the wind, always changing yet always rooted.

That night, James typed on his **iPad** under his desk lamp. He wrote about how **revolution** doesn't always mean **violence** or chaos. Sometimes it means questioning old **formulas**, **picking** new paths, and helping students **distinguish** between what they're told to believe and what they discover for themselves. His grandfather would have approved. The **purpose** of education, James concluded, wasn't to **comfort** students—it was to help them stay truly **alive**, thinking and growing, no matter how **tired** the journey made them.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

詹姆斯教授站在校园里古老的橡树下,手里拿着他的平板电脑。他被邀请就教育革命是必要还是危险进行辩论。这个问题让他想起了他的祖父,祖父经历过真正的革命并不断告诉他:"通过找到你的目的来保持活着。"

詹姆斯打开他的笔记,用他多年来发展的一个公式写成:观察、质疑、适应。他的教学方法不会僵化地区分新旧方法。相反,他相信从两者中挑选最好的。但最近,他感到疲倦,就像一个投手倒出了太多水。

在辩论中,詹姆斯与其他五位教授坐在一起,另外一位通过视频加入——总共六位。他的对手李教授认为,传统教育提供舒适和稳定。"学生需要参考点,"李说。"太多的变化会造成混乱。"

詹姆斯不同意。"有趣的是,你假设舒适等于学习,"他回答。"但真正的成长来自违背我们的舒适区。我祖父逃离革命时一无所有,但幸存下来是因为他不断学习、不断适应。"

他描述了他的教学练习:学生不仅仅是记忆;他们创造。上学期,一个相当肥胖且缺乏自信的学生写了一篇关于资本的精彩文章。詹姆斯帮她挑选了合适的词语来表达复杂的想法。她的文章赢得了独家奖学金。

"教育应该激励我们行动起来,"詹姆斯继续说,"而不是让我们陷入自满。我们需要区分什么才是真正重要的:不是分数,而是成长。不是记忆,而是意义。"

李教授用关于传统价值的解释回应。"没有对过去的参考,我们就失去了基础,"他说。詹姆斯点头,但补充道:"是的。但没有创新,我们就失去了未来。"

辩论后,一个年轻人走近詹姆斯。"我是棒球队的投手,"他说,"我在早上锻炼时阅读哲学。你的辩论帮我更好地理解了我的目的——在训练时思考,在保持身体强壮的同时保持精神活力。"詹姆斯微笑了。上方的橡树投下的阴影随风移动,总是在变化但总是扎根。

那天晚上,詹姆斯在台灯下用平板电脑打字。他写道革命并不总是意味着暴力或混乱。有时它意味着质疑旧公式、挑选新路径,并帮助学生区分他们被告知要相信的和他们自己发现的。他的祖父会赞同的。詹姆斯总结说,教育的目的不是让学生感到舒适——而是帮助他们保持真正活着,思考和成长,无论旅程让他们多么疲倦。

**亮点:** 通过 "kick" 和 "lull" 的对比,以及 "action" 和 "complacency" 的对比,生动表达了教育的真正作用。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: By doing 表示方式

**原句:** "Stay alive by finding your purpose."

**聚焦:** "by + 动名词" 表示方式或手段

**用法说明:** 
"By doing" 结构回答"如何做"的问题,表示通过某种方式达到目的。"by" 是介词,后面必须接动名词(doing)而不是不定式(to do)。

**常见用法:**
- by working hard (通过努力工作)
- by practicing daily (通过每天练习)
- by asking questions (通过提问)

**可替换句:** "Stay alive through finding your purpose." (使用 "through",意思相近)

---

### 聚焦点 2: Not...but... 对比结构在教育语境中的应用

**原句:** "Education should kick us into action, not lull us into complacency."

**聚焦:** "not...but..." 否定前者肯定后者

**用法说明:**
这个结构用于纠正错误观念或强调正确做法。在本句中,两个动词短语 "kick...into..." 和 "lull...into..." 形成语义和结构的平行对比。

**结构分析:**
- should + 动词1 (肯定的行为)
- not + 动词2 (否定的行为)
- 两个短语结构完全平行

**类似表达:**
- Education should challenge, not comfort.
- Teachers should inspire, not indoctrinate.

**可替换句:** "Education should kick us into action instead of lulling us into complacency." (使用 "instead of",较口语化)
