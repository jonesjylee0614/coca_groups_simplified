## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The **wilderness** /ˈwɪldərnəs/ (n. 荒野) stretched for miles in every direction. Maya stood **apart** /əˈpɑːrt/ (adv. 分开地) from her team, looking at the map one more time. The **demand** /dɪˈmænd/ (n./v. 需求；要求) was simple but **not** /nɑːt/ (adv. 不) easy: find the rare plant before winter. She had **experienced** /ɪkˈspɪriənst/ (adj. 有经验的) many difficult missions, **nevertheless** /ˌnevərðəˈles/ (adv. 然而；尽管如此), this one felt different. **While** /waɪl/ (conj. 当...时；而) others saw only trees and **grass** /ɡræs/ (n. 草), she saw possibilities.

"We should **listen** /ˈlɪsn/ (v. 听) to what Dr. Park said," spoke her **companion** /kəmˈpænjən/ (n. 同伴), Tom. He was **not** a scientist but a **cook** /kʊk/ (n./v. 厨师；烹饪) hired to keep them fed. His **involvement** /ɪnˈvɑːlvmənt/ (n. 参与) in the project had been **casual** /ˈkæʒuəl/ (adj. 随意的) at first, but now he cared deeply. "She said the plant grows in **square** /skwer/ (adj./n. 正方形的；广场) patches near water. **Full** /fʊl/ (adj. 满的；完整的) sunlight is needed."

Maya felt a **mix** /mɪks/ (n./v. 混合) of emotions. She had **no** /noʊ/ (det. 没有) **doubt** /daʊt/ (n./v. 怀疑) about the plant's importance for medicine. But was she **safe** /seɪf/ (adj. 安全的) leading her team deeper into unknown territory? The **alliance** /əˈlaɪəns/ (n. 联盟) between universities funding this trip had been formed **respectively** /rɪˈspektɪvli/ (adv. 分别地；各自地) by **giant** /ˈdʒaɪənt/ (adj./n. 巨大的；巨人) institutions, each with their own goals. The **policy** /ˈpɑːləsi/ (n. 政策) was clear: return with results or return with nothing.

As they walked deeper, Maya noticed something **apart** from the usual landscape. A **designer** /dɪˈzaɪnər/ (n. 设计师) could **not** have planned it better—**nature** /ˈneɪtʃər/ (n. 自然) had created a **performance** /pərˈfɔːrməns/ (n. 表演；性能) of light and shadow that pointed them forward. "Look," Tom whispered, "**hunting** /ˈhʌntɪŋ/ (n./v. 打猎) for this plant is like hunting for answers **while** searching for questions."

They found it growing in a small clearing, **respectively** protected by thick bushes on three sides. Maya knelt in the **grass**, her hands gentle. This **full** moment of discovery made all the **demand** worth it. Tom smiled, preparing to **cook** a celebration meal. "You know," he said, "I **mix** ingredients every day, but **nature** **mixes** elements in ways I could never imagine. We are just **companions** learning from a **giant** teacher." Maya agreed. **Nevertheless**, they had succeeded, and this **alliance** of strangers had become friends in the **wilderness**, **safe** in their shared **involvement** with something larger than themselves.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

荒野向四面八方延伸数英里。玛雅与她的团队分开站立，再次查看地图。需求很简单但不容易：在冬天之前找到稀有植物。她经历过许多困难的任务，然而，这一次感觉不同。当别人只看到树木和草时，她看到的是可能性。

"我们应该听帕克博士说的话，"她的同伴汤姆说。他不是科学家，而是被雇来让他们吃饱的厨师。他对项目的参与起初是随意的，但现在他深深关心。"她说植物在水边的正方形地块中生长。需要完全的阳光。"

玛雅感到一种混合的情绪。她对植物对医学的重要性没有怀疑。但是，带领团队深入未知领域是否安全？资助这次旅行的大学之间的联盟分别由巨大的机构组成，每个都有自己的目标。政策很明确：带着结果回来或什么都不带回来。

当他们走得更深时，玛雅注意到与通常景观分开的东西。设计师无法计划得更好——自然创造了一场光影表演，指引他们前进。"看，"汤姆低声说，"寻找这种植物就像在寻找问题的同时寻找答案。"

他们发现它生长在一个小空地上，分别被三面的厚灌木保护。玛雅跪在草地上，双手温柔。这个完整的发现时刻让所有的需求都值得。汤姆微笑着，准备烹饪庆祝餐。"你知道，"他说，"我每天混合食材，但自然混合元素的方式是我永远无法想象的。我们只是从一位巨大的老师那里学习的同伴。"玛雅同意。尽管如此，他们成功了，这个陌生人的联盟在荒野中成为了朋友，在他们共同参与比自己更伟大的事物中感到安全。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "She had experienced many difficult missions, nevertheless, this one felt different."

**分析：** "Nevertheless"是副词，表示"尽管如此，然而"，用于承认前面的事实，但提出对比或转折。这里用逗号隔开，强调转折关系。

**亮点：** "had experienced"使用过去完成时，强调在本次任务之前的经验积累。"felt different"用简单词汇传达深刻感受，体现了"show, don't tell"的写作原则——没有说"更难"或"更危险"，而是让读者感受这种不同。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "While others saw only trees and grass, she saw possibilities."

**分析：** "While"在此引导对比状语从句，不仅表示时间上的"当...时"，更强调对比——"然而"的意思。这是一个非常优雅的对比句式。

**亮点：** "saw only...saw possibilities"通过重复动词"saw"但搭配不同宾语，创造了强烈的对比效果。"possibilities"（可能性）与具体的"trees and grass"对比,暗示Maya有更深的洞察力和想象力。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "Hunting for this plant is like hunting for answers while searching for questions."

**分析：** 这是一个富有哲理的比喻句。"hunting for...is like hunting for..."使用平行结构，"while searching for"增加了层次，形成了"寻找答案"与"寻找问题"的悖论式表达。

**亮点：** 这句话体现了探索的本质——我们不仅在寻找答案，也在发现新的问题。这种表达超越了字面意思，让读者思考科学探索的深层含义。Tom作为厨师能说出如此有深度的话，也丰富了他的人物形象。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "Nature mixes elements in ways I could never imagine."

**分析：** "in ways (that) I could never imagine"中的关系代词"that"被省略了，这在口语和非正式书面语中很常见。"could never"表示能力上的不可能。

**亮点：** 将"mix"（混合）这个厨师熟悉的动作用在"nature"上，体现了职业思维。Tom从自己的专业角度理解自然，这种类比让抽象的"自然之美"变得可感知。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦1：While的多重含义

**原句1：** "While others saw only trees and grass, she saw possibilities."
**原句2：** "Hunting for this plant is like hunting for answers while searching for questions."

**聚焦：** While作为连词的两种主要用法

**用法区分：**

1. **时间关系（原句2）：** while = 当...的时候（同时进行）
   - 两个动作在同一时间发生
   - "hunting for answers"和"searching for questions"同时进行
   - 可替换为："at the same time as"

2. **对比关系（原句1）：** while = 然而，而（表对比）
   - 强调两个事物或情况的不同
   - "others saw only trees" vs. "she saw possibilities"
   - 可替换为："whereas" 或 "but"

**判断技巧：**
- 如果两个动作可以同时发生 → 时间关系
- 如果在对比两个不同的人或事 → 对比关系

**练习：** 判断下列句子中while的含义
1. "While I was cooking, he was reading." (时间)
2. "While I like tea, she prefers coffee." (对比)

---

### 聚焦2：Respectively的使用

**原句：** "The alliance between universities funding this trip had been formed respectively by giant institutions, each with their own goals."

**聚焦：** Respectively（分别地、各自地）的正确用法

**含义：** Respectively用于说明两个或多个事物与另外两个或多个事物一一对应的关系。

**典型用法：**
- "Tom and Jane are 25 and 30 years old respectively." (Tom 25岁，Jane 30岁)
- "They finished in first and second place respectively." (第一个人第一名，第二个人第二名)

**原句分析：**
这句话的respectively用法略显不够精确，因为没有明确列出对应关系。更清晰的表达可能是：
"The alliance had been formed by Harvard, MIT, and Yale, contributing $1M, $2M, and $1.5M respectively."

**常见错误：**
❌ "They each respectively have their own ideas." (redundant - each和respectively意思重复)
✅ "They have their own ideas respectively." 或 "They each have their own ideas."

**记忆技巧：** Respectively = "in the order mentioned"（按提到的顺序）

## Part 6. 延伸练习 (Practice & Reflection)

### 6.1 理解题 (Comprehension)

1. **Who** is Tom, and how does his role change during the expedition?
   - Tom is a cook hired to feed the team. His involvement starts casually, but he becomes deeply invested in the mission and proves to be a thoughtful companion.

2. **Why** does Maya feel this mission is different from her previous ones?
   - While the text doesn't specify exactly why, it suggests this mission feels different despite her experience, creating tension and anticipation.

3. **What** does Tom's statement about "hunting for answers while searching for questions" reveal about the nature of exploration?
   - It reveals that exploration is not just about finding predetermined answers, but also about discovering new questions and perspectives along the way.

4. **What if** the team had not listened to Dr. Park's advice about where the plant grows?
   - They likely would not have found the plant, as they followed her specific guidance about square patches near water with full sunlight.

5. **How** does the relationship between Maya and Tom evolve?
   - They start as colleagues with different roles (scientist and cook) but become genuine companions united by shared involvement in something meaningful.

### 6.3 写作任务 (Writing Prompt)

Write a short paragraph (80-100 words) about a time when you worked with someone very different from yourself to accomplish something meaningful. Use at least 5 words from this group's vocabulary list.

**参考示例：**
During my first year at university, I had to work with a group that seemed full of giants—everyone was more experienced than me. Nevertheless, our professor demanded that we each contribute equally to the project. While others focused on research, my involvement was in design. At first, I felt not safe sharing my casual ideas. But when I listened to my companions, I realized we needed to mix our different skills. The performance we created together was full of unexpected possibilities. Our alliance taught me that differences make teams stronger.

### 6.4 讨论问题 (Discussion)

**Question:** Maya and Tom come from very different professional backgrounds (scientist and cook), yet they form a meaningful partnership. How do diverse teams benefit from having members with different areas of expertise? Can you think of situations where this diversity might create challenges instead?

**思考提示：**
- Consider how different perspectives lead to creative solutions
- Think about the difference between diversity of skills vs. diversity of values
- Reflect on what makes collaboration work across different backgrounds
- Discuss whether some tasks demand specialized knowledge while others benefit from generalist thinking
- Consider Tom's philosophical insight—does being "apart" from the scientific establishment give him a unique perspective?
