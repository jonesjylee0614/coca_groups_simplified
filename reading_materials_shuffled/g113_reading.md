# Group 113 Learning Material

**#Adventure** – A story of courage under pressure

## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The **sheriff** /ˈʃerɪf/ (n. 警长) adjusted his **helmet** /ˈhelmɪt/ (n. 头盔) and stepped into the abandoned warehouse. **Notice** /ˈnoʊtɪs/ (v. 注意到) how quiet everything was—too quiet. His team had received a tip that the stolen medical supplies were hidden here, supplies that could save lives. The **tragedy** /ˈtrædʒədi/ (n. 悲剧) of recent storms had left hospitals empty, and someone was exploiting the crisis.

"Does this **seem** /siːm/ (v. 似乎；好像) right to you?" Deputy Marcus whispered, his hand near his **weapon** /ˈwepən/ (n. 武器). The building's **shadow** /ˈʃædoʊ/ (n. 阴影) stretched across the floor like a warning. Every **roll** /roʊl/ (n. 卷；名单) of bandages, every dose of medicine, was needed desperately. The **correlation** /ˌkɔːrəˈleɪʃn/ (n. 相关性) between the theft and rising **health-care** /helθ ker/ (n. 医疗保健) costs was obvious.

Sheriff Davis had been **official** /əˈfɪʃl/ (adj. 官方的；正式的) for twenty years, but this case felt personal. His own mother had been in the hospital during the **hurricane** /ˈhɜːrɪkeɪn/ (n. 飓风), waiting for treatment. The supplies they were tracking could mean the difference between life and death. He thought about the **army** /ˈɑːrmi/ (n. 军队) of volunteers who had helped during the disaster—ordinary people who didn't **struggle** /ˈstrʌɡl/ (v./n. 挣扎；奋斗) with moral questions like these criminals did.

"Over here," Marcus called, pointing to a door marked with a red **dot** /dɑːt/ (n. 点；圆点). Behind it, they found boxes stacked **high** /haɪ/ (adj. 高的；高级的). Medical equipment, syringes, even a **needle** /ˈniːdl/ (n. 针) marked with "**alpha** /ˈælfə/ (n. 阿尔法；首领) testing" labels. This wasn't just theft—someone was planning to **transfer** /trænsˈfɜːr/ (v. 转移；调动) these to the black market.

"**Think** /θɪŋk/ (v. 认为；思考) we can trace who did this?" Marcus asked. Sheriff Davis picked up a shipping label. The name had been crossed out, but he could still **correct** /kəˈrekt/ (v. 纠正；改正) his vision to read it. Before he could **announce** /əˈnaʊns/ (v. 宣布；通知) his finding, footsteps echoed outside. Someone was coming.

They moved **safely** /ˈseɪfli/ (adv. 安全地) behind a stack of crates. Through a crack in the wall, Davis could see two figures in **suit** /suːt/ (n. 西装；套装)s. One carried what looked like a **stem** /stem/ (n. 茎；干) of paperwork. The other spoke on a phone: "We need to **exhibit** /ɪɡˈzɪbɪt/ (v. 展示；表现) caution. The law is watching."

Davis felt anger rise. These weren't desperate people stealing to survive—they were organized criminals. The **itself** /ɪtˈself/ (pron. 它自己) warehouse belonged to a shell company, designed to hide the truth. He signaled to Marcus, and they prepared to move.

The arrest happened in **twelve** /twelv/ (num. 十二) minutes flat. Davis read them their rights **by** the book. "You're under arrest for theft and endangering public **health-care**," he said, his voice steady. The two men tried to argue, but it was a **mess** /mes/ (n. 混乱；困境)—their excuses fell apart quickly.

Back at the station, Davis filed his report. The medical supplies would be returned to hospitals within hours. He felt relieved but also exhausted. The **struggle** to keep communities safe never ended. As he walked to his car, he noticed the sky clearing after days of rain. **Fairly** /ˈferli/ (adv. 相当地；公平地) soon, things would return to normal—or as normal as they could be after such a **tragedy**.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

警长调整了他的头盔，走进废弃的仓库。注意一切是多么安静——太安静了。他的团队收到消息，被盗的医疗用品藏在这里，这些用品可以救命。最近风暴的悲剧让医院空空如也，而有人正在利用这场危机。

"这对你来说似乎对吗？"副警长马库斯低声说，他的手靠近武器。建筑的阴影像警告一样延伸在地板上。每一卷绷带，每一剂药物，都是迫切需要的。盗窃和不断上涨的医疗保健成本之间的相关性是显而易见的。

戴维斯警长已经当了二十年正式警长，但这个案子感觉很私人。他自己的母亲在飓风期间一直在医院，等待治疗。他们正在追踪的物资可能意味着生死之别。他想到了在灾难期间帮助的志愿者大军——那些不像这些罪犯那样在道德问题上挣扎的普通人。

"在这里，"马库斯喊道，指着一扇标有红点的门。在它后面，他们发现了堆叠得很高的箱子。医疗设备、注射器，甚至一根标有"阿尔法测试"标签的针。这不仅仅是盗窃——有人正在计划将这些转移到黑市。

"你认为我们能追踪到是谁干的吗？"马库斯问。戴维斯警长拿起一个运输标签。名字已经被划掉了，但他仍然可以纠正他的视力来阅读它。在他宣布他的发现之前，外面传来了脚步声。有人来了。

他们安全地移到一堆板条箱后面。透过墙上的一条裂缝，戴维斯可以看到两个穿着西装的人影。一个人拿着看起来像一堆文书工作的东西。另一个在打电话："我们需要表现出谨慎。法律在监视。"

戴维斯感到愤怒上升。这些不是为了生存而偷窃的绝望的人——他们是有组织的罪犯。仓库本身属于一个空壳公司，旨在隐藏真相。他向马库斯示意，他们准备行动。

逮捕在十二分钟内完成。戴维斯按照规定宣读了他们的权利。"你因盗窃和危害公共医疗保健而被捕，"他说，声音稳定。两个人试图争辩，但这是一团混乱——他们的借口很快就崩溃了。

回到警局，戴维斯提交了他的报告。医疗用品将在几小时内归还医院。他感到宽慰但也精疲力尽。保持社区安全的斗争永无止境。当他走向他的车时，他注意到天空在连续几天的雨后放晴了。相当快，事情会恢复正常——或者说在这样一场悲剧之后尽可能正常。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "Notice how quiet everything was—too quiet."

**分析：** 以动词"Notice"开头的祈使句，直接吸引读者注意。破折号后的"too quiet"强化了不祥的预感，这是悬疑小说中常用的技巧。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "The building's shadow stretched across the floor like a warning."

**分析：** 使用明喻（simile）"like a warning"，将物理现象（阴影）与抽象概念（警告）联系起来。这种拟人化手法营造紧张氛围。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "The correlation between the theft and rising health-care costs was obvious."

**分析：** "between A and B"结构表示两者之间的关系。"correlation"（相关性）是学术写作中常用词，这里用于表达因果关系的复杂性。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "These weren't desperate people stealing to survive—they were organized criminals."

**分析：** 使用破折号连接对比的两个陈述，强调转折。"stealing to survive"（为生存而偷）和"organized criminals"（有组织犯罪）形成道德判断上的对比。

---

### 句子5
**原句：** "The struggle to keep communities safe never ended."

**分析：** 简洁有力的陈述，使用"never ended"强调持续性。这种句子适合作为段落或文章的总结，点明主题。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点1：祈使句 + How从句（Imperative + How Clause）

**原句：** "Notice how quiet everything was—too quiet."

**聚焦：** 祈使句 + how引导的宾语从句

**用法：** "Notice/See/Look how..."是常用结构，用于引导读者或听众关注某个现象。"how + adj."从句作为notice的宾语，这种结构比简单的"Everything was quiet"更有叙事张力。

**可替换句：** Everything was very quiet—too quiet.（失去了直接呼吁读者注意的效果）

---

### 焦点2：动名词作主语（Gerund as Subject）

**原句：** "The struggle to keep communities safe never ended."

**聚焦：** "The struggle to keep..."（名词 + 不定式作定语）

**用法：** "struggle to do sth"表示"努力做某事的斗争"。虽然这里struggle是名词，但"to keep"不定式作为后置定语修饰struggle，说明斗争的内容。这种结构使句子更加正式和书面化。

**可替换句：** They struggled to keep communities safe continuously.（动词形式，不够简洁凝练）

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **Why was the sheriff's team investigating the warehouse?**  
   They received a tip that stolen medical supplies were hidden there, supplies desperately needed after recent storms.

2. **What made this case feel personal to Sheriff Davis?**  
   His own mother had been in the hospital during the hurricane, waiting for treatment.

3. **What did the sheriff and deputy find in the warehouse?**  
   They found boxes of medical equipment, syringes, and needles marked for testing, indicating the thieves planned to sell them on the black market.

4. **How long did the arrest take?**  
   The arrest took twelve minutes.

5. **What if the sheriff hadn't found the supplies in time? What might have happened?**  
   (Open answer: Students should consider the impact on patients, hospitals, and community health.)

---

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. The firefighters wore __________ (helmet) for protection.
   **Answer:** helmets

2. Did you __________ (notice) the change in her behavior?
   **Answer:** notice

3. The recent __________ (hurricane) caused extensive damage to the coast.
   **Answer:** hurricane

4. The data shows a strong __________ (correlation) between exercise and health.
   **Answer:** correlation

5. We need to __________ (transfer) the files to the new computer.
   **Answer:** transfer

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

**Topic:** Describe a situation where someone in your community showed courage in a difficult or dangerous situation. What happened? What was the outcome?

**Length:** 80-100 words

**提示：** 使用本组至少5个词汇，如struggle, seem, notice, official, tragedy等。

---

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion）

> **"Is it ever justified to steal if someone is desperate? Where should we draw the line between survival and crime?"**

Think about the distinction Sheriff Davis made between "desperate people stealing to survive" and "organized criminals." What factors should we consider when judging such actions? Discuss with a partner or write your thoughts.
