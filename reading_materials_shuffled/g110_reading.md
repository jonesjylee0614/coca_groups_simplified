## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The **development** /dɪˈveləpmənt/ (n. 发展) of the city hospital had been slow, **since** /sɪns/ (conj. 自从) budget cuts threatened every **element** /ˈelɪmənt/ (n. 要素) of healthcare. Dr. Maya stood **outside** /ˌaʊtˈsaɪd/ (prep. 在...外面) the old building, her **portfolio** /pɔːrtˈfoʊlioʊ/ (n. 文件夹；作品集) tucked under her arm. Inside were plans **that** /ðæt/ (pron. 那个) could change everything—a new wing for patients with **chronic** /ˈkrɑːnɪk/ (adj. 慢性的) conditions.

She felt a **slight** /slaɪt/ (adj. 轻微的) tremor of fear. The **procedure** /prəˈsiːdʒər/ (n. 程序；手续) for getting approval was **complicated** /ˈkɑːmplɪkeɪtɪd/ (adj. 复杂的), and **nobody** /ˈnoʊbədi/ (pron. 没有人) seemed interested in her ideas. Her **ego** /ˈiːɡoʊ/ (n. 自我) whispered that she should give up, but something deeper—a **philosophical** /ˌfɪləˈsɑːfɪkl/ (adj. 哲学的) belief **about** /əˈbaʊt/ (prep. 关于) human dignity—pushed her forward.

"We need to **weigh** /weɪ/ (v. 权衡) our options," the hospital director said during the meeting. He sat behind a desk, his words falling like rain on a **roof** /ruːf/ (n. 屋顶). "The **safety** /ˈseɪfti/ (n. 安全) of patients is paramount, but we have no money." Maya took a breath. "What if I told you we don't need a huge **battery** /ˈbætəri/ (n. 电池；一系列) of expensive machines? We need compassion and better space."

The room went quiet. One board member, a **historian** /hɪˈstɔːriən/ (n. 历史学家) by training, nodded slowly. "It's **dangerous** /ˈdeɪndʒərəs/ (adj. 危险的) to ignore the human factor," he said. Another added, "What if this isn't just **about** a building? What if it's **about** how we **sustain** /səˈsteɪn/ (v. 维持；支撑) hope?"

The **ending** /ˈendɪŋ/ (n. 结局) of the meeting was unexpected. The director agreed to a trial. Maya felt her heart **bleed** /bliːd/ (v. 流血；心痛) with relief and joy. She had stepped **out** /aʊt/ (adv. 出去) of her comfort zone and into the unknown. Now she understood: **normal** /ˈnɔːrml/ (adj. 正常的) was just another word for unchanging. Real progress demanded more.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

城市医院的发展一直很缓慢，自从预算削减威胁到医疗保健的每个要素以来。玛雅医生站在旧楼外面，她的文件夹夹在胳膊下。里面是那些可以改变一切的计划——一个为慢性病患者设立的新病区。

她感到轻微的恐惧颤抖。获得批准的程序很复杂，而且似乎没有人对她的想法感兴趣。她的自我低语说她应该放弃，但更深层的东西——一种关于人类尊严的哲学信念——推动她前进。

"我们需要权衡我们的选择，"医院主任在会议期间说。他坐在桌子后面，他的话语像雨点落在屋顶上。"患者的安全至关重要，但我们没有钱。"玛雅深吸一口气。"如果我告诉你，我们不需要一大堆昂贵的机器呢？我们需要的是同情心和更好的空间。"

房间安静了下来。一位董事会成员，一位受过训练的历史学家，缓慢地点了点头。"忽视人的因素是危险的，"他说。另一位补充道，"如果这不仅仅是关于一栋建筑呢？如果这是关于我们如何维持希望呢？"

会议的结局出人意料。主任同意试点。玛雅感到心中涌出如释重负和喜悦的血。她已经走出了自己的舒适区，进入了未知领域。现在她明白了：正常只是不变的另一个词。真正的进步需要更多。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "The development of the city hospital had been slow, since budget cuts threatened every element of healthcare."

**分析：** 这是一个主从复合句，使用了过去完成时（had been）来描述在主句时间点之前已经持续的状态。"since"引导原因状语从句，解释发展缓慢的原因。这种结构在叙述背景信息时非常有效。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "Her ego whispered that she should give up, but something deeper—a philosophical belief about human dignity—pushed her forward."

**分析：** 这个句子使用了拟人化修辞（ego whispered），使抽象概念具象化。破折号用于插入解释性短语，增强了句子的层次感。"but"连接对比关系，展现了内心的挣扎。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "We need to weigh our options."

**分析：** 简洁有力的句子，使用"weigh"这个动词的比喻意义（权衡），而不是字面意义（称重量）。这种用法在商务和决策语境中很常见。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "What if this isn't just about a building? What if it's about how we sustain hope?"

**分析：** 两个连续的"What if"问句形成了递进关系，从具体（建筑）到抽象（希望），引导听众进行更深层的思考。这种修辞手法在说服性演讲中非常有效。

---

### 句子5
**原句：** "Now she understood: normal was just another word for unchanging."

**分析：** 使用冒号引出结论或解释，这是正式写作中常用的标点技巧。句子将"normal"重新定义，展现了人物的思想转变。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点1：过去完成时（Past Perfect Tense）

**原句：** "The development of the city hospital had been slow, since budget cuts threatened every element of healthcare."

**聚焦：** 过去完成时（had been）

**用法：** 用于描述在过去某个时间点之前已经存在或持续的状态。在叙述故事时，过去完成时帮助建立时间线，说明哪些事件发生在先。

**可替换句：** The city hospital's development was slow because budget cuts threatened every element of healthcare.（使用一般过去时，更直接但失去了时间层次感）

---

### 焦点2：插入语的使用（Parenthetical Expressions）

**原句：** "...something deeper—a philosophical belief about human dignity—pushed her forward."

**聚焦：** 使用破折号插入解释性短语

**用法：** 破折号（dashes）用于插入额外信息或解释，比括号更醒目，比逗号更强调。这种用法让句子更有节奏感，同时保持流畅性。

**可替换句：** ...something deeper (a philosophical belief about human dignity) pushed her forward.（使用括号，语气较弱）

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **Why was Dr. Maya hesitant to present her ideas?**  
   Because the approval procedure was complicated and nobody seemed interested in her ideas.

2. **What did Dr. Maya believe was more important than expensive machines?**  
   She believed compassion and better space were more important.

3. **Who supported Dr. Maya's proposal during the meeting?**  
   A historian on the board and another member who spoke about sustaining hope.

4. **What if the director had rejected Maya's proposal? How might the story have ended differently?**  
   (Open answer: Students should discuss alternative outcomes, such as Maya trying a different approach or losing hope.)

5. **What does the phrase "normal was just another word for unchanging" mean in the context of the story?**  
   It means that accepting the status quo (normal) means refusing to make progress or changes that could improve lives.

---

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. The hospital faced many __________ (complicated) issues with funding.
   **Answer:** complicated

2. We need to __________ (weigh) the benefits against the risks before deciding.
   **Answer:** weigh

3. Her __________ (portfolio) contained impressive examples of her previous work.
   **Answer:** portfolio

4. The new policy aims to __________ (sustain) economic growth over the next decade.
   **Answer:** sustain

5. There was only a __________ (slight) chance of rain, so we went hiking anyway.
   **Answer:** slight
