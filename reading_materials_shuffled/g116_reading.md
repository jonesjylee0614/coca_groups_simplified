## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Tom sat **down** /daʊn/ (adv./prep. 向下；在下面) on the **chair** /tʃer/ (n. 椅子) in his **residential** /ˌrezɪˈdenʃl/ (adj. 住宅的) neighborhood clinic, clutching a **poster** /ˈpoʊstər/ (n. 海报) advertising a community **fitness** /ˈfɪtnəs/ (n. 健身；适合) program. At forty-five, he had let himself go. His doctor had been clear: "You need to **improve** /ɪmˈpruːv/ (v. 改善；提高) your health, or you're looking at serious problems."

The **difference** /ˈdɪfrəns/ (n. 差异；不同) between who he used to be and who he had become was striking. Once an active person who would **bike** /baɪk/ (n./v. 自行车；骑车) everywhere, Tom now took the **elevator** /ˈelɪveɪtər/ (n. 电梯) even for one floor. His old **bicycle** /ˈbaɪsɪkl/ (n. 自行车) collected dust in the garage. The **extent** /ɪkˈstent/ (n. 程度；范围) of his decline shocked him.

"**Wait** /weɪt/ (v. 等待) here," the nurse said. "Dr. Martinez will see you soon." Tom looked at the **poster** again. The program promised to help people **dedicate** /ˈdedɪkeɪt/ (v. 致力于；奉献) themselves to healthier lives. But could he really change? The **cost** /kɔːst/ (n. 费用；代价) wasn't financial—it was personal. It meant facing his fears and weaknesses.

Dr. Martinez entered, carrying a **detailed** /ˈdiːteɪld/ (adj. 详细的) health report. "Your **device** /dɪˈvaɪs/ (n. 装置；设备) readings show elevated blood pressure," she said, pointing to a **grid** /ɡrɪd/ (n. 网格；坐标) of numbers. "To what **extent** are you willing to change your lifestyle?"

Tom thought about his children, about wanting to be there for them. "I'm ready," he said. "Tell me what to do."

The plan was simple but demanding. **Quick** /kwɪk/ (adj. 快的；敏捷的) fixes wouldn't work—this required sustained effort. Tom would need to **insert** /ɪnˈsɜːrt/ (v. 插入；嵌入) healthy habits into his daily routine. Dr. Martinez explained the **design** /dɪˈzaɪn/ (n./v. 设计；图案) of the program: thirty minutes of movement daily, better food choices, and regular check-ins.

That evening, Tom dusted off his **bicycle**. The **engine** /ˈendʒɪn/ (n. 发动机；引擎) of change, he realized, wasn't some external force—it was his own will. He rode **down** the street slowly at first, wobbling slightly. His neighbors watched with curiosity. One woman waved **from** her yard. "Good to see you out!"

The first week was hard. Tom wanted to quit, to sit back **down** and **wait** for motivation that might never come. But he remembered the doctor's words. Every **individual** /ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəl/ (n./adj. 个人；个别的) choice mattered. Each time he chose movement over stillness, he was investing in his future.

**Abroad** /əˈbrɔːd/ (adv. 在国外；到国外), people spoke of wellness as a luxury. But here, in this ordinary **residential** area, Tom learned it was a necessity. He joined the community **fitness** class, where people of all ages worked together. The instructor, a woman named Rita, emphasized that **injury** /ˈɪndʒəri/ (n. 伤害；损伤) prevention was key. "Listen to your body," she said. "**Improve** gradually."

Tom made friends with another participant, Mr. Chen, who shared his story. "I was at the **top** /tɑːp/ (n./adj. 顶部；最高的) of my field professionally but at the bottom health-wise," Chen said. "Then I had a health scare. It was a **twist** /twɪst/ (n./v. 转折；扭转) of fate that saved my life—the wake-up call I needed."

Months passed. Tom noticed changes—not just physical but mental. He felt more present, more alive. The **cost** of the program was small compared to the benefits. One day, while riding his **bike** through the park, he saw the same **poster** that had started his journey. He smiled, remembering how **quick** he had been to doubt himself.

The **difference** now was belief. He had proven to himself that change was possible. The **device** on his wrist showed his progress—**detailed** records of steps, heart rate, and **improvement**. But the real measure wasn't in the numbers on a **grid**. It was in how he felt when he woke up each morning, ready to face the day.

Tom had learned to **dedicate** time to himself without guilt. He understood that taking care of his health wasn't selfish—it was necessary to be there for the people who mattered. The journey wasn't about reaching some **top** level of **fitness** but about consistent, sustainable choices.

One year after that first clinic visit, Tom sat **down** with Dr. Martinez again. The **detailed** test results showed remarkable **improvement**. "You've transformed your health," she said. "What made the **difference**?"

Tom thought for a moment. "I stopped waiting for the perfect time," he said. "I just started, one pedal stroke at a time."

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

汤姆在他住宅区的社区诊所坐下，手里拿着一张宣传社区健身计划的海报。四十五岁的他已经放任自己了。他的医生说得很清楚："你需要改善你的健康，否则你会面临严重的问题。"

他曾经的自己和现在的自己之间的差异是惊人的。曾经是一个到处骑自行车的活跃的人，汤姆现在即使是一层楼也要坐电梯。他的旧自行车在车库里积灰。他衰退的程度让他震惊。

"在这里等，"护士说。"马丁内斯医生很快就会见你。"汤姆又看了看海报。该计划承诺帮助人们致力于更健康的生活。但他真的能改变吗？费用不是经济上的——而是个人的。这意味着面对他的恐惧和弱点。

马丁内斯医生进来了，拿着一份详细的健康报告。"你的设备读数显示血压升高，"她说，指着一个数字网格。"你愿意在多大程度上改变你的生活方式？"

汤姆想到了他的孩子，想到了要陪伴他们。"我准备好了，"他说。"告诉我该怎么做。"

计划很简单但要求很高。快速修复不会起作用——这需要持续的努力。汤姆需要在日常生活中插入健康习惯。马丁内斯医生解释了该计划的设计：每天三十分钟的运动，更好的食物选择，以及定期检查。

那天晚上，汤姆掸掉了他自行车上的灰尘。他意识到，改变的引擎不是某种外部力量——而是他自己的意志。他慢慢地沿着街道骑行，最初有点摇晃。他的邻居好奇地看着。一位女士从院子里挥手。"很高兴看到你出来！"

第一周很艰难。汤姆想放弃，想坐下来等待可能永远不会到来的动力。但他记得医生的话。每一个个人选择都很重要。每次他选择运动而不是静止，他都是在投资自己的未来。

在国外，人们将健康视为一种奢侈品。但在这里，在这个普通的住宅区，汤姆了解到这是一种必需品。他加入了社区健身课，那里各个年龄段的人一起锻炼。教练是一位名叫丽塔的女士，她强调预防伤害是关键。"倾听你的身体，"她说。"逐渐改善。"

汤姆与另一位参与者陈先生成了朋友，后者分享了他的故事。"我在职业上处于领域的顶端，但在健康方面处于底部，"陈说。"然后我有了一次健康恐慌。这是命运的转折，拯救了我的生命——我需要的警钟。"

几个月过去了。汤姆注意到了变化——不仅仅是身体上的，还有精神上的。他感觉更在场，更有活力。该计划的费用与收益相比很小。一天，当他骑自行车穿过公园时，他看到了开始他旅程的同一张海报。他笑了，记得他曾经多么快速地怀疑自己。

现在的差异在于信念。他已经向自己证明改变是可能的。他手腕上的设备显示了他的进展——步数、心率和改善的详细记录。但真正的衡量标准不在网格上的数字中。而是在他每天早上醒来时的感觉，准备面对这一天。

汤姆学会了毫无愧疚地奉献时间给自己。他明白照顾自己的健康不是自私的——这是陪伴重要的人所必需的。这段旅程不是为了达到某个顶级健身水平，而是为了持续、可持续的选择。

在第一次诊所访问一年后，汤姆再次与马丁内斯医生坐下。详细的测试结果显示了显著的改善。"你已经改变了你的健康，"她说。"是什么带来了差异？"

汤姆想了一会儿。"我不再等待完美的时机了，"他说。"我只是开始了，一次一个踏板。"

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "The difference between who he used to be and who he had become was striking."

**分析：** "who he used to be"和"who he had become"是名词性从句作介词宾语，形成对比。"used to be"表示过去的习惯状态，而过去完成时"had become"强调变化的完成。这种对比结构有力地展现人物的转变。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "The cost wasn't financial—it was personal."

**分析：** 使用破折号连接对比的两个陈述，强调"cost"的真实含义。这种结构比使用"but"更有力度，适合强调重要的对比或转折。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "The engine of change, he realized, wasn't some external force—it was his own will."

**分析：** "engine of change"是隐喻，将抽象的"改变动力"具象化。插入语"he realized"表明这是人物的顿悟。再次使用破折号强调对比，这是文学性写作的有效技巧。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "Each time he chose movement over stillness, he was investing in his future."

**分析：** "Each time"引导时间状语从句。"choose A over B"表示"选择A而不是B"。"invest in"将健康选择比作投资，这个商业隐喻让抽象概念更易理解。

---

### 句子5
**原句：** "I stopped waiting for the perfect time. I just started, one pedal stroke at a time."

**分析：** 两个简短有力的句子形成节奏感。"one pedal stroke at a time"与"one step at a time"（一步一个脚印）是相似表达，强调渐进式改变的重要性。这种具象化表达比抽象说教更有感染力。

---

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点1：名词性从句作介词宾语

**原句：** "The difference between who he used to be and who he had become was striking."

**聚焦：** "between who... and who..."（介词 + 名词性从句）

**用法：** "who he used to be"和"who he had become"都是名词性从句，作介词"between"的宾语。这种结构用于表达抽象对比，常见于正式写作和文学作品中。

**可替换句：** The difference between his past self and present self was striking.（使用名词短语，不够生动）

---

### 焦点2：隐喻的使用（Metaphor）

**原句：** "The engine of change, he realized, wasn't some external force—it was his own will."

**聚焦：** "engine of change"（改变的引擎）

**用法：** 隐喻是将一个概念用另一个不同领域的概念来描述。"engine"（引擎）本是机械概念，用来形容"动力"，使抽象概念具象化。这种修辞手法在说服性和文学性写作中非常有效。

**可替换句：** He realized that the force behind change wasn't external—it was his own will.（直接表达，缺少生动性）

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **What motivated Tom to start improving his health?**  
   His doctor's warning about serious health problems and his desire to be there for his children motivated him.

2. **What was Tom's main form of exercise?**  
   Riding his bicycle, which he had neglected for a long time.

3. **What did Tom learn from Mr. Chen's story?**  
   That even successful professionals can neglect their health, and that sometimes a health scare can be the wake-up call needed to make changes.

4. **According to Tom, what made the difference in his transformation?**  
   He stopped waiting for the perfect time and just started, taking it one step (or one pedal stroke) at a time.

5. **What if Tom had given up during the first difficult week? How might his life have been different?**  
   (Open answer: Students should consider health consequences, impact on family, missed opportunities for personal growth.)

---

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. The teacher gave a __________ (detailed) explanation of the concept.
   **Answer:** detailed

2. Regular exercise can __________ (improve) your overall health.
   **Answer:** improve

3. What's the __________ (difference) between these two options?
   **Answer:** difference

4. Each __________ (individual) has the right to express their opinion.
   **Answer:** individual

5. She decided to __________ (dedicate) her time to helping others.
   **Answer:** dedicate

---

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

**Topic:** Describe a positive change you made in your life. What inspired you to change? What challenges did you face? How did you overcome them?

**Length:** 80-100 words

**提示：** 使用本组至少5个词汇，如improve, dedicate, individual, difference, extent等。

---

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion）

> **"Is it ever too late to make positive changes in your health and lifestyle? What obstacles prevent people from taking the first step?"**

Think about Tom's journey and the fact that he started at age 45. In your opinion, what are the biggest barriers to starting a healthier lifestyle? How can communities support individuals in making these changes? Discuss with a partner or write your thoughts.
