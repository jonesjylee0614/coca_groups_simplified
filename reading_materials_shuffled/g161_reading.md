## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The old **casino** /kəˈsiːnoʊ/ (n. 赌场) stood at the edge of town, **here** /hɪr/ (adv. 这里) where few people dared to walk after dark. Maya worked as a **retailer** /ˈriːteɪlər/ (n. 零售商) nearby, selling **rice** /raɪs/ (n. 米) and basic goods to families who could barely afford them. She would **move** /muːv/ (v. 移动；搬家) between the shelves, arranging products with care, but her mind was always somewhere else.

**Love** /lʌv/ (n./v. 爱；热爱) had brought her to this country years ago, following a man who promised her **citizenship** /ˈsɪtɪzənʃɪp/ (n. 公民身份). But that dream had cracked like old glass. Now she lived in constant fear, **vulnerable** /ˈvʌlnərəbl/ (adj. 脆弱的；易受伤害的) to anyone who might **send** /send/ (v. 发送；派遣) her back across the border. She could **feel** /fiːl/ (v. 感觉) the weight of uncertainty every morning when she woke.

The city council had decided to **ban** /bæn/ (v./n. 禁止) undocumented workers from public spaces. The new **regulatory** /ˈreɡjələtɔːri/ (adj. 监管的；管理的) framework was **forth** /fɔːrθ/ (adv. 向前) in the newspapers, and Maya **know** /noʊ/ (v. 知道) it meant trouble. Her **girlfriend** /ˈɡɜːrlfrend/ (n. 女朋友), Lisa, tried to comfort her. "We'll fight this," Lisa said with **intention** /ɪnˈtenʃn/ (n. 意图；目的). "They can't just **register** /ˈredʒɪstər/ (v. 注册；登记) people like criminals."

But Maya had already tasted **disappointment** /ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt/ (n. 失望) too many times. She remembered the **crack** /kræk/ (n./v. 裂缝；破裂) in the system—how promises turned to dust, how hope could shatter overnight. The thought of **prison** /ˈprɪzn/ (n. 监狱) terrified her, though she had done nothing wrong except exist without papers.

One evening, while **reading** /ˈriːdɪŋ/ (n./v. 阅读) the local news at a small **park** /pɑːrk/ (n. 公园), Maya noticed a group gathering. They were organizing a protest against the new law. A woman spoke about **Islam** /ɪˈslɑːm/ (n. 伊斯兰教), about how her community faced similar persecution. Another man talked about **ease** /iːz/ (n. 轻松；容易) of judgment and how quickly people forgot their own immigrant roots.

Maya felt something shift inside her. Perhaps **expansion** /ɪkˈspænʃn/ (n. 扩张；扩大) of her courage was possible. Perhaps she didn't have to hide anymore. She stood up, her voice shaking but clear, and told her story. The crowd listened. Some cried. Others nodded in understanding.

That night, Maya walked home differently. She still carried fear, but alongside it was something new—a sense that she belonged, that her voice mattered. The fight wasn't over, but for the first time in years, she felt truly **here**, truly present, truly alive.

那天晚上,玛雅走回家的方式不同了。她仍然怀着恐惧,但与之并存的是一些新东西——一种归属感,一种她的声音很重要的感觉。斗争还没有结束,但多年来第一次,她真正感到自己在这里,真正在场,真正活着。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "Love had brought her to this country years ago, following a man who promised her citizenship."

**分析:** 过去完成时 "had brought" 表示在故事主要时间线之前就已经完成的动作。现在分词短语 "following a man..." 作伴随状语,说明她来这个国家的方式。定语从句 "who promised..." 修饰 "a man",交代了更多背景信息。

**亮点:** 一个句子中巧妙融合了三个时间层次和因果关系,展现了复杂叙事技巧。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "She could feel the weight of uncertainty every morning when she woke."

**分析:** "feel the weight of..." 是英语中将抽象概念具象化的经典表达方式。"uncertainty" 本是抽象名词,但通过 "weight" 让读者能够感同身受。时间状语从句 "when she woke" 强调这种感受的日常性和持续性。

**亮点:** 用身体感受来表达心理状态,是文学写作中的高级技巧。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "She still carried fear, but alongside it was something new—a sense that she belonged, that her voice mattered."

**分析:** 这是一个对比句,通过 "but" 连接两个相反的情感状态。"alongside it" 表示并存而非完全替代,更真实地反映人的复杂情感。破折号后的同位语解释 "something new",两个 "that" 引导的同位语从句进一步说明这种新感受的具体内容。

**亮点:** 不简单地用积极情绪取代消极情绪,而是展现两者共存,这种写法更有深度和真实感。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "Perhaps expansion of her courage was possible."

**分析:** 使用 "expansion" 这个通常用于描述物理或商业增长的词来描述勇气的增长,是一种创新的隐喻用法。"was possible" 而非 "is possible" 使用过去时,与整个故事时态保持一致,同时也体现了玛雅当时内心的不确定性。

**亮点:** 将抽象的勇气用 "expansion" 来表达,暗示勇气可以像空间一样被拓展,富有想象力。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: 过去完成时表示"过去的过去"

**原句:** "Love had brought her to this country years ago, following a man who promised her citizenship."

**聚焦:** 过去完成时 (had brought) 的使用

**用法说明:** 
过去完成时用于表示在故事主要时间线之前就已经完成的动作。故事的主要时间线是"现在的玛雅",而她来到这个国家是更早的过去,所以用 "had brought" 而不是 "brought"。这样能清晰地区分时间层次。

**时态对比:**
- 一般过去时: "Love brought her to this country" (只说明过去的事实)
- 过去完成时: "Love had brought her to this country" (强调这发生在故事开始前,是背景信息)

**可替换句:** "Years ago, love brought her to this country when she followed a man who promised her citizenship."

---

### 聚焦点 2: 现在分词作伴随状语

**原句:** "She would move between the shelves, arranging products with care, but her mind was always somewhere else."

**聚焦:** 现在分词 (arranging) 作伴随状语

**用法说明:**
"arranging products with care" 是现在分词短语,作伴随状语,说明 "move between the shelves" 时同时发生的另一个动作。这种结构让句子更简洁,避免了使用连词 "and" 造成的平铺直叙。

**结构分析:**
- 主要动作: would move between the shelves
- 伴随动作: arranging products with care
- 转折: but her mind was always somewhere else

**可替换句:** "She would move between the shelves and arrange products with care, but her mind was always somewhere else." (用 "and" 连接,但不如原句优雅)
