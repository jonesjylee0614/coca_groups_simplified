## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The old **shop** /ʃɑːp/ (n./v. 商店;购物) on Second Street had been there **throughout** /θruːˈaʊt/ (prep. 遍及;贯穿) three generations. Its owner, Elena, sat at the counter reading an **essay** /ˈeseɪ/ (n. 散文;论文) her granddaughter had written about **tradition** /trəˈdɪʃn/ (n. 传统). The young girl argued that old ways could be **silly** /ˈsɪli/ (adj. 愚蠢的;幼稚的), and that **advanced** /ədˈvænst/ (adj. 先进的;高级的) methods were always better. Elena smiled. She understood the **psychology** /saɪˈkɑːlədʒi/ (n. 心理学) behind youth—the desire to **cut** /kʌt/ (v. 切;削减) ties with the past and create something new.

"**Damn** /dæm/ (interj. 该死;v. 诅咒)," she whispered, not in anger but in recognition. Change was coming whether she liked it or not. The **second** /ˈsekənd/ (num./n. 第二;秒) she accepted this, she felt lighter. She had spent years trying **to** /tuː/ (prep. 向;到) **preserve** /prɪˈzɜːrv/ (v. 保护;保存) every detail of how her father ran the business. But maybe it was time to **extend** /ɪkˈstend/ (v. 延伸;扩展) trust to the next generation.

The **shop** specialized in **sacred** /ˈseɪkrɪd/ (adj. 神圣的;宗教的) objects for ceremonies—things people used in weddings, **play** /pleɪ/ (v./n. 玩;演奏;戏剧) ed at funerals, kept in homes for protection. But **productivity** /ˌprɑːdʌkˈtɪvəti/ (n. 生产力;生产率) had declined. Young people didn't **bet** /bet/ (v. 打赌;确信) on old customs anymore. They wanted **well-being** /ˌwel ˈbiːɪŋ/ (n. 幸福;福祉) through science, not ritual.

Elena's granddaughter, Maya, entered with her usual energy. "I'm here **to** help today!" she announced. She wore **advanced** clothing—what the young called 'modern style.' Elena thought it looked odd, but she didn't say so. Instead, she asked Maya to **respond** /r�İˈspɑːnd/ (v. 回应;反应) to customer questions while she went upstairs.

In the small room above the **shop**, Elena opened an old box. Inside were letters from her father, written **throughout** his life. One caught her eye: "The **secret** /ˈsiːkrət/ (n./adj. 秘密) to life," he wrote, "is not to resist change, but to understand what must **preserve** and what must grow. A tree doesn't **quit** /kwɪt/ (v. 停止;辞职) being a tree when it grows new branches."

She felt tears form. Her father would have been **confident** /ˈkɑːnfɪdənt/ (adj. 自信的) in Maya's generation. He wouldn't have seen their **advanced** ideas as threats. He would have asked: "How can **tradition** **play** a role in this new world?"

Downstairs, Maya was explaining **to** a customer how certain objects weren't just **sacred** because of age, but because of meaning. "My grandmother doesn't just sell things," Maya said. "She helps people remember what matters. That's **well-being** on a deep level."

Elena listened from the stairs, her heart full. The girl had understood something important. When Maya finished, Elena came down. "You spoke **well**," she said. "You didn't **cut** the connection to the past. You **extend** ed its meaning **to** the present."

Maya grinned. "I've been reading your old **essay** s. You wrote about how **tradition** isn't about repeating the same actions, but about carrying forward values. That's not **silly**—that's wisdom."

That afternoon, they worked together **to** reorganize the **shop**. Elena showed Maya how **to** **estimate** /ˈestɪmeɪt/ (v./n. 估计;估价) the value of old items, not just in money but in meaning. Maya brought her knowledge of digital tools, showing how they could reach more people online. **Throughout** the day, they laughed and debated. Elena felt something she hadn't in years: hope.

A man came in looking for a **flight** /flaɪt/ (n. 飞行;航班) blessing—a small item travelers carried for safety. Elena started **to** **respond**, but Maya jumped in. "We have several," she said. "But let me ask: What **excite** /ɪkˈsaɪt/ (v. 使兴奋;激发) s you about your trip? What do you hope **to** find?"

The man paused, surprised. "I... I want **to** reconnect with my roots. My parents came from that country."

Maya nodded. "Then this one." She handed him a small **frame** /freɪm/ (n./v. 框架;相框;构建) ed picture. "It's simple, but it carries the **notion** /ˈnoʊʃn/ (n. 概念;想法) of home. My grandmother says home isn't a place—it's a feeling you carry **throughout** life."

The man's eyes grew wet. "Perfect," he said. "How did you know?"

"I **play** ed a **bet**," Maya said with a smile. "I listened **to** what you didn't say."

After he left, Elena hugged her granddaughter. "You **invent** /ɪnˈvent/ (v. 发明;创造) ed a new way **to** do this work. Not by destroying the old, but by understanding the **psychology** behind why people come here."

That evening, they **close** /kloʊz/ (v. 关闭;结束) d the **shop** together. Elena no longer felt the weight of protecting every **tradition** alone. Maya didn't need **to** **quit** the past **to** build the future. They had found a middle path—one that would **preserve** wisdom while welcoming change.

As they walked home, Elena asked, "What will you write in your next **essay**?"

"About how **tradition** and progress aren't opposites," Maya replied. "About how real **productivity** comes from understanding both. And about how my **damn** **confident** grandmother taught me that being **advanced** doesn't mean forgetting where you came from."

Elena laughed. "Don't curse in your **essay**."

"I won't," Maya promised. "But **to** tell you the **secret**—sometimes a little rebellion makes the **tradition** more **sacred**."

They walked **throughout** the quiet streets, two generations finding peace in each other's company. The moon rose, full and bright. Somewhere, a bird began **to** sing—a **song** /sɔːŋ/ (n. 歌曲) that had been sung **throughout** centuries, yet felt new every time.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

第二街上的老商店已经存在了三代人。店主埃琳娜坐在柜台前,读着她孙女写的一篇关于传统的文章。这个年轻女孩认为旧方式可能很愚蠢,先进的方法总是更好。埃琳娜微笑了。她理解年轻人背后的心理——渴望切断与过去的联系,创造新事物。

"该死,"她低声说,不是出于愤怒而是出于认识。无论她喜欢与否,变革即将到来。在她接受这一点的那一秒,她感到轻松了。她花了多年时间试图保存父亲经营企业的每一个细节。但也许是时候把信任延伸给下一代了。

这家商店专门经营用于仪式的神圣物品——人们在婚礼上使用、在葬礼上演奏、在家中保存以求保护的东西。但生产力已经下降。年轻人不再押注于旧习俗。他们想要通过科学而非仪式获得幸福。

埃琳娜的孙女玛雅带着她惯有的活力进来了。"我今天来帮忙!"她宣布。她穿着先进的衣服——年轻人所谓的"现代风格"。埃琳娜觉得看起来很奇怪,但她没有说。相反,她让玛雅在她上楼时回答顾客的问题。

在商店上方的小房间里,埃琳娜打开了一个旧盒子。里面是她父亲的信,贯穿他一生所写。一封信引起了她的注意:"生活的秘密,"他写道,"不是抵制变化,而是理解什么必须保存,什么必须成长。一棵树长出新枝时,它不会停止成为一棵树。"

她感到泪水涌出。她父亲会对玛雅这一代充满信心。他不会把他们的先进想法视为威胁。他会问:"传统如何在这个新世界中扮演角色?"

楼下,玛雅正在向一位顾客解释,某些物品之所以神圣,不仅仅是因为年代,而是因为意义。"我祖母不只是卖东西,"玛雅说。"她帮助人们记住什么是重要的。这才是深层次的幸福。"

埃琳娜在楼梯上听着,心中充满感动。这个女孩理解了一些重要的东西。当玛雅说完时,埃琳娜走下来。"你说得很好,"她说。"你没有切断与过去的联系。你把它的意义延伸到了现在。"

玛雅咧嘴笑了。"我一直在读你的旧文章。你写过传统不是重复相同的行动,而是传承价值观。这不愚蠢——这是智慧。"

那天下午,她们一起重新组织商店。埃琳娜向玛雅展示如何估计旧物品的价值,不仅仅是金钱价值,还有意义价值。玛雅带来了她的数字工具知识,展示他们如何在线上接触更多人。整个下午,她们欢笑和辩论。埃琳娜感受到了多年未有的东西:希望。

一个男人进来寻找飞行祝福——旅行者携带的小物件以求安全。埃琳娜开始回应,但玛雅抢先了。"我们有几个,"她说。"但让我问:你的旅行让你兴奋的是什么?你希望找到什么?"

那个男人停顿了,感到惊讶。"我...我想重新连接我的根。我父母来自那个国家。"

玛雅点点头。"那就这个。"她递给他一个小相框。"它很简单,但它承载着家的概念。我祖母说家不是一个地方——而是你贯穿一生携带的一种感觉。"

那个男人眼睛湿润了。"完美,"他说。"你怎么知道的?"

"我打了个赌,"玛雅微笑着说。"我听到了你没有说的话。"

他离开后,埃琳娜拥抱了她的孙女。"你发明了一种新的做这项工作的方式。不是通过摧毁旧的,而是通过理解人们为什么来这里的心理。"

那天晚上,她们一起关闭了商店。埃琳娜不再感到独自保护每个传统的重量。玛雅不需要放弃过去来建设未来。她们找到了中间道路——一条既保存智慧又欢迎变化的道路。

当她们走回家时,埃琳娜问:"你下一篇文章会写什么?"

"关于传统和进步不是对立的,"玛雅回答。"关于真正的生产力来自理解两者。关于我那该死的自信的祖母如何教会我,先进并不意味着忘记你来自哪里。"

埃琳娜笑了。"不要在你的文章里骂人。"

"我不会的,"玛雅保证。"但告诉你秘密——有时候一点叛逆让传统更加神圣。"

她们走过安静的街道,两代人在彼此的陪伴中找到了平静。月亮升起,又圆又亮。某处,一只鸟开始唱歌——一首贯穿几个世纪的歌曲,但每次都感觉是新的。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句:** "The secret to life is not to resist change, but to understand what must preserve and what must grow."

**分析:** 使用"not...but..."结构对比两种态度。"what must preserve and what must grow"使用疑问词引导的从句作"understand"的宾语,展现哲学思考。

**要点:** 这种结构适合表达深刻的人生智慧,对比错误和正确的态度。

---

### 句子2
**原句:** "A tree doesn't quit being a tree when it grows new branches."

**分析:** 使用比喻来说明变化与本质的关系。"quit being"表示"停止成为","when"引导时间状语从句。

**要点:** 自然界的比喻能让抽象概念变得具体可感,增强说服力。

---

### 句子3
**原句:** "I listened to what you didn't say."

**分析:** 巧妙地使用否定式"didn't say"来强调洞察力。"what you didn't say"作"listen to"的宾语,表达了深层理解。

**要点:** 这种表达方式展现了敏锐的观察力和同理心,比直接说"I understood your feelings"更有力。

---

### 句子4
**原句:** "Being advanced doesn't mean forgetting where you came from."

**分析:** 动名词"Being advanced"作主语,"doesn't mean"后跟动名词"forgetting"。这个句式清晰地纠正误解。

**要点:** "doesn't mean"是澄清误解的有效句式,后面跟动名词或名词从句。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 焦点1: 不定式表示目的

**原句:** "Elena opened an old box to find letters from her father."

**聚焦:** 不定式to find作目的状语

**用法:** 不定式可以表达行为的目的,相当于"in order to"。这比使用从句更简洁。注意不定式前不需要逗号。

**可替换句:** "Elena opened an old box in order to find letters." (更正式,但略显累赘)

---

### 焦点2: 疑问词引导的名词性从句

**原句:** "I listened to what you didn't say."

**聚焦:** what引导的宾语从句

**用法:** 疑问词what引导名词性从句,在句中作介词to的宾语。"what you didn't say"表示"你没有说的话",what既起引导作用,又在从句中作宾语。

**可替换句:** "I listened to the things that you didn't say." (用定语从句,但不够简洁)

## Part 6. 延伸练习 (Practice & Reflection)

### 6.1 理解题 (Comprehension)

1. **Why did Elena initially feel the weight of protecting tradition?**
   - 提示:思考她对父亲遗产的责任感。

2. **What changed Elena's perspective on Maya's generation?**
   - 提示:关注父亲信中关于树的比喻。

3. **How did Maya show understanding of the shop's deeper purpose?**
   - 提示:注意她与顾客的对话方式。

   - 提示:思考"中间道路"的概念。

   - 提示:想象拒绝变化的后果。
