## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Dr. Sarah Chen stood **on** /ɑːn/ (prep. 在...上) the deck of the research vessel, watching the sun rise **from** /frʌm/ (prep. 从；来自) the horizon. She was a **researcher** /rɪˈsɜːrtʃər/ (n. 研究员) who had dedicated her life to studying ocean life, and this expedition was her **first** /fɜːrst/ (adj./adv. 第一的；首先) chance to explore the **rare** /rer/ (adj. 稀有的；罕见的) coral reefs near the **global** /ˈɡloʊbl/ (adj. 全球的；全世界的) conservation zone.

"**Hello** /həˈloʊ/ (interj./n. 你好)," a voice called behind her. It was Marcus, the team's biologist. "Ready for the **test** /test/ (n./v. 测试；考验) dive?" Sarah nodded, though she felt nervous. The **summit** /ˈsʌmɪt/ (n. 峰会；顶点) of her career was approaching—this research could change how the world understood marine ecosystems.

**While** /waɪl/ (conj. 当...时；虽然) preparing equipment, Sarah noticed something strange **on** the **observation** /ˌɑːbzərˈveɪʃn/ (n. 观察；观测) screen. A **layer** /ˈleɪər/ (n. 层；层次) of sediment had shifted, revealing what looked like ancient formations. "We need to **contact** /ˈkɑːntækt/ (n./v. 联系；接触) the main station," she said urgently. "This could be significant."

As they descended into the water, the beauty below took Sarah's breath away. Schools of fish moved in perfect harmony, and a **turtle** /ˈtɜːrtl/ (n. 海龟) glided past, ancient and wise. But what caught her attention was a **newly** /ˈnuːli/ (adv. 最近；新近) formed reef structure. It was unlike anything she had seen before, with colors that seemed almost unreal.

"**Remember** /rɪˈmembər/ (v. 记得；记住) the **prescription** /prɪˈskrɪpʃn/ (n. 处方；指示) **from** the conservation board?" Marcus's voice crackled through the radio. "We can observe but not **drop** /drɑːp/ (v. 掉落；放弃) anything or disturb the environment."

Sarah was **pleased** /pliːzd/ (adj. 高兴的；满意的) to see the ecosystem thriving despite human impact elsewhere. But as they explored deeper, her initial excitement turned to concern. She noticed unusual patterns—coral bleaching in small patches, signs that the **global** warming **reaction** /riˈækʃn/ (n. 反应；反响) was reaching even these protected waters.

Later, back **on** the ship, Sarah analyzed the data with growing worry. The **observation** showed that the **layer** of protection they thought existed might not be enough. She called an emergency **summit** meeting with the research team. "We need to **force** /fɔːrs/ (v./n. 强迫；力量) action," she said. "These reefs won't survive without immediate intervention."

One team member was **disappointed** /ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪd/ (adj. 失望的). "But we're just **researcher**s. What can we do?" Sarah paused, gathering her thoughts. She thought about the **sword** /sɔːrd/ (n. 剑) of knowledge—how **insight** /ˈɪnsaɪt/ (n. 洞察力；见解) could be a powerful weapon against ignorance.

"We document everything," she said firmly. "Every **observation**, every **test** result. Then we take it to the world. The **elevator** /ˈelɪveɪtər/ (n. 电梯；升降机) pitch? 'These reefs are the ocean's memory. Lose them, and we lose **insight** into our planet's past and future.'"

The team worked tirelessly. **While** days turned into weeks, they gathered comprehensive data. Sarah made sure to **contact** every major environmental organization, sending them evidence they couldn't ignore. The **reaction** was swift—within months, a **global** initiative was launched to expand protection zones.

A year later, Sarah returned to the same spot. She was no longer just a **researcher** but an advocate recognized worldwide. As the vessel anchored, she felt a **newly** found sense of purpose. The **turtle** she had seen before appeared **again**, as if to **remember** her. Sarah smiled.

Standing **on** the deck at dawn, she reflected **on** her journey. From a **first** dive filled with wonder to becoming a voice for the voiceless creatures below, her path had been unexpected. She had learned that being **pleased** with discovery wasn't enough—action was required. The ocean had taught her that **rare** beauty demanded **force**ful protection, that every **layer** of life mattered, and that **while** challenges were endless, so was hope.

In the ship's galley, someone made coffee. The smell reminded her of home, of **newly** brewed mornings before everything changed. But she wouldn't trade this for anything. This was where she belonged—**from** the depths **to** the surface, connecting humanity with the **global** ocean that sustained all life.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

莎拉·陈博士站在研究船的甲板上，看着太阳从地平线升起。她是一位研究员，一生致力于研究海洋生物，这次探险是她第一次有机会探索全球保护区附近稀有的珊瑚礁。

"你好，"身后传来一个声音。是团队的生物学家马库斯。"准备好测试潜水了吗？"莎拉点了点头，尽管她感到紧张。她职业生涯的顶点正在临近——这项研究可以改变世界对海洋生态系统的理解。

在准备设备时，莎拉在观察屏幕上注意到了一些奇怪的东西。一层沉积物移动了，露出了看起来像古代构造的东西。"我们需要联系主站，"她急切地说。"这可能很重要。"

当他们下降到水中时，下面的美景让莎拉屏息。成群的鱼完美和谐地移动，一只海龟滑过，古老而睿智。但吸引她注意的是一个新近形成的珊瑚礁结构。它不像她以前见过的任何东西，颜色似乎几乎不真实。

"记得保护委员会的指示吗？"马库斯的声音通过无线电噼啪作响。"我们可以观察，但不能掉落任何东西或干扰环境。"

莎拉很高兴看到生态系统在其他地方受到人类影响的情况下仍然繁荣。但当他们探索更深时，她最初的兴奋变成了担忧。她注意到不寻常的模式——小片珊瑚白化，这是全球变暖反应甚至到达这些受保护水域的迹象。

后来，回到船上，莎拉带着越来越多的担忧分析数据。观察显示，他们认为存在的保护层可能不够。她召开了与研究团队的紧急峰会会议。"我们需要强制采取行动，"她说。"没有立即干预，这些珊瑚礁将无法生存。"

一位团队成员感到失望。"但我们只是研究人员。我们能做什么？"莎拉停顿了一下，整理她的思绪。她想到了知识之剑——洞察力如何成为对抗无知的强大武器。

"我们记录一切，"她坚定地说。"每一次观察，每一个测试结果。然后我们向世界展示。电梯演讲？'这些珊瑚礁是海洋的记忆。失去它们，我们就失去了对地球过去和未来的洞察。'"

团队不知疲倦地工作。当日子变成几周时，他们收集了全面的数据。莎拉确保联系每一个主要的环境组织，向他们发送他们无法忽视的证据。反应很迅速——几个月内，一个全球倡议启动了扩大保护区。

一年后，莎拉回到了同一个地方。她不再只是一名研究员，而是一位世界公认的倡导者。当船只抛锚时，她感到了一种新近找到的使命感。她之前看到的那只海龟再次出现了，仿佛在记住她。莎拉笑了。

在黎明时站在甲板上，她反思自己的旅程。从充满惊奇的第一次潜水到成为下面无声生物的声音，她的道路是出乎意料的。她学到了对发现感到高兴是不够的——需要行动。海洋教会她，稀有的美需要强有力的保护，每一层生命都很重要，虽然挑战无穷无尽，但希望也是如此。

在船的厨房里，有人煮了咖啡。这种气味让她想起了家，想起了一切改变之前新近煮好的早晨。但她不会用这个换任何东西。这就是她的归属——从深处到表面，将人类与维持所有生命的全球海洋联系起来。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "This expedition was her first chance to explore the rare coral reefs near the global conservation zone."

**分析：** "chance to do sth"表示"做某事的机会"，这是常用结构。"near the global conservation zone"是地点状语，精确说明研究地点的重要性。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "While preparing equipment, Sarah noticed something strange on the observation screen."

**分析：** "While + 现在分词"结构表示时间状语从句的省略形式（省略了主语和be动词）。这种结构让句子更简洁，适合描述同时发生的动作。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "We can observe but not drop anything or disturb the environment."

**分析：** "but not"连接两个对比的动作。"drop... or disturb..."使用并列动词，说明多个禁止行为。这种结构清晰地表达了规则和限制。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "The sword of knowledge—how insight could be a powerful weapon against ignorance."

**分析：** 使用隐喻（metaphor）将抽象的"知识"比作"剑"，将"洞察力"比作"武器"。破折号引出解释，这种修辞手法让抽象概念更加生动有力。

---

### 句子5
**原句：** "From a first dive filled with wonder to becoming a voice for the voiceless creatures below, her path had been unexpected."

**分析：** "From A to B"结构表示变化和转变。"filled with wonder"和"voiceless creatures"使用形象化语言，增强感染力。过去完成时"had been"强调回顾性视角。

---

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点1：While引导的省略式状语从句

**原句：** "While preparing equipment, Sarah noticed something strange on the observation screen."

**聚焦：** While + 现在分词（省略主语和be动词）

**用法：** 当时间状语从句的主语与主句主语相同，且从句动词为进行时态时，可以省略从句的主语和be动词，使句子更加简洁流畅。

**完整形式：** While she was preparing equipment, Sarah noticed something strange on the observation screen.

**可替换句：** During equipment preparation, Sarah noticed something strange.（使用介词短语，较正式但缺少动态感）

---

### 焦点2：From...to...结构表示转变

**原句：** "From a first dive filled with wonder to becoming a voice for the voiceless creatures below, her path had been unexpected."

**聚焦：** From + 名词/动名词 to + 名词/动名词

**用法：** 这个结构用于表达时间、空间或状态的转变，常用于总结人物成长或事物发展。配合过去完成时，强调回顾性叙述。

**可替换句：** Her path had been unexpected—from her first dive filled with wonder, she became a voice for voiceless creatures below.（使用破折号代替，结构不够紧凑）

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **What was Sarah's profession and what was she studying?**  
   Sarah was a researcher who studied ocean life, specifically rare coral reefs near global conservation zones.

2. **What did Sarah discover that concerned her?**  
   She discovered coral bleaching in small patches, showing that global warming was affecting even protected waters.

3. **How did Sarah respond to her disappointing findings?**  
   She called an emergency summit meeting and decided to document everything and contact environmental organizations to force action.

4. **What metaphor did Sarah use to describe knowledge?**  
   She described knowledge as a "sword" and insight as a "powerful weapon against ignorance."

5. **What if Sarah had kept her findings private instead of sharing them? How might that have affected the coral reefs?**  
   (Open answer: Students should discuss potential consequences like lack of protection, continued environmental damage, and lost opportunities for conservation.)

---

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. Please __________ (contact) me if you need any help.
   **Answer:** contact

2. The __________ (observation) of natural behavior requires patience.
   **Answer:** observation

3. She was __________ (disappointed) when the concert was cancelled.
   **Answer:** disappointed

4. This is a __________ (rare) species of bird.
   **Answer:** rare

5. His __________ (insight) into the problem was very helpful.
   **Answer:** insight

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

**Topic:** Describe a moment when you discovered something that changed your perspective or motivated you to take action. What did you discover? What did you do about it?

**Length:** 80-100 words

**提示：** 使用本组至少5个词汇，如observation, discover, reaction, pleased, insight等。

---

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion）

> **"As individuals, what responsibility do we have to protect the environment, even when problems seem global and overwhelming?"**

Think about Sarah's journey from researcher to advocate. She could have simply documented problems without taking further action. What would you have done in her situation? How can ordinary people make a difference in environmental protection? Discuss with a partner or write your thoughts.
