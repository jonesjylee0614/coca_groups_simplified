## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Daniel sat in his small apartment, staring at a blank page. He wanted to **live** /lɪv/ (v. 生活；居住) as a writer, but bills demanded he work as a waiter. The **pot** /pɑːt/ (n. 锅；壶) of coffee on his desk had gone cold hours ago. His eyes felt **dry** /draɪ/ (adj. 干燥的), and his **neck** /nek/ (n. 脖子；颈部) ached from hunching over the laptop. Yet he couldn't stop. This **film** /fɪlm/ (n. 电影；胶片) script **who** /huː/ (pron. 谁) had been living in his head for months needed to exist in the world.

The **shooting** /ˈʃuːtɪŋ/ (n. 射击；拍摄) of independent films was expensive, but Daniel believed his story mattered. It was about his grandmother, a woman **who** had survived war and built a life from nothing. Her **shape** /ʃeɪp/ (n. 形状；状况) had been small and bent with age, but her spirit was **gorgeous** /ˈɡɔːrdʒəs/ (adj. 华丽的；极好的).

A **knock** /nɑːk/ (n./v. 敲击) at the door interrupted his thoughts. It was his friend Lisa, an **author** /ˈɔːθər/ (n. 作者；作家) **who** wrote about food and culture. "You look terrible," she said, **according** /əˈkɔːrdɪŋ/ (prep. 根据) to her usual blunt honesty. "When did you **last** /læst/ (adj./adv. 最后的；上次) eat?"

Daniel shrugged. "I had a **carbohydrate** /ˌkɑːrboʊˈhaɪdreɪt/ (n. 碳水化合物)-heavy lunch yesterday, I think." His **saving** /ˈseɪvɪŋ/ (n. 节约；储蓄) grace was that he worked at a restaurant where staff ate for free. "Want to **call** /kɔːl/ (v. 打电话；称呼) it dinner if I make pasta?"

"Only if you're **eligible** /ˈelɪdʒəbl/ (adj. 合格的；符合条件的) to cook without burning down the building," Lisa joked. She noticed the script pages scattered around. "Still working on the **film**? You know there's a **commission** /kəˈmɪʃn/ (n. 委员会；佣金) reviewing grants for emerging filmmakers. **According** to their website, the **deadline** /ˈdedlaɪn/ (n. 截止日期) is next month."

Daniel felt a mix of hope and fear. "I'm not a **regular** /ˈreɡjələr/ (adj. 定期的；常规的) filmmaker. I don't have connections or experience. They probably want someone with **potential** /pəˈtenʃl/ (n./adj. 潜力；可能的), not just passion."

"**Less** /les/ (adj./adv. 更少的；较少地) self-doubt, more action," Lisa countered. "Every great **author** or director was a **starter** /ˈstɑːrtər/ (n. 初学者；起动装置) once. Besides, your story about your grandmother—it has real power. It could be a **gorgeous** piece of cinema if you let it take **shape**."

Over the following weeks, Daniel worked intensely. He refined the script, frame **by** /baɪ/ (prep. 通过；由) frame. The opening would **call** back to his grandmother's childhood, showing her village before violence tore it apart. The middle would explore her journey as a refugee, learning to **live** in a foreign land. The **last** act would reveal her triumph—small, quiet, but profound—as she taught Daniel to cook her traditional dishes, passing **down** /daʊn/ (adv./prep. 向下) her heritage through food.

When he finally submitted the application, Daniel felt exhausted but proud. The **commission** would review hundreds of proposals. His chances seemed **less** than slim. But he had tried, and that alone felt like victory.

Months passed. Daniel continued his **regular** routine—writing in the morning, waiting tables at night. His **savings** /ˈseɪvɪŋz/ (n. 储蓄；存款) were almost gone. He considered giving up, moving back home, accepting a more stable life. The creative path felt like a gamble **by** **default** /dɪˈfɔːlt/ (n. 违约；默认)—one that **according** to statistics, most people lost.

Then, one afternoon, a **call** came. Daniel's hand shook as he answered. "This is the **engineering** /ˌendʒɪˈnɪrɪŋ/ (n. 工程；设计) arts **commission**," a voice said. (He later learned they called it "**engineering**" because they saw filmmaking as constructing emotional architecture.) "Your project has been selected. You're **eligible** for a production grant."

Daniel couldn't speak. **Chaos** /ˈkeɪɑːs/ (n. 混乱；混沌) erupted in his mind—joy, disbelief, fear. "Are you sure?" he finally managed. "There's no mistake?"

"No mistake. Your script has real **potential**. We'd like you to be one of our **regular** supported artists. **According** to our evaluation, your work demonstrates both technical skill and emotional depth. You've captured something essential about the refugee experience—not as a tragedy **who** defines people, but as a challenge they **shape** themselves around."

After the **call**, Daniel sat in silence. Then he laughed, then cried. He picked up his phone to **call** Lisa, his grandmother's sister, everyone **who** had believed in him. His **saving** grace had been stubbornness—refusing to quit even when the odds seemed impossible.

Weeks later, **shooting** began. The production was small and intense. Daniel discovered he loved directing, **shaping** each scene like a sculptor. One day, while **filming** /ˈfɪlmɪŋ/ (v. 拍摄) a **cooking** /ˈkʊkɪŋ/ (n. 烹饪) sequence, the lead actress improvised a line: "Food isn't just **carbohydrate**s and protein. It's memory, it's love." Daniel knew that line would stay.

The finished **film** premiered at a small festival. Critics called it "a **gorgeous** meditation on identity and survival." But the moment that mattered most was when his grandmother's sister watched it and whispered, "She would have been so proud."

Daniel had learned important lessons. First, **less** planning and more action sometimes worked better. Second, being a **starter** didn't mean being unworthy—everyone began somewhere. Third, **potential** meant nothing without the courage to pursue it. And **last**, the stories we tell **shape** not only how others see us, but how we see ourselves.

That night, Daniel cooked the same dish his grandmother had taught him—the one featured in the **film**. As steam rose from the **pot**, he felt her presence. He was **living** the life she had hoped for him: one of purpose, creativity, and connection. The journey from that cold **dry** night of doubt to this moment of fulfillment proved that sometimes, the **default** path isn't the right one. Sometimes, you have to **knock** on different doors, take risks, and trust that your voice matters.

His **neck** no longer ached from stress but from honest work. He was **eligible** now—not because anyone gave him permission, but because he had claimed it. And that made all the difference.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

丹尼尔坐在他的小公寓里，盯着一张空白的纸。他想作为一名作家生活，但账单要求他做服务员。他桌上的咖啡壶几小时前就已经冷了。他的眼睛感到干涩，脖子因为趴在笔记本电脑上而疼痛。然而他无法停止。这个在他脑海中生活了几个月的电影剧本需要在世界上存在。

独立电影的拍摄是昂贵的，但丹尼尔相信他的故事很重要。这是关于他祖母的，一个在战争中幸存下来并从无到有建立生活的女人。她的身形随着年龄变得小而弯曲，但她的精神是华丽的。

敲门声打断了他的思绪。是他的朋友丽莎，一位写关于食物和文化的作家。"你看起来糟透了，"她根据她一贯的直率诚实说道。"你上次吃东西是什么时候？"

丹尼尔耸了耸肩。"我想我昨天吃了一顿碳水化合物含量高的午餐。"他的节约恩典是他在一家员工免费吃饭的餐厅工作。"如果我做意大利面，算晚餐吗？"

"只要你有资格做饭而不烧毁建筑，"丽莎开玩笑说。她注意到散落在周围的剧本页。"还在做电影？你知道有一个委员会正在审查新兴电影制作人的资助。根据他们的网站，截止日期是下个月。"

丹尼尔感到既希望又害怕。"我不是一个常规电影制作人。我没有人脉或经验。他们可能想要有潜力的人，而不仅仅是激情。"

"少点自我怀疑，多点行动，"丽莎反驳道。"每一位伟大的作家或导演都曾经是初学者。此外，你关于你祖母的故事——它具有真正的力量。如果你让它成形，它可能成为一部华丽的电影。"

在接下来的几周里，丹尼尔努力工作。他完善了剧本，一帧一帧。开场将回溯到他祖母的童年，展示暴力撕裂之前的村庄。中间将探索她作为难民的旅程，学习在异国他乡生活。最后一幕将揭示她的胜利——小而安静，但深刻——当她教丹尼尔烹饪她的传统菜肴，通过食物传承她的遗产。

当他最终提交申请时，丹尼尔感到精疲力尽但自豪。委员会将审查数百份提案。他的机会似乎微乎其微。但他尝试了，仅此一项就感觉像是胜利。

几个月过去了。丹尼尔继续他的常规日程——早上写作，晚上当服务员。他的储蓄几乎用完了。他考虑放弃，搬回家，接受一个更稳定的生活。创意之路感觉像是一场默认的赌博——根据统计，大多数人都会输。

然后，一天下午，一个电话来了。丹尼尔的手在接听时颤抖。"这是工程艺术委员会，"一个声音说。（他后来得知他们称之为"工程"，因为他们认为电影制作是构建情感建筑。）"你的项目已被选中。你有资格获得制作资助。"

丹尼尔说不出话来。混乱在他脑海中爆发——喜悦、难以置信、恐惧。"你确定吗？"他终于设法说道。"没有错误吗？"

"没有错误。你的剧本有真正的潜力。我们希望你成为我们定期支持的艺术家之一。根据我们的评估，你的作品展示了技术技能和情感深度。你捕捉到了难民经历的一些本质——不是作为定义人们的悲剧，而是作为他们围绕其塑造自己的挑战。"

电话结束后，丹尼尔坐着沉默。然后他笑了，然后哭了。他拿起电话打给丽莎、他祖母的妹妹、每一个相信他的人。他的节约恩典是固执——即使在几率似乎不可能时也拒绝放弃。

几周后，拍摄开始了。制作规模小而紧张。丹尼尔发现他喜欢导演，像雕塑家一样塑造每个场景。一天，在拍摄烹饪序列时，主演即兴发挥了一句台词："食物不仅仅是碳水化合物和蛋白质。它是记忆，它是爱。"丹尼尔知道这句话会留下来。

完成的电影在一个小型电影节首映。评论家称其为"关于身份和生存的华丽冥想"。但最重要的时刻是当他祖母的妹妹观看时低声说："她会为此感到骄傲。"

丹尼尔学到了重要的教训。首先，少计划多行动有时效果更好。其次，作为初学者并不意味着不值得——每个人都从某个地方开始。第三，没有追求的勇气，潜力毫无意义。最后，我们讲述的故事不仅塑造了别人如何看待我们，还塑造了我们如何看待自己。

那天晚上，丹尼尔烹饪了他祖母教他的同一道菜——电影中出现的那道。当蒸汽从锅里升起时，他感到了她的存在。他正在过着她为他希望的生活：一个有目标、有创造力、有联系的生活。从那个寒冷干燥的怀疑之夜到这个成就的时刻的旅程证明，有时候，默认的道路不是正确的。有时候，你必须敲不同的门，冒险，并相信你的声音很重要。

他的脖子不再因压力而疼痛，而是因为诚实的工作。他现在有资格了——不是因为任何人给了他许可，而是因为他自己争取到了。这就是所有的差异。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "He wanted to live as a writer, but bills demanded he work as a waiter."

**分析：** 使用"but"连接对比的两个愿望和现实。"demand + 宾语 + (should) do"结构表示强烈要求，这里省略了"should"。这种对比展现了追梦者面临的现实困境。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "Less self-doubt, more action."

**分析：** 省略式对比结构，完整形式是"[Have] less self-doubt, [take] more action."。这种简洁有力的表达方式常用于建议和激励，比完整句式更有冲击力。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "Every great author or director was a starter once."

**分析：** 使用过去时"was"强调历史事实，安慰和鼓励当前的初学者。"once"（曾经）暗示时间的流逝和成长的可能性。这种句式在激励话语中非常有效。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "Not as a tragedy who defines people, but as a challenge they shape themselves around."

**分析：** "not as A but as B"结构表达对比和纠正。使用"shape themselves around"（围绕...塑造自己）这个动态表达，强调人的主动性而非被动受害。

---

### 句子5
**原句：** "Sometimes, the default path isn't the right one."

**分析：** 简单句表达深刻道理。"default path"（默认路径）是现代生活的隐喻，表示社会期待的常规选择。这种表达将抽象概念具象化。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点1：省略式祈使句（Elliptical Imperative）

**原句：** "Less self-doubt, more action."

**聚焦：** 省略动词的祈使句

**用法：** 在口语和激励性语言中，可以省略祈使句的动词部分，只保留关键信息。这种结构简洁有力，常用于口号、建议和鼓励。

**完整形式：** Have less self-doubt, take more action.

**可替换句：** You should have less self-doubt and take more action.（使用完整句式，失去简洁性和冲击力）

---

### 焦点2：定语从句修饰人（Relative Clause with "who")

**原句：** "It was about his grandmother, a woman who had survived war and built a life from nothing."

**聚焦：** "who had survived..."定语从句

**用法：** "who"引导定语从句修饰人（woman）。过去完成时"had survived"和"built"强调这些行为发生在故事主要时间线之前，建立背景信息。

**可替换句：** It was about his grandmother. She was a woman. She had survived war and built a life from nothing.（分成三个句子，不够流畅）

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **What was Daniel's day job and what did he want to do?**  
   He worked as a waiter but wanted to live as a writer/filmmaker.

2. **What was Daniel's film about?**  
   It was about his grandmother who survived war, became a refugee, and built a new life, passing down her heritage through cooking.

3. **How did Lisa encourage Daniel?**  
   She told him about the commission grant opportunity and encouraged him to have less self-doubt and take more action.

4. **What happened when Daniel finally received the phone call?**  
   He learned that his project had been selected for a production grant, making him eligible for funding.

5. **What if Daniel had given up before submitting his application? How might his life have been different?**  
   (Open answer: Students should consider missed opportunities, unfulfilled potential, and the importance of perseverance.)

   **Answer:** commission

2. She has great __________ (potential) as a musician.
   **Answer:** potential

3. __________ (According) to the report, sales have increased.
   **Answer:** According

   **Answer:** eligible

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

**Topic:** Write about a creative project or goal you've pursued or would like to pursue. What obstacles have you faced or do you anticipate? What motivates you to continue?

**Length:** 80-100 words

**提示：** 使用本组至少5个词汇，如potential, regular, shape, call, author等。

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion）

> **"Is it more important to follow a stable, predictable career path or to pursue creative passions that may not guarantee financial security?"**
