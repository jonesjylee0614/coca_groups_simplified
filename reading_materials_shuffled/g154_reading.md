## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

**However** /haʊˈevər/ (adv./conj. 然而;无论如何), the morning started **like** /laɪk/ (prep./v. 像;喜欢) any other Tuesday. Chen Wei sat at the kitchen table, checking the **document** /ˈdɑːkjumənt/ (n. 文件;文献) on his **laptop** /ˈlæptɒp/ (n. 笔记本电脑). His **family** /ˈfæməli/ (n. 家庭;家人) was still sleeping. There was **no** /noʊ/ (det./adv. 没有;不) sound except the quiet hum of the computer.

Then his phone rang. It was the hospital. "Mr. Chen, we **need** /niːd/ (v./n. 需要) you here right away. There's been an **emergency** /ɪˈmɜːrdʒənsi/ (n. 紧急情况;急诊)." His father had suffered a heart attack. Chen Wei felt **terror** /ˈterər/ (n. 恐怖;可怕的人或事) grip him. **No**, not now. Not his father.

**Meanwhile** /ˈmiːnwaɪl/ (adv. 同时;其间), his wife Lin woke up and found him at the door. "What happened?" she asked. He couldn't **talk** /tɔːk/ (v./n. 谈话;交谈) at first. The words caught in his throat. Finally, he managed to say, "It's Dad. We **need** to go now."

At the hospital, Chen Wei met Dr. Wang, the **candidate** /ˈkændɪdeɪt/ (n. 候选人;申请者) for head of cardiology. "Your father is in **complete** /kəmˈpliːt/ (adj./v. 完全的;完整的;完成) stable condition now," Dr. Wang said. "**However**, the next few days are important. He'll **need** constant care."

Chen Wei's mother arrived **later** /ˈleɪtər/ (adv./adj. 后来;较晚的), her face showing **clear** /klɪr/ (adj./v. 清楚的;清澈的;清除) signs of worry. "Can we see him?" she asked. The doctor nodded. Inside the room, Chen Wei's father lay connected to machines. He looked small and weak, nothing **like** the strong man who had raised him.

"Dad," Chen Wei whispered. His father's eyes opened slowly. "**No** crying," the old man said with a weak smile. "I'm still here." Chen Wei let out a **gasp** /ɡæsp/ (n./v. 喘气;倒抽气) of relief. His father was still himself, still trying to stay strong.

Over the next week, Chen Wei learned many things. He learned that the hospital **accounting** /əˈkaʊntɪŋ/ (n. 会计;会计学) department would **need** detailed insurance **documents**. He learned that **error** /ˈerər/ (n. 错误;差错) in medicine could be deadly, and that every decision mattered. But most of all, he learned to **admire** /ədˈmaɪr/ (v. 钦佩;赞赏) his mother in a new way.

She stayed by his father's side day and night. She **talked** to the doctors, asked questions, and kept careful notes. When the hospital food was bad, she cooked soup at home and brought it in containers. She was not **armed** /ɑːrmd/ (adj. 武装的;配备的) with medical knowledge, but she was armed with love and determination.

"Your mother is strong," a nurse told Chen Wei. "I've seen many families here. The love between them is **mutual** /ˈmjuːtʃuəl/ (adj. 相互的;共同的). That helps healing." Chen Wei nodded. He thought about how his parents had always been a team. When he was young, his father had been the one everyone noticed—the **chef** /ʃef/ (n. 厨师;主厨) who ran a famous restaurant. His mother had worked in **accounting**, managing the books. Different roles, but **mutual** respect and love.

One afternoon, as Chen Wei sat by his father's bed, the old man **talked** about the past. "When I was young," he said, "I thought I needed to **inherit** /ɪnˈherɪt/ (v. 继承;遗传) my father's restaurant. I felt like I had **no** choice. **However**, **later** I realized that what I really **inherited** was not the restaurant, but the love of cooking, the joy of feeding people. That was the real gift."

"You chose your own path," Chen Wei said. His father nodded. "Yes. And you should too. Don't feel you must follow me. The **emergency** today made me think. Life is short. Do what makes you happy. **No** **terror** should stop you from living fully."

Chen Wei felt something **clear** in his mind. He had been working in **accounting** **like** his mother, but it had never felt right. He had always wanted to write, to **talk** about ideas through stories. He had been a **candidate** for a writing program once, but turned it down because he thought he should earn more money to help his **family**.

"Dad," he said, "I want to **talk** to you about something. I've been thinking of going back to school." His father smiled. "Good. Life isn't **complete** without following your dreams. Your mother and I, we **need** you to be happy more than we **need** your money."

Two weeks **later**, Chen Wei's father came home. The recovery would be slow, but the doctors said there had been **no** major damage. The **terror** of almost losing him was fading, replaced by gratitude. Chen Wei had **completed** his application to the writing program. **Meanwhile**, he was learning to cook from his father, who **insisted** on teaching him family recipes, even though he was supposed to be resting.

"These recipes are part of what I'll **inherit** from you," Chen Wei said, laughing as he tried to **string** /strɪŋ/ (n./v. 线;串;一系列) green beans correctly. His father watched with approval. "And I **admire** your courage to follow your heart. That's what I hope you **inherit** most of all."

Lin took a photo of them cooking together and sent it to the **family** group chat. "**No** more hospital food!" she wrote. Everyone replied with happy messages. Chen Wei looked at his phone and smiled. His mother sent a private message: "I **admire** you both. Your father for his **strength**, and you for making him so proud."

Chen Wei realized that the **emergency** had been terrible, but it had also brought **clarity**. Sometimes it takes facing **terror** to see what really matters. His **family**, their **mutual** love and support—that was what life was about. Everything else was just details in a **document**, numbers in **accounting** books. What mattered was being here, being present, being true to yourself while loving others.

As he watched his father show him how to **chop** vegetables the right way, Chen Wei thought about the **candidate** he would be at the writing program—**no** longer **like** someone pretending to be someone else, but himself, **complete** and **clear** about his path. And that felt right.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

然而,这个早晨开始时就像其他任何一个星期二。陈伟坐在厨房的桌子旁,在笔记本电脑上检查文件。他的家人还在睡觉。除了电脑安静的嗡嗡声,没有其他声音。

然后他的电话响了。是医院打来的。"陈先生,我们需要你马上来。有紧急情况。"他的父亲心脏病发作了。陈伟感到恐惧攫住了他。不,不要现在。不要是他父亲。

同时,他的妻子林醒了,发现他在门口。"发生了什么?"她问。他一开始说不出话来。话语卡在喉咙里。最后,他勉强说道:"是爸爸。我们现在需要去。"

在医院,陈伟见到了王医生,心脏科主任的候选人。"你父亲现在情况完全稳定,"王医生说。"然而,接下来几天很重要。他需要持续护理。"

陈伟的母亲后来到了,脸上清楚地显示出担忧的迹象。"我们能看他吗?"她问。医生点点头。在房间里,陈伟的父亲躺着,连接着机器。他看起来又小又弱,一点也不像养育他的那个强壮的人。

"爸爸,"陈伟低声说。他父亲的眼睛慢慢睁开。"不要哭,"老人带着虚弱的微笑说。"我还在这里。"陈伟倒抽一口气,如释重负。他父亲还是他自己,仍然努力保持坚强。

在接下来的一周里,陈伟学到了很多东西。他了解到医院会计部门需要详细的保险文件。他了解到医学上的错误可能是致命的,每个决定都很重要。但最重要的是,他学会了以一种新的方式钦佩他的母亲。

她日夜守在他父亲身边。她与医生交谈,提出问题,并仔细记录。当医院的食物很糟糕时,她在家煮汤,用容器带来。她没有配备医学知识,但她配备着爱和决心。

"你母亲很坚强,"一位护士告诉陈伟。"我在这里见过很多家庭。他们之间的爱是相互的。这有助于康复。"陈伟点点头。他想到他的父母一直是一个团队。当他年轻时,他父亲是每个人注意的那个人——经营着一家著名餐厅的厨师。他母亲从事会计工作,管理账目。不同的角色,但相互尊重和爱。

一天下午,当陈伟坐在父亲床边时,老人谈起了过去。"当我年轻时,"他说,"我以为我需要继承我父亲的餐厅。我觉得我没有选择。然而,后来我意识到我真正继承的不是餐厅,而是对烹饪的热爱,喂养人们的快乐。那才是真正的礼物。"

"你选择了自己的道路,"陈伟说。他父亲点点头。"是的。你也应该这样。不要觉得你必须跟随我。今天的紧急情况让我思考。生命短暂。做让你快乐的事。任何恐惧都不应该阻止你充分生活。"

陈伟感到头脑中有些东西变得清晰了。他一直像他母亲一样从事会计工作,但从未感觉对。他一直想写作,通过故事谈论想法。他曾经是一个写作项目的候选人,但拒绝了,因为他认为他应该赚更多的钱来帮助他的家人。

"爸爸,"他说,"我想和你谈谈。我一直在考虑回学校。"他父亲微笑着。"好。没有追随你的梦想,生活就不完整。你母亲和我,我们需要你快乐,胜过需要你的钱。"

两周后,陈伟的父亲回家了。康复会很慢,但医生说没有重大损伤。几乎失去他的恐惧正在消退,被感激所取代。陈伟已经完成了写作项目的申请。同时,他正在跟父亲学习烹饪,父亲坚持教他家传食谱,尽管他应该休息。

"这些食谱是我将从你那里继承的一部分,"陈伟笑着说,一边努力正确地串四季豆。他父亲赞许地看着。"我钦佩你追随内心的勇气。那是我最希望你继承的。"

林给他们一起做饭拍了照片,发到家庭群聊。"不再吃医院的食物了!"她写道。每个人都回复了快乐的消息。陈伟看着他的手机微笑。他母亲发了私信:"我钦佩你们俩。你父亲的力量,和你让他如此自豪。"

陈伟意识到紧急情况很可怕,但它也带来了清晰。有时需要面对恐惧才能看到真正重要的东西。他的家人,他们相互的爱和支持——这就是生活的意义。其他一切只是文件中的细节,会计账簿中的数字。重要的是在这里,活在当下,忠于自己同时爱着他人。

当他看着父亲向他展示如何正确切菜时,陈伟想到他在写作项目中将成为的候选人——不再像某个假装成别人的人,而是他自己,完整而清楚地知道自己的道路。这感觉是对的。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "However, the morning started like any other Tuesday."

**分析:** "However" 置于句首,作为转折连词引出对比。"like any other Tuesday" 使用 "like" 作介词,意为"像...一样",这是日常英语中非常常见的表达方式。

**亮点:** 这句话为叙事设置了"平静的开始",为后续的突发事件制造对比,是经典的故事开场技巧。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "Chen Wei felt terror grip him. No, not now. Not his father."

**分析:** 第一句使用 "feel + 宾语 + 动词原形" 的结构,生动描绘情感。后两句是短促的内心独白,用重复的否定词 "No" 和 "Not" 表达强烈的拒绝情绪。

**亮点:** 长句和短句的结合,加上内心独白的运用,让读者深刻感受到主角的恐慌和不愿接受现实的心理。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "She was not armed with medical knowledge, but she was armed with love and determination."

**分析:** 使用 "armed with" (配备着) 的比喻,将抽象的爱和决心比作武器。前后两个 "armed with" 形成平行对比结构,第一个否定,第二个肯定。

**亮点:** 将 "armed" 从字面意义(武装)延伸到比喻意义(具备、配备),这种一词多用的技巧让表达更有力量。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "Life isn't complete without following your dreams."

**分析:** 使用双重否定 "isn't...without..." 来表达肯定意义,这比直接说 "Life is complete when you follow your dreams" 更有强调效果。

**亮点:** 双重否定的使用增强了语气,让建议听起来更加确定和有力。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: 并列转折连词 however 的使用

**原句:** "However, the morning started like any other Tuesday."

**聚焦:** however 作转折连词的位置和用法

**用法说明:** 
"However" 可以放在句首、句中或句末,表示转折。放在句首时,后面通常加逗号。它比 "but" 更正式,常用于书面语。

**位置对比:**
- 句首: However, the plan failed.
- 句中: The plan, however, failed.
- 句末: The plan failed, however.

**与 but 的区别:**
- but: 并列连词,连接两个并列句,不能单独开启新句
- however: 副词,可以单独开启新句,更正式

**可替换句:** "But the morning started like any other Tuesday." (更口语化)

---

### 聚焦点 2: need 作情态动词和实义动词

**原句:** "We need to go now." 和 "He'll need constant care."

**聚焦:** need 的两种用法

**用法说明:**
"need" 既可以作实义动词(后接 to do),也可以作情态动词(后接动词原形,多用于否定和疑问句)。在现代英语中,作实义动词更常见。

**作实义动词:**
- 肯定句: We need to leave. ✓
- 否定句: We don't need to leave. ✓
- 疑问句: Do we need to leave? ✓

**作情态动词(较少用,更正式):**
- 否定句: We needn't leave. (英式英语)
- 疑问句: Need we leave? (较罕见)

**注意:** 在美式英语中,"need" 几乎总是作实义动词使用。
