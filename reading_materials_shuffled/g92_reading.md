## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The courtroom was **never** /ˈnevər/ (adv. 从不;决不) silent—not truly. There were whispers, coughs, the **sound** /saʊnd/ (n. 声音) of papers shuffling. **Judge** /dʒʌdʒ/ (n./v. 法官;判断) Martinez sat behind her bench, preparing to **analyze** /ˈænəlaɪz/ (v. 分析) the most difficult case of her career.

The **divorce** /dɪˈvɔːrs/ (n./v. 离婚) proceeding before her involved a **massive** /ˈmæsɪv/ (adj. 巨大的;大量的) fortune and two people who had once loved each other. Sarah, a businesswoman who had **consistently** /kənˈsɪstəntli/ (adv. 一直;一贯地) built her career, now faced **accusations** /ˌækjuˈzeɪʃnz/ (n. 指控) from her husband, Marcus, that she had been **racist** /ˈreɪsɪst/ (adj./n. 种族主义的;种族主义者) toward his family.

The case had sparked **interest** /ˈɪntrəst/ (n. 兴趣;利益) across the **county** /ˈkaʊnti/ (n. 县;郡), partly because both were public figures, but also because the **payment** /ˈpeɪmənt/ (n. 支付;报酬) arrangements would affect charitable organizations. The judge knew she needed to see **through** /θruː/ (prep. 通过;穿过) the anger to find truth.

"The situation is **awful** /ˈɔːfl/ (adj. 可怕的;糟糕的)," Judge Martinez thought. Marcus claimed Sarah had **hurt** /hɜːrt/ (v. 伤害; n. 痛苦) him emotionally and financially. He wanted half of everything, including the **supply** /səˈplaɪ/ (n./v. 供应;供给) business Sarah had built **before** /bɪˈfɔːr/ (prep./conj. 在...之前) they married.

During testimony, tensions rose. Marcus's lawyer tried to **trigger** /ˈtrɪɡər/ (v. 触发;引起) emotional responses, bringing up painful memories. Sarah remained calm, though Judge Martinez could see the strain. The businesswoman explained that her **cabinet** /ˈkæbɪnət/ (n. 橱柜;内阁) of advisors had warned her about prenuptial agreements, but love had made her ignore the **tip** /tɪp/ (n. 建议;小费; v. 倾斜).

"I **never** claimed to be **perfect** /ˈpɜːrfɪkt/ (adj. 完美的)," Sarah said quietly. "But I'm not the person he's describing. **Consequently** /ˈkɑːnsəkwentli/ (adv. 因此;所以), I ask the court to examine the evidence fairly."

Judge Martinez studied the financial records. There was no pattern of discrimination or malice. **Ultimately** /ˈʌltɪmətli/ (adv. 最终;根本上), she had to **judge** based on facts, not emotions. The **ranking** /ˈræŋkɪŋ/ (n. 排名;等级) of credibility favored Sarah's version of events.

"This case has been **magical** /ˈmædʒɪkl/ (adj. 魔术的;神奇的) in no way," the judge began her ruling. "It represents how **other** /ˈʌðər/ (adj./pron. 其他的) couples might learn from mistakes. Neither party is entirely blameless, but the **accusations** of **racism** /ˈreɪsɪzəm/ (n. 种族主义) lack **supporting** /səˈpɔːrtɪŋ/ (v. 支持) evidence."

She paused, looking at both parties. "Marriage requires **consistency** /kənˈsɪstənsi/ (n. 一致性;连贯性) in kindness. **Through** the years, you both failed at times. But destroying each other won't heal the **hurt**."

Judge Martinez divided the assets fairly, tied a metaphorical **rope** /roʊp/ (n. 绳子) around the loose ends, and concluded: "**Ultimately**, you must both move forward. The **payments** /ˈpeɪmənts/ (n. 付款) are settled. Now work on healing **yourselves** /jɔːrˈselvz/ (pron. 你们自己)."

As the court adjourned, Judge Martinez reflected that nothing about **judging** /ˈdʒʌdʒɪŋ/ (v. 判断) people's lives was **perfect**. The real **magic** /ˈmædʒɪk/ (n. 魔法;魔力) would be if both learned to stop **hurting** /ˈhɜːrtɪŋ/ (v. 伤害) each **other** and found peace.

"情况很糟糕,"马丁内斯法官想。马库斯声称莎拉在情感和经济上伤害了他。他想要一切的一半,包括莎拉在他们结婚前建立的供应企业。

"我从来没有声称自己是完美的,"莎拉平静地说。"但我不是他描述的那个人。因此,我请求法庭公平地审查证据。"

"这个案子绝不神奇,"法官开始她的裁决。"它代表了其他夫妇如何从错误中学习。双方都不是完全无可指责的,但种族主义的指控缺乏支持证据。"

她停顿了一下,看着双方。"婚姻需要善良的一致性。这些年来,你们都曾失败过。但互相摧毁不会治愈伤害。"

马丁内斯法官公平地分配了资产,为松散的结局系上了一根隐喻的绳子,并总结道:"最终,你们都必须向前看。付款已经解决。现在努力治愈你们自己。"

法庭休庭后,马丁内斯法官反思,判断人们的生活没有什么是完美的。真正的魔力将是,如果双方都学会停止伤害彼此并找到平静。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "The judge knew she needed to see through the anger to find truth."

**分析:** "see through" 是短语动词,意为"看透;识破"。不定式"to find truth"表示目的。

**亮点:** 使用短语动词让表达更地道,体现了法官需要超越表面情绪的洞察力。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "Ultimately, she had to judge based on facts, not emotions."

**分析:** "based on" 表示"基于...",not emotions" 否定对比,强调应该依据什么。副词 "ultimately" 位于句首,强调最终的决定原则。

**亮点:** 简洁地表达了司法公正的核心原则。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "Marriage requires consistency in kindness."

**分析:** "require" 后接名词作宾语。"consistency in" 表示"在...方面的一致性"。这是一个哲理性的陈述句。

**亮点:** 用简单的句式表达深刻的婚姻智慧。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "The real magic would be if both learned to stop hurting each other and found peace."

**分析:** "would be if..." 是虚拟语气结构,表示假设。"learn to do" 和 "find" 是并列的动词短语,由"and"连接。

**亮点:** 用"magic"的隐喻和虚拟语气表达美好的愿望,结尾富有诗意和哲理。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: "see through" 短语动词

**原句:** "The judge knew she needed to see through the anger to find truth."

**聚焦:** 短语动词 "see through" 的用法

**用法说明:** 
"see through" 有多个含义:1) 看透;识破;2) 完成;进行到底。这里是第一个意思,表示透过表象看到本质。

**类似短语动词:**
- "look through" (浏览;审阅)
- "go through" (经历;检查)
- "get through" (完成;接通)

**可替换句:** "The judge knew she needed to look beyond the anger to find truth." (用 look beyond,意思相近)

---

### 聚焦点 2: 虚拟语气 "would be if..."

**原句:** "The real magic would be if both learned to stop hurting each other."

**聚焦:** "would be if..." 表示假设的虚拟语气

**用法说明:**
这种结构用于表达对未来的假设或愿望。"would be"是条件句的主句,"if..."是条件从句,这里省略了"it"作形式主语。

**完整结构:**
"It would be real magic if both learned..."

**对比:**
- 真实条件: "It will be magic if they learn..." (认为可能发生)
- 虚拟条件: "It would be magic if they learned..." (不太可能但希望发生)

**可替换句:** "The real magic would occur if both learned to stop hurting each other." (用 occur,更正式)
