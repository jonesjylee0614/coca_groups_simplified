## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Elena walked **along** /əˈlɔːŋ/ (prep. 沿着) the corridor of the research lab, her mind racing. The **automatic** /ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk/ (adj. 自动的；无意识的) doors slid open as she approached, a small reminder of how much technology had changed everything. She clutched a **newspaper** /ˈnuːzpeɪpər/ (n. 报纸) article about **nuclear** /ˈnuːkliər/ (adj. 核的；核能的) energy accidents. The piece was meant to **scare** /sker/ (v. 使害怕；惊吓) people, **show** /ʃoʊ/ (v. 显示；展示) only the dangers, never the potential.

But Elena knew better. For months, she had been working on a **strategy** /ˈstrætədʒi/ (n. 策略；战略) to make fusion energy safer and more efficient. The **exercise** /ˈeksərsaɪz/ (n. 锻炼；练习) of testing and retesting was exhausting, but necessary. "We need to **return** /rɪˈtɜːrn/ (v. 返回；归还) to first principles," she **always** /ˈɔːlweɪz/ (adv. 总是；一直) told her team. "**Imagine** /ɪˈmædʒɪn/ (v. 想象；设想) what's possible, not just what's **difficult** /ˈdɪfɪkəlt/ (adj. 困难的)."

The problem was funding. **Countless** /ˈkaʊntləs/ (adj. 无数的；数不尽的) proposals had been rejected. Investors didn't see the **incentive** /ɪnˈsentɪv/ (n. 激励；刺激) to **exercise** patience when solar and wind power seemed easier. But Elena believed fusion was the **response** /rɪˈspɑːns/ (n. 回应；反应) to the planet's energy crisis. The **heat** /hiːt/ (n. 热；热度) generated could power cities for **countless** years without the **protection** /prəˈtekʃn/ (n. 保护) concerns of older **nuclear** plants.

One evening, **again** /əˈɡen/ (adv. 再次；又) facing rejection, Elena felt **tired** /ˈtaɪərd/ (adj. 疲倦的；厌倦的). She sat at her desk, staring at the **image** /ˈɪmɪdʒ/ (n. 图像；形象) on her computer screen—a simulation of the fusion reactor. A colleague, David, opened a **tab** /tæb/ (n. 标签；账单) on his laptop to **show** her something. "Look at this," he said. "There's a new fund for **luck** /lʌk/ (n. 运气)—I mean, for risky innovations."

Elena **hardly** /ˈhɑːrdli/ (adv. 几乎不；刚刚) dared to hope. **Along** with the application, they would need to present their work at a conference. The pressure was intense, but she felt a spark of determination. "Let's **bother** /ˈbɑːðər/ (v. 打扰；费心) to do this right," she said. "No shortcuts."

The presentation day arrived. The room was full—scientists, investors, policymakers. Elena took a breath and began. She explained how their design could **loop** /luːp/ (n./v. 循环；环) energy production sustainably. She showed data proving the **automatic** safety features. When someone asked about risks, she didn't **shove** /ʃʌv/ (v. 推；挤) the question aside. Instead, she addressed it directly: "Every technology carries risk. Our **strategy** is to minimize it through rigorous testing and transparent **protection** protocols."

One investor raised a hand. "Why should we fund this when success seems so distant and **difficult**?" Elena paused, then smiled. "Because the **return** on investment isn't just financial. It's the survival of future generations. We **always** have a choice—wait for **luck** to save us or create solutions ourselves."

The silence that followed felt eternal. Then, slowly, applause filled the room. By the end of the day, they had secured initial funding. Elena could **hardly** believe it. That night, she opened her **newspaper** to find an article about the conference. The headline read: "Fusion Energy Gets Second Chance." She felt **tired** but fulfilled.

Walking home **along** the river, Elena thought about the long road ahead. There would be setbacks, moments when she'd want to **scare** herself into quitting. But she also knew: **imagine** what could be achieved if they didn't give up. The **loop** of doubt and determination would continue, but so would the work. And that, she realized, was enough.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

埃琳娜沿着研究实验室的走廊走着，她的思绪飞转。当她接近时，自动门滑开了，这是一个小小的提醒，说明技术改变了一切。她紧握着一篇关于核能事故的报纸文章。这篇文章旨在吓唬人们，只展示危险，从不展示潜力。

但埃琳娜知道得更多。几个月来，她一直在制定一个策略，使聚变能源更安全、更高效。测试和重新测试的练习令人精疲力尽，但却是必要的。"我们需要回到第一原理，"她总是告诉她的团队。"想象可能的事情，而不仅仅是困难的事情。"

问题在于资金。无数的提案被拒绝了。投资者看不到在太阳能和风能似乎更容易的情况下保持耐心的激励。但埃琳娜相信聚变是应对地球能源危机的回应。产生的热量可以为城市供电无数年，而不会有老式核电站的保护问题。

一天晚上，再次面临拒绝，埃琳娜感到疲倦。她坐在办公桌前，盯着电脑屏幕上的图像——聚变反应堆的模拟。一位同事大卫在他的笔记本电脑上打开了一个标签来向她展示一些东西。"看看这个，"他说。"有一个新的基金用于运气——我是说，用于冒险的创新。"

埃琳娜几乎不敢抱希望。除了申请之外，他们还需要在会议上展示他们的工作。压力很大，但她感到了一丝决心。"让我们费心把这件事做好，"她说。"不走捷径。"

演讲的日子到了。房间里挤满了人——科学家、投资者、政策制定者。埃琳娜深吸一口气，开始了。她解释了他们的设计如何能够可持续地循环能源生产。她展示了证明自动安全功能的数据。当有人问到风险时，她没有把问题推到一边。相反，她直接回答："每项技术都有风险。我们的策略是通过严格的测试和透明的保护协议来最小化它。"

一位投资者举起了手。"当成功似乎如此遥远和困难时，我们为什么要资助这个？"埃琳娜停顿了一下，然后微笑着说："因为投资回报不仅仅是财务上的。这是后代的生存。我们总是有选择——等待运气来拯救我们或自己创造解决方案。"

随后的沉默感觉永恒。然后，慢慢地，掌声充满了房间。到当天结束时，他们获得了初始资金。埃琳娜几乎不敢相信。那天晚上，她打开报纸，发现了一篇关于会议的文章。标题是："聚变能源获得第二次机会。"她感到疲倦但满足。

沿着河边走回家，埃琳娜想到了前方的漫长道路。会有挫折，会有她想吓自己放弃的时刻。但她也知道：想象如果他们不放弃能取得什么成就。怀疑和决心的循环将继续，但工作也会继续。她意识到，这就足够了。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "She clutched a newspaper article about nuclear energy accidents."

**分析：** "clutch"（紧握）这个动词比简单的"hold"更能传达紧张和重要性。"about"引导介词短语，说明文章的主题。这种具象化描写让读者感受到人物的情绪状态。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "Imagine what's possible, not just what's difficult."

**分析：** 祈使句用于激励和建议。"what's possible vs. what's difficult"形成对比，这种对比结构在演讲和说服性语言中非常有力。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "Elena could hardly believe it."

**分析：** "hardly"（几乎不）是程度副词，表示否定意义，但比"not"更微妙。"can hardly believe"是常用表达，表示惊讶和难以置信。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "Because the return on investment isn't just financial. It's the survival of future generations."

**分析：** 两个短句形成递进关系。第一句否定表面的理由，第二句揭示更深层的意义。"return on investment"（投资回报）是商业术语，这里被赋予更广阔的含义。

---

### 句子5
**原句：** "The loop of doubt and determination would continue, but so would the work."

**分析：** "so + 助动词 + 主语"是倒装结构，表示"...也是如此"。这里"so would the work"呼应前半句，强调工作的持续性，语言简洁有力。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点1：祈使句表达建议（Imperative for Suggestion）

**原句：** "Imagine what's possible, not just what's difficult."

**聚焦：** 祈使句（Imagine...）+ 对比结构

**用法：** 祈使句除了表示命令，还可以表达建议、鼓励或邀请。"Imagine"开头的句子邀请听众进行思想实验，是激励性演讲的常用手法。

**可替换句：** You should imagine what's possible, not just what's difficult.（使用should，语气较弱，缺少直接的感染力）

---

### 焦点2：部分倒装（Partial Inversion）

**原句：** "The loop of doubt and determination would continue, but so would the work."

**聚焦：** so + 助动词（would）+ 主语（the work）

**用法：** "so + 助动词 + 主语"结构表示"...也一样"，用于避免重复。这种倒装让句子更加简洁优雅，常见于书面语和正式场合。

**可替换句：** The loop of doubt and determination would continue, and the work would continue too.（不使用倒装，显得啰嗦）

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **What was Elena working on in the lab?**  
   She was working on a strategy to make fusion energy safer and more efficient.

2. **Why had Elena's proposals been rejected countless times?**  
   Because investors didn't see the incentive to exercise patience when solar and wind power seemed easier, and they thought success seemed too distant and difficult.

3. **What question did Elena address directly during her presentation?**  
   She addressed questions about risk, explaining that every technology carries risk and their strategy was to minimize it through rigorous testing and transparent protection protocols.

4. **What convinced the investors to fund Elena's project?**  
   Her argument that the return on investment wasn't just financial, but the survival of future generations, and that they had a choice between waiting for luck or creating solutions themselves.

5. **What if Elena had given up after facing rejection? How might that have affected the future of fusion energy?**  
   (Open answer: Students should discuss potential impacts on innovation, climate change, and energy solutions.)

---

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. She has faced __________ (countless) challenges in her career.
   **Answer:** countless

2. The teacher tried to __________ (show) students how to solve the problem.
   **Answer:** show

3. We need a clear __________ (strategy) to succeed.
   **Answer:** strategy

4. Can you __________ (imagine) a world without poverty?
   **Answer:** imagine

5. The __________ (automatic) system makes everything easier.
   **Answer:** automatic

---

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

**Topic:** Think of a time when you or someone you know persisted despite facing many difficulties. What motivated the continued effort? What was the outcome?

**Length:** 80-100 words

**提示：** 使用本组至少5个词汇，如difficult, again, hardly, strategy, imagine等。

---

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion）

> **"Should we invest in risky but potentially transformative technologies, or focus on safer, proven solutions? Why?"**

Think about Elena's fusion energy project. In your opinion, how should society balance innovation with risk? What role should luck versus planning play in scientific advancement? Discuss with a partner or write your thoughts.
