## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The **producer** /prəˈduːsər/ (n. 制片人;生产者) sat in her small office in the **northwest** /ˌnɔːrθˈwest/ (n./adj. 西北;西北的) corner of the city, watching the rain through the window. Maya had been working in film for ten **long** /lɔːŋ/ (adj./adv. 长的;长久地) years, but success still felt **vast** /væst/ (adj. 巨大的;广阔的) and far away. She wiped the **sweat** /swet/ (n./v. 汗水;出汗) from her forehead—the office was hot, and the air conditioning had broken again.

**Presumably** /prɪˈzuːməbli/ (adv. 大概;可能), she thought, this would be another year of struggle. Her latest project, a film about **developing** /dɪˈveləpɪŋ/ (adj./v. 发展中的;发展) communities in small towns, needed funding. She had pitched it to investors, but they wanted something different. "**Please** /pliːz/ (v./interj. 请;使高兴)," one had said, "make something people want to see. Not this slow, quiet stuff."

But Maya believed in her vision. The film would show the **spirit** /ˈspɪrɪt/ (n. 精神;心灵) of small-town life, the way people supported each other, the **frequent** /ˈfriːkwənt/ (adj. 频繁的;经常的) acts of kindness that went unnoticed. She had grown up in such a town, and those memories from her **childhood** /ˈtʃaɪldhʊd/ (n. 童年;儿童时代) still shaped who she was.

That **evening** /ˈiːvnɪŋ/ (n. 傍晚;晚上), Maya received a call from her old friend James, who now **lived** /lɪvd/ (v. 居住;生活[live的过去式]) in the same **northwest** town where they had grown up. "You should come visit," he said. "Take a break. **Honestly** /ˈɑːnɪstli/ (adv. 诚实地;真的), you sound tired."

Maya thought about it. She had **prior** /ˈpraɪər/ (adj. 先前的;优先的) commitments in the city, meetings she attended **regularly** /ˈreɡjələrli/ (adv. 定期地;有规律地). But maybe James was right. Maybe she needed to reconnect with the places and feelings that inspired her work in the first place.

The next week, Maya took a bus north. The journey was **long**, but she didn't mind. She watched the city fade away and the **vast** countryside appear. Fields **rolled** /roʊld/ (v. 滚动;翻转[roll的过去式]) past the window like waves. She felt something inside her begin to shift.

When she arrived, James met her at the bus stop. "Welcome home," he said with a smile. They walked through town, past the old **pipe** /paɪp/ (n. 管子;烟斗) factory where his father had worked, past the community center with its faded **cloth** /klɔːθ/ (n. 布;织物) banners. Everything looked smaller than Maya remembered, but also more real, more solid.

That night, James and his family invited Maya for a **meal** /miːl/ (n. 餐;一顿饭). His wife Anna had cooked **substantial** /səbˈstænʃl/ (adj. 大量的;实质的) portions—roast chicken, fresh bread, vegetables from their garden. As they ate, they talked about the town's changes. A new **producer** of organic food had opened a farm nearby. Young people were starting to return, seeing value in a slower pace of life.

"The town is different now," Anna said. "People are building things, creating **substantial** businesses. It's not like when we were kids, when everyone wanted to leave."

Maya listened, feeling a **long**-buried part of herself wake up. This was the story she wanted to **tell**—not just about the past, but about how places could change while keeping their **spirit**. She thought about her film with new eyes.

The next morning, Maya woke early and went for a walk. The town was quiet in the soft light. She passed a woman opening a bakery, a man **rolling** out his cart to sell flowers, children heading to school with bright faces. These **frequent** small moments were what made life rich.

She stopped by the town's old theater, where she had watched movies as a child. A sign said it would **lower** /ˈloʊər/ (v./adj. 降低;较低的) ticket prices for a special showing that **evening**. **Presumably**, someone was trying to keep the place alive. Maya felt moved by this effort.

Back at James's house, she sat down and began writing. The ideas flowed easily now. Her film would show a **learner** /ˈlɜːrnər/ (n. 学习者;初学者) coming back to her hometown, rediscovering its value. It would show how **developing** communities could thrive without losing their character. It would be honest about the challenges but also celebrate the **spirit** of persistence.

"What are you working on?" James asked, bringing her tea.

"A new version of my film," Maya said. "**Honestly**, I think I was trying too hard to make it perfect. I forgot to make it true." She paused. "I want to **buy** /baɪ/ (v. 购买) the rights to shoot some scenes here. Would the town **please** let me do that?"

James laughed. "I think they'd love it. But you'll need to ask at the town council meeting. They meet **regularly**—next one is tomorrow **evening**."

At the meeting, Maya presented her idea. She explained how the film would show the town's **vast** potential, how it could **attract** attention to small communities everywhere. The council members listened carefully. One older man, who had **lived** there his whole life, spoke up.

"In my **childhood**," he said, "this town was everything to me. Then I grew up and thought it was too small, not important enough. But **prior** to retiring, I traveled the world, and I learned something: home isn't about size. It's about **spirit**. If your film can show that, you have my support."

Others nodded. They agreed to let Maya film, asking only that she **honestly** represent their community—both its struggles and its strengths. Maya felt **sweat** on her palms, not from heat but from emotion. This was the break she needed.

Over the next few weeks, Maya worked harder than ever. She **lived** in James's guest room, woke early, worked late. She interviewed people, captured scenes of daily life, and **regularly** returned to edit her footage. The **substantial** amount of work required dedication, but she felt the **spirit** of the project carried her forward.

One day, while filming at the old **pipe** factory that was being converted into artist studios, Maya noticed a young girl watching. The girl wore a dress made of colorful **cloth**, hand-sewn and unique. "Are you making a movie?" she asked shyly.

"Yes," Maya said. "Do you **like** movies?"

"I love them," the girl said. "**Presumably**, it's hard to make one?"

"It is," Maya admitted. "But it's also wonderful. It's like learning—you get better the more you do it. Are you a **learner** too?"

The girl nodded. "I want to make clothes. My mother says I should study **prior** to starting a business, but I already practice **regularly**."

Maya smiled. Here was the **spirit** she was trying to capture—young people with dreams, working toward them despite uncertainty. She asked if she could film the girl sewing, and the girl agreed. That footage became one of the most powerful parts of the film.

Months **later**, after countless hours of editing, Maya's film was complete. She screened it first in the **northwest** town's old theater. People filled every seat. When the lights came up, there was silence, then applause that seemed to **roll** through the room like thunder.

An older woman approached Maya with tears in her eyes. "Thank you," she said. "You showed us as we really are—not perfect, but full of life and **spirit**. **Honestly**, I haven't felt this proud of our town in years."

The film went on to **hit** /hɪt/ (v. 打击;击中;达到) several film festivals. Critics praised its authentic portrayal of **developing** communities. One reviewer wrote: "**Presumably**, this **producer** could have made something flashier, but instead she chose truth. The **vast** beauty of ordinary life shines through every frame."

Maya's success didn't come with wealth—independent films rarely made **substantial** money. But she had found something more valuable: a connection to her roots, a clear sense of purpose, and the knowledge that her work mattered. She had learned that sometimes you need to go back to move forward, to remember your **childhood** dreams in order to create **substantial** adult achievements.

She continued to **live** between the city and the **northwest** town, making films that told stories others overlooked. And every **evening**, when she finished work, she felt a deep satisfaction. She had found her **spirit**, her voice, her home.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

制片人坐在城市西北角的小办公室里,透过窗户看着雨。玛雅从事电影工作已经十年了,但成功仍然感觉遥远而广阔。她擦了擦额头上的汗水——办公室很热,空调又坏了。

大概,她想,这又会是艰难的一年。她的最新项目是一部关于小镇发展中社区的电影,需要资金。她向投资者推介了,但他们想要不同的东西。"拜托,"一位投资者说,"做一些人们想看的东西。不要这种缓慢、安静的内容。"

但玛雅相信自己的愿景。这部电影将展现小镇生活的精神,人们相互支持的方式,那些被忽视的频繁善举。她在这样的小镇长大,那些童年回忆仍然塑造着她。

那天傍晚,玛雅接到老朋友詹姆斯的电话,他现在住在他们长大的那个西北小镇。"你应该来看看,"他说。"休息一下。说实话,你听起来很累。"

玛雅考虑了一下。她在城市有先前的承诺,定期参加的会议。但也许詹姆斯是对的。也许她需要重新连接最初激发她工作的地方和感受。

第二周,玛雅坐公交车北上。旅程很长,但她不介意。她看着城市消失,广阔的乡村出现。田野像波浪一样在窗外翻滚而过。她感到内心有什么开始转变。

当她到达时,詹姆斯在车站接她。"欢迎回家,"他笑着说。他们穿过小镇,经过他父亲曾经工作的旧管道厂,经过挂着褪色布条幅的社区中心。一切看起来都比玛雅记忆中小,但也更真实、更坚实。

那天晚上,詹姆斯和他的家人邀请玛雅吃饭。他的妻子安娜做了大量的食物——烤鸡、新鲜面包、来自他们花园的蔬菜。吃饭时,他们谈论小镇的变化。附近开了一个新的有机食品生产农场。年轻人开始回来,看到了慢节奏生活的价值。

"小镇现在不同了,"安娜说。"人们在建设,创造实质性的业务。不像我们小时候,每个人都想离开。"

玛雅倾听着,感到自己内心长期沉睡的部分苏醒了。这就是她想讲述的故事——不仅关于过去,还关于地方如何在保持精神的同时改变。她用新的眼光思考她的电影。

第二天早上,玛雅早起去散步。小镇在柔和的光线中很安静。她经过一位打开面包店的女士,一位推出花车卖花的男士,带着明亮面孔去上学的孩子们。这些频繁的小时刻让生活丰富。

她在小镇的老剧院停下,她小时候在那里看电影。一个标志说当天傍晚的特别放映会降低票价。大概有人在努力让这个地方保持活力。玛雅被这种努力感动了。

回到詹姆斯的家,她坐下来开始写作。想法现在轻松地涌出。她的电影将展示一个学习者回到家乡,重新发现它的价值。它将展示发展中的社区如何在不失去特色的情况下繁荣。它将诚实地面对挑战,但也庆祝坚持的精神。

"你在做什么?"詹姆斯问,给她端来茶。

"我电影的新版本,"玛雅说。"说实话,我想我太努力让它完美了。我忘记了让它真实。"她停顿了一下。"我想购买在这里拍摄一些场景的权利。小镇会允许吗?"

詹姆斯笑了。"我想他们会喜欢的。但你需要在镇议会会议上提出。他们定期开会——下一次是明天傍晚。"

在会议上,玛雅介绍了她的想法。她解释了这部电影将如何展示小镇的巨大潜力,如何能吸引人们对各地小社区的关注。议会成员仔细聆听。一位在那里生活了一辈子的老人发言了。

"在我的童年,"他说,"这个小镇对我来说就是一切。然后我长大了,认为它太小,不够重要。但在退休之前,我环游世界,我学到了一些东西:家不是关于大小。而是关于精神。如果你的电影能展示这一点,你有我的支持。"

其他人点头。他们同意让玛雅拍摄,只要求她诚实地代表他们的社区——包括挣扎和优势。玛雅感到手心出汗,不是因为热,而是因为情感。这是她需要的突破。

接下来的几周,玛雅比以往任何时候都更加努力。她住在詹姆斯的客房,早起晚睡。她采访人们,捕捉日常生活的场景,并定期回去编辑素材。大量的工作需要奉献精神,但她感到项目的精神推动着她前进。

一天,在被改造成艺术家工作室的旧管道厂拍摄时,玛雅注意到一个小女孩在看。女孩穿着一件彩色布料做的连衣裙,手工缝制,独一无二。"你在拍电影吗?"她害羞地问。

"是的,"玛雅说。"你喜欢电影吗?"

"我爱它们,"女孩说。"大概,制作电影很难?"

"是的,"玛雅承认。"但也很美妙。就像学习——你做得越多就越好。你也是学习者吗?"

女孩点点头。"我想做衣服。我妈妈说在创业之前我应该学习,但我已经定期练习了。"

玛雅微笑。这就是她试图捕捉的精神——有梦想的年轻人,尽管不确定仍在努力实现它们。她问能否拍摄女孩缝纫,女孩同意了。那个镜头成为电影中最有力量的部分之一。

几个月后,经过无数小时的编辑,玛雅的电影完成了。她首先在西北小镇的老剧院放映。人们坐满了每个座位。灯亮起时,先是安静,然后是掌声像雷声一样在房间里翻滚。

一位老妇人流着泪走近玛雅。"谢谢你,"她说。"你展示了真实的我们——不完美,但充满生命和精神。说实话,我多年来没有这样为我们的小镇感到骄傲过。"

这部电影继续参加了几个电影节。评论家称赞它对发展中社区的真实描绘。一位评论家写道:"大概这位制片人可以制作更华丽的东西,但她选择了真实。普通生活的巨大美丽在每一帧中闪耀。"

玛雅的成功并没有带来财富——独立电影很少赚大钱。但她找到了更有价值的东西:与根源的连接,清晰的目标感,以及她的工作很重要的认知。她学到了有时你需要回到过去才能前进,记住你的童年梦想才能创造实质性的成人成就。

她继续在城市和西北小镇之间生活,制作讲述被忽视故事的电影。每天傍晚,当她完成工作时,她都感到深深的满足。她找到了自己的精神、声音和家。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "Presumably, she thought, this would be another year of struggle."

**分析:** "presumably" 作为插入语置于句首,表示一种推测或假设。"she thought" 作为插入成分,让读者了解这是人物的内心想法。

**亮点:** 使用副词 "presumably" 和插入语 "she thought" 让句子层次丰富,既表达推测又显示这是主观想法。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "Fields rolled past the window like waves."

**分析:** 使用 "rolled" 这个动词拟人化地描绘田野的移动,然后用 "like waves" 的明喻加强视觉效果。这种写法让静态景物变得动态而优美。

**亮点:** 动词选择和比喻的结合创造了诗意的画面,是优秀叙事写作的典范。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "Maya felt sweat on her palms, not from heat but from emotion."

**分析:** 使用 "not...but..." 结构明确区分两种可能的原因,突出真正的原因是情感而非温度。这种对比让读者更深刻地理解人物的内心状态。

**亮点:** 通过身体反应(出汗)来表达情感,是"show, don't tell"(展示而非告知)写作原则的优秀实践。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "She had found her spirit, her voice, her home."

**分析:** 三个并列的名词短语,前两个都有所有格 "her",形成排比结构。这种递进式的排列从抽象(精神)到具体(声音)再到比喻(家),层层深入。

**亮点:** 排比结构创造节奏感和情感高潮,是结尾段落的有力写法。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: 副词 presumably 表示推测

**原句:** "Presumably, this would be another year of struggle."

**聚焦:** 表示推测的副词用法

**用法说明:** 
"Presumably" 意为"大概、可能",用来表示基于现有信息的合理推测。它比 "probably" 稍正式,常用于书面语。类似的副词还有 apparently, supposedly, allegedly 等。

**类似表达:**
- Probably: 可能(更口语)
- Apparently: 显然;据说
- Supposedly: 据说;据推测
- Evidently: 显然;明显地

**位置:** 通常放在句首(后接逗号)或主语和动词之间。

**可替换句:** "She thought this would probably be another year of struggle."

---

### 聚焦点 2: 过去完成时表示过去之前的状态

**原句:** "She had grown up in such a town, and those memories from her childhood still shaped who she was."

**聚焦:** 过去完成时与一般过去时的配合使用

**用法说明:**
"had grown up" 使用过去完成时,表示在故事时间(过去)之前已经完成的动作。这个时态帮助建立时间层次:童年(更早的过去) → 故事现在(过去) → 叙述时刻(现在)。

**时间线:**
1. 更早的过去: had grown up (童年)
2. 故事的过去时: shaped (那些记忆影响她)
3. 现在: [读者阅读的时刻]

**可替换句:** "She grew up in such a town, and those childhood memories still shaped who she was." (用一般过去时,但时间层次不如原句清晰)

### 6.4 讨论问题 (Discussion)
