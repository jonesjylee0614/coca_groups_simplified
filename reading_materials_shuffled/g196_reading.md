## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Marcus stood in his **bedroom** /ˈbedruːm/ (n. 卧室), looking at blueprints spread across his desk. He wanted to **develop** /dɪˈveləp/ (v. 开发；发展) a device that could help **senior** /ˈsiːniər/ (adj. 年长的；高级的) citizens stay independent. **But** /bʌt/ (conj. 但是) the challenge was enormous. The **tower** /ˈtaʊər/ (n. 塔；高楼) where he lived offered little space for experiments.

His **main** /meɪn/ (adj. 主要的) goal was simple: create a **guide** /ɡaɪd/ (n. 指南；向导；v. 引导) system that could **detect** /dɪˈtekt/ (v. 发现；探测) when someone needed help. He had already secured a small **fund** /fʌnd/ (n. 基金；资金；v. 资助) from a local tech competition. The **administration** /ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃn/ (n. 管理；行政部门) at his university had been supportive, **typically** /ˈtɪpɪkli/ (adv. 通常；一般) offering resources to promising students.

**Another** /əˈnʌðər/ (adj. 另一个；pron. 另一个) challenge was the device's design. Marcus envisioned a small sensor—no bigger than a deck of cards—that could **maintain** /meɪnˈteɪn/ (v. 维持；保养) constant communication through a wireless **port** /pɔːrt/ (n. 港口；端口). The **quality** /ˈkwɑːləti/ (n. 质量；品质) of the signal needed to be perfect.

One evening, around 9 **PM** /ˌpiːˈem/ (n. 下午；晚上), Marcus had a breakthrough. He realized the device could use the same technology as a **runner** /ˈrʌnər/ (n. 跑步者；奔跑的人)'s fitness tracker, monitoring movement patterns. If a **senior** /ˈsiːniər/ (adj. 年长的) person fell, the device would **catch** /kætʃ/ (v. 抓住；捕捉) the unusual motion and send alerts.

He called his grandmother to explain the idea. She lived alone in a different **section** /ˈsekʃn/ (n. 部分；区域) of the city. "That sounds wonderful," she said warmly. "**But** /bʌt/ (conj. 但是) will it know if I'm just sitting down **vs** /ˈvɜːrsəs/ (prep. 对；与...相对) falling?" Marcus laughed. "That's the **exact** /ɪɡˈzækt/ (adj. 精确的；准确的) problem I'm solving, Grandma."

**Depending** /dɪˈpendɪŋ/ (prep. 取决于) on his tests, Marcus hoped to launch a prototype within six months. He felt **embarrassed** /ɪmˈbærəst/ (adj. 尴尬的；窘迫的) asking for help, but his roommate Sam offered to assist. "I'm not an engineer," Sam admitted, "**but** /bʌt/ (conj. 但是) I can help with the app interface."

Sam worked on designing screens that were **helpful** /ˈhelpfəl/ (adj. 有帮助的) and easy to read. The buttons needed to be large enough for those with limited dexterity. They avoided anything that seemed **alien** /ˈeɪliən/ (adj. 外星的；陌生的) or confusing to older users.

During testing, something unexpected happened. A volunteer's **wrist** /rɪst/ (n. 手腕) sensor detected trembling that **typically** /ˈtɪpɪkli/ (adv. 通常) indicated stress. Marcus realized the device could do more than **catch** /kætʃ/ (v. 抓住) falls—it could monitor overall health. This was beyond **flesh** /fleʃ/ (n. 肉；肉体) and bone; it was about **quality** /ˈkwɑːləti/ (n. 质量) of life.

By the end of the year, Marcus's device won recognition. Standing on stage, he wasn't **embarrassed** /ɪmˈbærəst/ (adj. 尴尬的) anymore. His **rational** /ˈræʃənl/ (adj. 理性的；合理的) approach had worked. The distinction between dream and **reality** /riˈæləti/ (n. 现实；真实) had blurred. He had **developed** /dɪˈveləp/ (v. 开发) something **helpful** /ˈhelpfəl/ (adj. 有帮助的), and that was what mattered most.

As he looked at the audience, Marcus realized the **main** /meɪn/ (adj. 主要的) lesson: technology should serve humanity, not the other way around. His grandmother's **wrist** /rɪst/ (n. 手腕) would soon wear his creation, and that thought filled him with purpose.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

马库斯站在卧室里，看着桌上摊开的蓝图。他想开发一种能帮助老年人保持独立的设备。但挑战是巨大的。他住的高楼几乎没有实验空间。

他的主要目标很简单：创建一个能发现有人需要帮助的引导系统。他已经从当地的科技竞赛中获得了一小笔资金。他大学的管理部门一直很支持，通常会向有前途的学生提供资源。

另一个挑战是设备的设计。马库斯设想了一个小传感器——不比一副扑克牌大——可以通过无线端口维持持续通信。信号的质量需要完美。

一天晚上，大约晚上9点，马库斯有了突破。他意识到该设备可以使用与跑步者的健身追踪器相同的技术，监测运动模式。如果老年人跌倒，设备会捕捉到异常动作并发送警报。

他打电话给祖母解释这个想法。她独自住在城市的另一个区域。"听起来很棒，"她温暖地说。"但它能知道我只是坐下而不是跌倒吗？"马库斯笑了。"这正是我要解决的问题，奶奶。"

取决于他的测试，马库斯希望在六个月内推出原型。他觉得请求帮助很尴尬，但他的室友山姆主动提出协助。"我不是工程师，"山姆承认，"但我可以帮助设计应用界面。"

山姆致力于设计有用且易于阅读的屏幕。按钮需要足够大，以便灵活性有限的人使用。他们避免任何对老年用户来说陌生或令人困惑的东西。

在测试过程中，发生了意想不到的事情。一位志愿者的手腕传感器检测到通常表示压力的颤抖。马库斯意识到该设备不仅可以捕捉跌倒——还可以监测整体健康。这超越了血肉之躯；这是关于生活质量。

到年底，马库斯的设备获得了认可。站在舞台上，他不再感到尴尬。他的理性方法奏效了。梦想与现实之间的区别已经模糊。他开发了一些有用的东西，这才是最重要的。

当他看着观众时，马库斯意识到主要的教训：技术应该服务于人类，而不是相反。他祖母的手腕很快就会戴上他的创作，这个想法让他充满了目标感。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "He wanted to develop a device that could help senior citizens stay independent."

**分析:** "want to do"表示意愿。定语从句"that could help..."修饰"device"。"help sb do sth"结构中省略了"to"。"stay"作为连系动词，后接形容词"independent"。

**亮点:** 多层结构（主句+定语从句+help结构）自然流畅，展现了复杂句的实用性。

### 句子 2
**原句:** "The administration at his university had been supportive, typically offering resources to promising students."

**分析:** 过去完成时"had been"表示持续的状态。现在分词短语"typically offering..."作伴随状语，说明supportive的具体表现。

**亮点:** 使用分词短语避免了重复使用连词，使句子更紧凑优雅。

### 句子 3
**原句:** "That sounds wonderful, but will it know if I'm just sitting down vs falling?"

**分析:** 对话中的问句，"if"引导宾语从句。"vs"(versus)用于口语中对比两种情况，使表达更生动。

**亮点:** 在正式的叙述中巧妙插入口语化的"vs"，增加真实感和可读性。

### 句子 4
**原句:** "This was beyond flesh and bone; it was about quality of life."

**分析:** 分号连接两个相关但独立的句子。"beyond"表示超越物质层面。"flesh and bone"是固定搭配，代表物质身体。

**亮点:** 从具体(flesh and bone)到抽象(quality of life)的升华，富有哲理性。

### 句子 5
**原句:** "The distinction between dream and reality had blurred."

**分析:** "distinction between A and B"表示"A和B之间的区别"。"blur"作为动词，表示"变模糊"。过去完成时强调状态的改变。

**亮点:** 用"blur"这个动词生动地描述了梦想实现的过程，比直接说"achieved"更富诗意。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点 1: 分词短语作状语

**原句:** "The administration at his university had been supportive, typically offering resources to promising students."

**聚焦:** 现在分词短语作伴随状语

**用法:** 现在分词短语可以作伴随状语，说明主句动作的方式或伴随情况。这种结构使句子更简洁，避免过多使用连词。

**可替换句:** "The administration at his university had been supportive and typically offered resources to promising students."

**区别:** 使用"and"连接的版本语法简单但略显生硬；分词短语版本更流畅自然。

### 焦点 2: "help + 宾语 + (to) do"结构

**原句:** "He wanted to develop a device that could help senior citizens stay independent."

**聚焦:** "help sb (to) do sth" 中"to"的省略

**用法:** "help"后面的不定式可以省略"to"，在口语和书面语中都很常见。省略"to"使句子更简洁自然。

**可替换句:** "He wanted to develop a device that could help senior citizens to stay independent."

**区别:** 加"to"更正式，但在现代英语中省略更常见。

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **What** was Marcus trying to develop, and who was it designed to help?
2. **How** did Marcus get the idea to use fitness tracker technology?
3. **Why** did Marcus feel embarrassed about asking for help?
4. **What** unexpected discovery did Marcus make during testing?
5. **What if** Marcus had given up when faced with the initial challenges—how would that have affected his grandmother and others?

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. The company wants to ___________ (develop/maintain) a new product line.
2. ___________ (Typically/Exactly), the meeting starts at 9 AM, ___________ (but/and) today it's delayed.
3. The ___________ (quality/section) of this work is excellent; it's very ___________ (helpful/embarrassed).
4. ___________ (Senior/Another) citizens need technology that isn't ___________ (alien/rational) to them.
5. She felt ___________ (embarrassed/exact) when she couldn't ___________ (catch/guide) the ball.

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

Write a short paragraph (80-100 words) about a piece of technology that could improve people's lives. Explain what it does and why it's needed. Try to use at least 5 words from this group's vocabulary list.

**提示词:** develop, helpful, maintain, quality, typically

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion）

> **"Should technology prioritize convenience or focus on solving real human needs? Can it do both?"**

Think about:
- How did Marcus's design philosophy put human needs first?
- What's the difference between technology that's "cool" vs technology that's "helpful"?
- How can we ensure technology remains accessible to all ages and abilities?

**学习建议 (Study Tips):**

1. 掌握分词短语作状语的用法，让句子更简洁优雅
2. 注意"help"后面不定式可以省略"to"
3. 学习使用"beyond"、"blur"等词创造抽象表达
4. 观察如何在叙事中插入对话增加真实感

**词汇记忆技巧 (Memory Tips):**

- **develop** 联想：de-(向下) + velop(包裹) = 打开包裹 = 开发
- **maintain** 联想：main(主要) + tain(保持) = 保持主要状态 = 维持
- **rational** 联想：ratio(比率) + nal = 按比率的 = 理性的
- **embarrassed** 联想：em-(使) + bar(障碍) + rass + ed = 使面临障碍 = 尴尬的
