## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Maya had always been **ordinary** /ˈɔːrdəneri/ (adj. 普通的), or so she thought. She lived **near** /nɪr/ (prep./adv. 靠近) the old theater district where her father worked. Every morning, she would **name** /neɪm/ (v./n. 命名；名字) the things she saw—birds, clouds, strangers—**just** /dʒʌst/ (adv. 仅仅；刚刚) to feel less alone. It had become a **habit** /ˈhæbɪt/ (n. 习惯) after her father's death. People said grief was like a **thread** /θred/ (n. 线；线索) that connected everything, making even simple moments heavy with memory.

One day, Maya received an **offer** /ˈɔːfər/ (n./v. 提议；提供) to audition for a play. "**No** /noʊ/ (det./adv. 不；没有)," she said immediately. The idea made her want to **resist** /rɪˈzɪst/ (v. 抵抗；抵制), to hide. She was **no** **loser** /ˈluːzər/ (n. 失败者), but she wasn't brave either. Standing **naked** /ˈneɪkɪd/ (adj. 赤裸的；无遮蔽的) before an audience, exposing her **voice** /vɔɪs/ (n. 声音) and emotions—it seemed impossible. Yet something pulled at that **thread** of memory. Her father had always said, "Don't **suffer** /ˈsʌfər/ (v. 遭受；忍受) in silence. **Choose** /tʃuːz/ (v. 选择) to speak."

She called her **partner** /ˈpɑːrtnər/ (n. 伙伴；搭档), Alex, who had been on a **mission** /ˈmɪʃn/ (n. 任务；使命) to encourage her for months. "How **much** /mʌtʃ/ (det./adv./pron. 很多；多少) do you **respect** /rɪˈspekt/ (v./n. 尊重) yourself?" Alex asked. "Not as a **number** /ˈnʌmbər/ (n. 数字；号码) or a **figure** /ˈfɪɡjər/ (n./v. 人物；计算) in the background, but as someone with something to say?" The question stung because Maya realized she had spent years trying **not** to be noticed, treating her own life as if it meant **no** more than anyone else's **egg** /eɡ/ (n. 鸡蛋)—fragile and easily broken.

The day of the audition arrived in **no** **hurry** /ˈhɜːri/ (n./v. 匆忙；赶紧), yet somehow too fast. Maya walked into the theater, her heart a **riot** /ˈraɪət/ (n. 暴动；骚乱) of fear and hope. The director, a woman with kind eyes, asked her to read. Maya began, her **voice** shaky at first. But as she continued, something shifted. The words weren't **just** lines on a page—they were a **thread** connecting her to her father's memory, to every moment she'd felt like a **fool** /fuːl/ (n. 傻瓜) for feeling too **much** /mʌtʃ/.

When she finished, there was silence. Then the director spoke: "You have a gift. Don't **resist** it. The world needs voices like yours—**ordinary** people who **choose** to share their truth." Maya felt tears come. She had thought **love** /lʌv/ (n./v. 爱) and art were for others, not for her. But standing there, she understood: **no** one is **just** **ordinary**. We are all **figures** /ˈfɪɡjərz/ in a larger story, and each **voice** matters **just** as **much** /mʌtʃ/ as any other. The **thread** that had once felt like a burden now felt like a connection to something beautiful.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

玛雅一直很普通，或者她这么认为。她住在父亲工作的老剧院区附近。每天早上，她都会给看到的东西命名——鸟儿、云朵、陌生人——只是为了感觉不那么孤单。这在她父亲去世后成了一种习惯。人们说悲伤就像一根线，将一切连接起来，使即使简单的时刻也充满记忆的重量。

有一天，玛雅收到了试演一部戏剧的邀请。"不，"她立即说。这个想法让她想要抵抗，想要躲藏。她不是一个失败者，但她也不勇敢。站在观众面前赤裸，暴露她的声音和情感——这似乎是不可能的。然而，某些东西牵动着那根记忆之线。她父亲总是说："不要默默忍受。选择说话。"

她打电话给她的伙伴亚历克斯，他几个月来一直在执行鼓励她的任务。"你有多尊重自己？"亚历克斯问。"不是作为一个数字或背景中的人物，而是作为有话要说的人？"这个问题很刺痛，因为玛雅意识到她花了多年时间试图不被注意，把自己的生活当作不比别人的鸡蛋更重要——脆弱且容易破碎。

试镜的日子不慌不忙地到来了，但不知怎么又太快了。玛雅走进剧院，她的心是恐惧和希望的骚乱。导演是一位眼神和善的女士，让她朗读。玛雅开始了，她的声音一开始颤抖。但随着她继续，有些东西改变了。这些话不仅仅是纸上的台词——它们是连接她与父亲记忆的线索，连接她每一个因为感觉太多而觉得自己像傻瓜的时刻。

当她完成时，一片寂静。然后导演说话了："你有天赋。不要抵抗它。世界需要像你这样的声音——选择分享真相的普通人。"玛雅感到泪水涌来。她曾认为爱和艺术是别人的，不是她的。但站在那里，她明白了：没有人只是普通人。我们都是更大故事中的人物，每个声音都同样重要。曾经感觉像负担的线索现在感觉像是与美好事物的连接。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "People said grief was like a thread that connected everything, making even simple moments heavy with memory."

**分析：** 这句包含比喻（grief as thread）和现在分词短语（making...）作结果状语。"heavy with memory"使用形容词+介词短语的结构，生动描绘情感重量。

**亮点：** 
- "thread"的比喻贯穿全文，是重要的意象
- "making even simple moments heavy"展示悲伤如何渗透日常
- "heavy with"是固定搭配，表示"充满...的沉重感"

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "How much do you respect yourself? Not as a number or a figure in the background, but as someone with something to say?"

**分析：** 这是一组修辞性问句。第二个问句通过"not...but..."结构澄清第一个问句的真正含义。

**亮点：** 
- "number"和"figure"都暗示非人格化、被量化
- "someone with something to say"简洁有力，强调声音和表达的价值
- 问句形式比陈述更有力，促使Maya反思

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "Maya realized she had spent years trying not to be noticed, treating her own life as if it meant no more than anyone else's egg—fragile and easily broken."

**分析：** "trying not to be noticed"使用否定的不定式。"treating...as if..."引导方式状语从句，表达假设。破折号后补充说明"egg"的含义。

**亮点：** 
- "egg"的比喻独特：不仅表示脆弱，也暗示Maya低估了自己的独特性（每个蛋都是独一无二的）
- "no more than"强调她如何贬低自己的价值
- "fragile and easily broken"呼应她的恐惧和自我保护

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "No one is just ordinary. We are all figures in a larger story, and each voice matters just as much as any other."

**分析：** 这是全文的主题句。三个短句层层递进：否定陈词滥调→提出新视角→强调平等价值。

**亮点：** 
- "just ordinary"的"just"被挑战——没有人"仅仅"是普通的
- "figures in a larger story"提升视角，将个体放入宏大叙事中
- "just as much as"强调平等，呼应开头Alex关于尊重的问题
- 简洁的陈述句比复杂论述更有力量

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦1：Just的多重含义

**原句1：** "She would name the things she saw—just to feel less alone."
**原句2：** "The words weren't just lines on a page."
**原句3：** "Each voice matters just as much as any other."

**聚焦：** Just的三种主要用法

**用法区分：**

1. **Just = only/merely (仅仅，只是)**
   - 例句1："just to feel less alone" = 仅仅为了感觉不那么孤独
   - 位置：通常在不定式前或名词/动词前
   - 可替换：only, merely

2. **Just = not more than (不过是，仅仅是)**
   - 例句2："weren't just lines" = 不仅仅是台词
   - 常用于否定句中
   - 强调"不止于此"

3. **Just = exactly, precisely (恰好，正好)**
   - 例句3："just as much" = 正如...一样多
   - 用于比较句式
   - 强调完全相同的程度

**时间意义的Just：**
- "I **just** arrived." = 刚刚到达
- 强调动作刚完成

**记忆技巧：**
- Just + to do = 仅仅为了
- Not just = 不仅仅
- Just as = 正如
- Just + 过去时 = 刚刚

---

### 聚焦2：动名词的否定形式

**原句：** "She had spent years trying not to be noticed."

**聚焦：** "trying not to do" vs. "not trying to do"

**结构对比：**

1. **Trying not to do something** = 努力不做某事
   - 重点：有意识地避免做某事
   - Example: "I'm trying not to eat sugar." (我正努力不吃糖)
   - 意味着：存在做这件事的诱惑或可能，但在努力避免

2. **Not trying to do something** = 没有努力做某事
   - 重点：不去努力、不去尝试
   - Example: "I'm not trying to offend you." (我不是想冒犯你)
   - 意味着：不是有意为之

**否定的位置很关键：**

| 句子 | 含义 |
|------|------|
| She tried **not to cry**. | 她努力不哭（但可能想哭） |
| She **didn't try to cry**. | 她没有试图哭（这说法通常不合理） |
| He's learning **not to smoke**. | 他在学习戒烟 |
| He's **not learning to smoke**. | 他没在学抽烟 |

**原句深意：**
"trying not to be noticed" 表明Maya：
- 有意识地让自己隐形
- 持续努力避免引起注意
- 这是一个主动但消极的过程

如果是"not trying to be noticed"，则意味着她只是不主动寻求关注，但没有主动隐藏。

## Part 6. 延伸练习 (Practice & Reflection)

### 6.1 理解题 (Comprehension)

1. **Who** is Maya, and what habit has she developed after her father's death?
   - Maya is an ordinary woman who lives near the theater district. She developed the habit of naming things she sees to feel less alone.

2. **Why** does Maya initially resist the offer to audition?
   - She feels it would expose her voice and emotions too nakedly to an audience, and she lacks confidence despite not considering herself a loser.

3. **What** question does Alex ask that makes Maya rethink her self-perception?
   - Alex asks how much Maya respects herself, not as a number or background figure, but as someone with something to say.

4. **What if** Maya had continued to resist and never auditioned?
   - She would likely have continued suffering in silence, never discovering her gift or understanding that her ordinary voice matters.

5. **How** does the metaphor of "thread" evolve throughout the story?
   - It starts as a burden (grief connecting everything heavily), but transforms into a connection to something beautiful (linking her to her father's memory and her authentic voice).

### 6.3 写作任务 (Writing Prompt)

Write a short paragraph (80-100 words) about a time when you discovered something special about yourself that you had overlooked or undervalued. Use at least 5 words from this group's vocabulary list.

**参考示例：**
For much of my life, I thought I was just ordinary—no special voice, no unique figure worth notice. I developed the habit of staying near the background, trying to resist attention. Then my partner encouraged me to choose a different path. I joined a mission to help teach children, and discovered my love for education. The threads of patience and respect I'd always had suddenly mattered. I wasn't a fool for caring; I was someone with something to offer. Each of us has a number of gifts we haven't named yet.

### 6.4 讨论问题 (Discussion)

**Question:** The story concludes that "no one is just ordinary" and "each voice matters just as much as any other." Do you believe this is true? In a world of billions of people, how can every individual voice matter? What makes a voice worth hearing?

**思考提示：**
- Consider the difference between being heard and being valuable
- Think about whether uniqueness comes from inherent qualities or from choices we make
- Reflect on the role of audience—does a voice only matter if someone listens?
- Discuss the relationship between "ordinary" life and extraordinary moments
- Consider whether encouraging everyone to "find their voice" is realistic or idealistic
- Think about Maya's father's advice: "Don't suffer in silence. Choose to speak."—is silence always suffering?
- Reflect on the "thread" metaphor: how do individual voices connect to create larger narratives?
