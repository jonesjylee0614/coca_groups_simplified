## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

On ground **level** /ˈlevl/ (n./adj. 水平;等级;平的), in the basement of an old university building, there was a small translation office **where** /wer/ (adv./conj. 在哪里) Professor Lin worked **every** /ˈevri/ (det. 每个的;所有的) day. Her job was to **translate** /trænsˈleɪt/ (v. 翻译;转化) ancient texts, bridging languages and centuries. The work required not just linguistic skill but an understanding of cultural **meaning** /ˈmiːnɪŋ/ (n. 意义;含义), of how words carry **signal** /ˈsɪɡnəl/ (n./v. 信号;标志;发信号)s beyond their dictionary definitions.

Professor Lin had spent a **lot** /lɑːt/ (n./pron. 许多;一块地) of years perfecting her craft. She would often sit **carefully** /ˈkerfəli/ (adv. 仔细地;小心地) examining manuscripts, trying to capture not just words but the **memory** /ˈmeməri/ (n. 记忆;回忆) and emotion embedded within them. "Translation," she once told her students, "is not about replacing words. It's about preserving **meaning** **across** /əˈkrɔːs/ (prep. 穿过;跨越) language barriers."

One autumn morning, a young graduate student named Emma knocked on her door. Emma had a **project** /ˈprɑːdʒekt/ (n./v. 项目;工程;投射) that required **translating** old letters between a Chinese **employee** /ɪmˈplɔɪiː/ (n. 雇员;员工) and an American **observer** /əbˈzɜːrvər/ (n. 观察者;观测者) from the 1920s. "I need your help," Emma said. "These letters contain so much history, but I can't fully understand the cultural context."

Professor Lin welcomed her in. The office was filled with books at every **level** of the shelves, creating a **bright** /braɪt/ (adj. 明亮的;聪明的) **impression** /ɪmˈpreʃn/ (n. 印象;影响) despite the lack of windows. "Show me what you have," she said. Emma spread the letters across the desk. The handwriting was faded, the paper yellowed, but the **meaning** was still there, waiting to be uncovered.

As they worked, Professor Lin would **prompt** /prɑːmpt/ (v./adj. 促使;迅速的) Emma with questions. "What do you think this phrase means? Not literally—what's the emotional **signal**?" Emma would think **carefully**, trying to feel her way into the minds of people who lived a century ago. The **interval** /ˈɪntərvl/ (n. 间隔;间距) between then and now seemed vast, yet these letters made it feel immediate.

The American **observer**, they discovered, had been documenting life in rural China. His letters home described **neighbor** /ˈneɪbər/ (n. 邻居) relationships, local customs, the way villagers **support** /səˈpɔːrt/ (v./n. 支持;支撑)ed each other during difficult times. The Chinese **employee**, who worked as his **translate**r and guide, added her own perspective, explaining things the American couldn't see.

"Look at this passage," Professor Lin said, pointing to a particularly dense section. "She's trying to explain a concept that has no direct English **alternative** /ɔːlˈtɜːrnətɪv/ (n./adj. 替代品;可供选择的). She describes **how** /haʊ/ (adv./conj. 如何;怎样) the community functions, but she knows he'll interpret it through his own cultural lens. So she's **translating** not just language but worldview."

Emma felt excitement building. This wasn't just academic work—it was detective work, uncovering **meaning** layer by layer. "The **employee** was so **intelligent** /ɪnˈtelɪdʒənt/ (adj. 聪明的;有才智的)," Emma remarked. "She understood both cultures and served as a bridge." Professor Lin nodded. "And she did it without modern tools, without the **quantity** /ˈkwɑːntəti/ (n. 数量;大量) of resources we have today."

As weeks passed, Emma and Professor Lin developed a **friendly** /ˈfrendli/ (adj. 友好的;友善的) working relationship. Emma would arrive each morning with coffee, and they'd **isolate** /ˈaɪsəleɪt/ (v. 隔离;孤立) themselves from the busy campus above, diving into their research. The work was meticulous—**translating** letters **carefully**, checking each word multiple times, discussing possible interpretations.

One day, Emma arrived looking troubled. "I found something **angry** /ˈæŋɡri/ (adj. 生气的;愤怒的)," she said. "Or at least, I think it's anger. But the translator—the Chinese **employee**—never uses direct language. How do I know what she really felt?" Professor Lin smiled gently. "That's your **prompt** to dig deeper. In her culture and time, direct expression of **angry** feelings wasn't always acceptable, especially for women. You must look at word choice, at what she chooses to include or **isolate** from her account."

They examined the passage together. The **employee** described an incident where villagers were mistreated by authorities. Her words were measured, her tone respectful, but underneath, Emma began to sense outrage. "She's **angry**," Emma realized, "but she's **translating** that anger into documentation. She's creating a **memory** for the future—for us."

Professor Lin touched the old paper gently. "Every **translate**d text carries the **translator**'s fingerprints," she said. "This woman wasn't **just** /dʒʌst/ (adv. 仅仅;刚刚) **translating** words. She was **specializing** /ˈspeʃəlaɪz/ (v. 专门研究;专攻) in creating understanding between two very different worlds. That's an art form."

As their **project** neared completion, Emma reflected on what she'd learned. The **interval** between past and present had collapsed. These **translate**d letters had brought **Earth** /ɜːrθ/ (n. 地球;泥土)'s people **across** time to speak to each other. She felt **lucky** /ˈlʌki/ (adj. 幸运的) to have Professor Lin as a guide, **support**ing her through the complex work.

On the final day, they sat together reviewing their completed translation. "We should thank your **neighbor** upstairs for being so **quiet** /ˈkwaɪət/ (adj./n. 安静的;寂静)," Professor Lin joked. "This work requires concentration." Emma laughed. "Actually, I live alone now. No **cow** /kaʊ/ (n. 奶牛;母牛)s mooing outside my window like when I grew up on a farm."

Professor Lin's eyes **bright**ened with interest. "You're from a farm? That must give you a different **impression** of the rural life described in these letters." Emma nodded. "It does. I understand **how** important **neighbor** relationships are in isolated communities. **How** people **support** each other becomes essential for survival."

They talked for hours, sharing **memory**s and perspectives. Professor Lin told stories of growing up during a different era, of learning multiple languages, of becoming an **observer** of cultures. Emma shared her journey from farm life to academia, the cultural **translate**ion she'd performed in her own life.

As Emma prepared to leave, Professor Lin gave her a small gift—a book on translation theory. "Keep working with words and **meaning**," she said. "Keep being that bridge. The world needs people who can **translate** not **just** between languages but between experiences, between times, between cultures."

Walking **up** /ʌp/ (adv./prep. 向上;在...上) from the basement **level** into the **bright** afternoon sun, Emma felt transformed. She'd entered this **project** thinking it was about old letters. She left understanding it was about human connection, about **how** people across **Earth** and across time find ways to **signal** to each other, to **support** each other, to create **meaning** together.

The **translate**d letters would be published, would add to the **quantity** of historical knowledge. But more than that, they'd preserve the **memory** of two people—the American **observer** and the Chinese **employee**—who'd worked to understand each other despite every obstacle. Their **friendly** collaboration, documented in faded ink, was a **signal** that understanding is always possible with **careful** effort and genuine goodwill.

Professor Lin returned to her desk, surrounded by centuries of **translate**d texts. She thought about all the students she'd taught, all the **project**s she'd guided. Each one was an **alternative** to isolation, a way of connecting humanity's scattered voices. She felt neither **angry** nor **sad** /sæd/ (adj. 悲伤的) but deeply satisfied. Her work mattered. Words mattered. **Meaning** mattered.

Outside, the university campus bustled with life. Students rushed between classes, **neighbor**s chatted, birds sang. But down here, at ground **level**, in this quiet basement, Professor Lin continued her essential work: preserving **memory**, creating understanding, **translating** the human experience one **careful** word at a time.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

在底层,在一座大学旧楼的地下室里,有一个小翻译办公室,林教授每天都在那里工作。她的工作是翻译古代文本,连接语言和世纪。这项工作不仅需要语言技能,还需要理解文化意义,理解词语如何承载超越字典定义的信号。

林教授花了很多年完善她的技艺。她经常坐着仔细检查手稿,试图捕捉的不仅是文字,还有嵌入其中的记忆和情感。"翻译,"她曾告诉学生们,"不是替换单词。而是跨越语言障碍保存意义。"

一个秋天的早晨,一位名叫艾玛的年轻研究生敲了她的门。艾玛有一个项目需要翻译1920年代一位中国雇员和一位美国观察者之间的旧信件。"我需要你的帮助,"艾玛说。"这些信件包含了很多历史,但我无法完全理解文化背景。"

林教授欢迎她进来。办公室的每一层架子上都摆满了书,尽管没有窗户,却给人明亮的印象。"给我看看你有什么,"她说。艾玛把信件摊在桌上。笔迹褪色了,纸张发黄了,但意义仍在那里,等待被发现。

在工作时,林教授会用问题促使艾玛思考。"你认为这个短语是什么意思?不是字面上的——情感信号是什么?"艾玛会仔细思考,试图感受一个世纪前人们的想法。那时与现在之间的间隔似乎很大,但这些信件让它感觉很近。

他们发现,美国观察者一直在记录中国农村的生活。他寄回家的信描述了邻居关系、当地习俗、村民们在困难时期如何互相支持。中国雇员作为他的翻译和向导,加上了她自己的视角,解释美国人看不到的东西。

"看这一段,"林教授说,指着一个特别密集的部分。"她试图解释一个没有直接英语替代品的概念。她描述了社区如何运作,但她知道他会通过自己的文化视角来解释。所以她翻译的不仅是语言,还有世界观。"

艾玛感到兴奋。这不仅仅是学术工作——这是侦探工作,一层层揭示意义。"这位雇员如此聪明,"艾玛说。"她理解两种文化,充当桥梁。"林教授点头。"而且她在没有现代工具、没有我们今天拥有的大量资源的情况下做到了这一点。"

几周过去了,艾玛和林教授建立了友好的工作关系。艾玛每天早上都会带着咖啡来,她们会将自己从上面繁忙的校园中隔离出来,投入研究。工作很细致——仔细翻译信件,多次检查每个单词,讨论可能的解释。

一天,艾玛到达时看起来很困扰。"我发现了一些愤怒,"她说。"或者至少,我认为这是愤怒。但翻译——中国雇员——从不使用直接的语言。我怎么知道她真正的感受?"林教授温柔地微笑。"这是你深入挖掘的提示。在她的文化和时代,直接表达愤怒的感情并不总是可以接受的,尤其是对女性。你必须看词语选择,看她选择在她的叙述中包含或隔离什么。"

他们一起检查了这段话。雇员描述了一个村民被当局虐待的事件。她的话是谨慎的,语气是尊重的,但在下面,艾玛开始感到愤怒。"她很生气,"艾玛意识到,"但她把愤怒翻译成了文档。她正在为未来——为我们——创造记忆。"

林教授轻轻地触摸着旧纸。"每一个翻译的文本都带着翻译者的指纹,"她说。"这个女人不仅仅是翻译单词。她专攻在两个非常不同的世界之间创造理解。这是一种艺术形式。"

当他们的项目接近完成时,艾玛反思了她所学到的东西。过去和现在之间的间隔已经消失。这些翻译的信件把地球上的人跨越时间带到一起互相交谈。她觉得很幸运有林教授作为向导,支持她完成复杂的工作。

最后一天,她们一起坐着回顾完成的翻译。"我们应该感谢楼上你的邻居如此安静,"林教授开玩笑说。"这项工作需要专注。"艾玛笑了。"实际上,我现在独自生活。窗外没有奶牛哞哞叫,不像我在农场长大时。"

林教授的眼睛因兴趣而明亮。"你来自农场?那一定让你对这些信件中描述的农村生活有不同的印象。"艾玛点头。"确实如此。我理解邻居关系在孤立社区中有多重要。人们如何互相支持对生存变得至关重要。"

她们聊了几个小时,分享记忆和观点。林教授讲述了在不同时代长大、学习多种语言、成为文化观察者的故事。艾玛分享了她从农场生活到学术界的旅程,她在自己生活中进行的文化翻译。

当艾玛准备离开时,林教授给了她一个小礼物——一本关于翻译理论的书。"继续与词语和意义打交道,"她说。"继续做那座桥梁。世界需要能够不仅在语言之间翻译,而且在经验之间、时代之间、文化之间翻译的人。"

从地下室走上明亮的午后阳光下,艾玛感到变了。她进入这个项目时认为它是关于旧信件的。她离开时理解它是关于人类连接的,关于地球上和跨越时间的人们如何找到方法互相发信号、互相支持、共同创造意义。

翻译的信件将被出版,将增加历史知识的数量。但更重要的是,它们将保存两个人的记忆——美国观察者和中国雇员——他们努力在每个障碍下理解彼此。他们友好的合作,用褪色的墨水记录,是一个信号,表明只要有仔细的努力和真诚的善意,理解总是可能的。

林教授回到她的桌子,被几个世纪的翻译文本包围。她想起了她教过的所有学生,她指导过的所有项目。每一个都是孤立的替代品,是连接人类分散声音的一种方式。她既不生气也不悲伤,而是深感满足。她的工作很重要。词语很重要。意义很重要。

外面,大学校园熙熙攘攘。学生们在课堂间奔跑,邻居们聊天,鸟儿歌唱。但在这里,在底层,在这个安静的地下室里,林教授继续她的重要工作:保存记忆,创造理解,每次一个仔细的词翻译人类经验。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "Translation is not about replacing words. It's about preserving meaning across language barriers."

**分析:** "not about...It's about..." 对比结构明确定义了翻译的本质。"across language barriers" 使用介词短语表达跨越障碍的意象。

**亮点:** 通过简洁的对比,这个句子深刻地重新定义了翻译工作的核心价值。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "She was translating that anger into documentation. She was creating a memory for the future—for us."

**分析:** "translate...into..." 表示"将...转化为..."。破折号后的 "for us" 强调了历史文献对当代的意义。两个句子形成递进关系。

**亮点:** 通过动词"translate"的延伸使用,展示了情感转化为历史记录的过程,富有洞察力。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "Every translated text carries the translator's fingerprints."

**分析:** 使用"fingerprints"(指纹)作为隐喻,表示翻译者的个人印记。简单句,但意象生动,意义深刻。

**亮点:** 一个完美的隐喻,用具体的"指纹"表达抽象的"个人风格和影响",是优秀的修辞。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "The world needs people who can translate not just between languages but between experiences, between times, between cultures."

**分析:** "not just...but..." 结构扩展了翻译的含义。三个 "between" 短语形成排比,逐层递进,强化语气。

**亮点:** 通过优美的排比结构,这个句子将翻译的概念提升到文化桥梁的高度,发人深省。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: "translate...into..." 结构

**原句:** "She was translating that anger into documentation."

**聚焦:** translate 的搭配用法

**用法说明:** 
"translate A into B" 表示"将A转化/翻译成B"。这个搭配不仅用于语言翻译,也可用于抽象概念的转化。注意 "into" 表示结果或目标。

**结构扩展:**
- translate words into action (将话语转化为行动)
- translate ideas into reality (将想法变为现实)
- translate feelings into art (将感情转化为艺术)

**可替换句:** "She was transforming that anger into documentation." (使用 transform,意思相近)

---

### 聚焦点 2: 排比结构的使用

**原句:** "...translate not just between languages but between experiences, between times, between cultures."

**聚焦:** 平行结构的修辞效果

**用法说明:**
排比(parallelism)通过重复相同的语法结构来增强语气和节奏感。这里三个 "between" 短语形成排比,每个短语都是 "between + 复数名词" 的结构。

**排比的作用:**
- 增强说服力和感染力
- 使表达更有节奏感
- 突出核心概念的多个层面

**可替换句:** "...translate among languages, experiences, times, and cultures." (不使用排比,但失去了原句的修辞效果)
