## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Sarah walked up the **stair** /ster/ (n. 楼梯) to her new apartment, her **attention** /əˈtenʃn/ (n. 注意力) fixed on not dropping the boxes. The building was **near** /nɪr/ (prep./adj. 在...附近；近的) the city center, an **elegant** /ˈelɪɡənt/ (adj. 优雅的) old structure with character. Moving **home** /hoʊm/ (n. 家；adv. 回家) felt **very** /ˈveri/ (adv. 非常；很) strange after living with her **mother** /ˈmʌðər/ (n. 母亲) for so long.

Inside, the apartment felt empty. Sarah's **own** /oʊn/ (adj. 自己的；v. 拥有) furniture hadn't arrived yet. She noticed an **envelope** /ˈenvəloʊp/ (n. 信封) slipped under her door—a welcome note from her neighbor. "Come by if you need anything. The **remaining** /rɪˈmeɪnɪŋ/ (adj. 剩余的) neighbors are friendly," it read.

Sarah decided to explore. **Behind** /bɪˈhaɪnd/ (prep. 在...后面) her building, she found a small garden where someone grew vegetables. An old man was working there, his hands rough from years of labor. "You must be the new **journalist** /ˈdʒɜːrnəlɪst/ (n. 记者)," he said with a smile. "Word travels fast in this **household** /ˈhaʊshoʊld/ (n. 家庭；一家人)... I mean, this building."

His name was Tom, and he invited Sarah to sit. "What's your story?" he asked. Sarah explained she had left a **tough** /tʌf/ (adj. 艰难的；坚韧的) job **behind** /bɪˈhaɪnd/ (prep. 在...后面) to chase a **dream** /driːm/ (n. 梦想；v. 做梦) of independent reporting. "**Increasingly** /ɪnˈkriːsɪŋli/ (adv. 越来越；日益), I felt trapped," she said. "Like **cattle** /ˈkætl/ (n. 牛；家畜) in a pen."

Tom laughed. "That's quite a comparison. But I understand. Life in the city can **wound** /wuːnd/ (v. 使受伤；n. 伤口) your spirit if you're not careful." He showed her his plants. "Gardens **vary** /ˈveri/ (v. 变化；不同) in size, but they all need **attention** /əˈtenʃn/ (n. 注意力)."

As they talked, Sarah learned Tom had been a teacher. He used to **preach** /priːtʃ/ (v. 讲道；说教) about the **importance** /ɪmˈpɔːrtns/ (n. 重要性) of curiosity to his students. "There's always **possibility** /ˌpɑːsəˈbɪləti/ (n. 可能性) in the unknown," he said. Sarah felt inspired.

That evening, Sarah received a call. Her **mate** /meɪt/ (n. 伙伴；配偶) from her old **instance** /ˈɪnstəns/ (n. 实例；情况)... no, her old job... wanted to know if she'd made the right choice. Sarah looked at the garden through her window. "I think so," she said. "The **point** /pɔɪnt/ (n. 要点；观点) isn't perfection. It's growth."

Later, Tom knocked on her door with a basket of vegetables. "Welcome **home** /hoʊm/ (n. 家)," he said warmly. Sarah realized this place, though new, already felt less strange. Her **android** /ˈændrɔɪd/ (n. 安卓手机；机器人) phone buzzed—a reminder to write her first independent story. She smiled, feeling ready.

Her editor wanted to know the **pitch** /pɪtʃ/ (n. 推销；音调；v. 投掷). "Community gardens in urban spaces," Sarah typed. "Places where **animal** /ˈænɪml/ (n. 动物) instinct meets human care, where **trouble** /ˈtrʌbl/ (n. 麻烦；困难) fades and connection grows." She thought about Tom's **wound** /wuːnd/ (n. 伤口)-ed hands and kind eyes. The **grade** /ɡreɪd/ (n. 等级；分数) of human kindness could never be measured, but it could transform a day.

Before bed, Sarah looked around her **household** /ˈhaʊshoʊld/ (n. 家庭). The **accident** /ˈæksɪdənt/ (n. 事故；意外) of meeting Tom felt like fate. She had worried about making the move, but now she saw the **possibility** /ˌpɑːsəˈbɪləti/ (n. 可能性) clearly. Sometimes, the most **elegant** /ˈelɪɡənt/ (adj. 优雅的) solutions came from the simplest connections.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

莎拉走上新公寓的楼梯，注意力集中在不掉落箱子上。这栋建筑靠近市中心,是一座优雅的老建筑,很有特色。在与母亲住了这么久之后,搬回家的感觉非常奇怪。

公寓里面感觉很空。莎拉自己的家具还没送到。她注意到门下塞进来一个信封——是邻居的欢迎信。"如果你需要什么就过来。剩下的邻居都很友好,"信上写道。

莎拉决定探索一下。在她的建筑后面,她发现了一个有人种菜的小花园。一位老人正在那里工作,他的手因多年劳作而粗糙。"你一定是新来的记者,"他微笑着说。"在这个家庭...我是说,这栋楼里,消息传得很快。"

他叫汤姆,邀请莎拉坐下。"你的故事是什么?"他问。莎拉解释说,她离开了一份艰难的工作,去追逐独立报道的梦想。"我越来越感到被困住了,"她说。"就像牛圈里的牛一样。"

汤姆笑了。"这个比喻很有意思。但我理解。如果你不小心,城市生活会伤害你的精神。"他向她展示他的植物。"花园的大小各不相同,但它们都需要关注。"

在交谈中,莎拉了解到汤姆曾是一名教师。他过去常向学生说教好奇心的重要性。"未知中总有可能性,"他说。莎拉感到受到启发。

那天晚上,莎拉接到一个电话。她以前工作的伙伴想知道她是否做了正确的选择。莎拉透过窗户看着花园。"我想是的,"她说。"重点不是完美。而是成长。"

后来,汤姆带着一篮子蔬菜敲她的门。"欢迎回家,"他温暖地说。莎拉意识到这个地方虽然新,但已经不那么陌生了。她的安卓手机嗡嗡响——提醒她写第一篇独立报道。她微笑着,感觉准备好了。

她的编辑想知道推销点。"城市空间中的社区花园,"莎拉打字道。"动物本能与人类关怀相遇的地方,麻烦消退,联系增长的地方。"她想起了汤姆受伤的手和善良的眼睛。人类善良的等级永远无法衡量,但它可以改变一天。

睡前,莎拉环顾她的家。遇见汤姆的意外感觉像是命运。她曾担心搬家,但现在她清楚地看到了可能性。有时候,最优雅的解决方案来自最简单的联系。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "Moving home felt very strange after living with her mother for so long."

**分析:** 动名词"moving"作主语。"after"引导时间状语，其后也是动名词"living"。"for so long"强调时间之久。

**亮点:** 两个动名词的使用展现了英语中动名词的灵活性和自然性。

### 句子 2
**原句:** "Increasingly, I felt trapped, like cattle in a pen."

**分析:** 副词"increasingly"置于句首,强调渐进的变化。明喻"like cattle in a pen"生动地表达了被困的感觉。

**亮点:** 将人的感受比作关在围栏里的牛,形象且有冲击力。

### 句子 3
**原句:** "Gardens vary in size, but they all need attention."

**分析:** "vary in..."表示"在...方面有所不同"。转折词"but"连接对比:虽然不同,但有共同需求。

**亮点:** 简单句式蕴含深层含义,暗示了生活的多样性和共性。

### 句子 4
**原句:** "The point isn't perfection. It's growth."

**分析:** 两个简短的判断句形成对比。使用"isn't"和"It's"创造节奏感。第二句省略了主语的重复。

**亮点:** 短句和重复结构强化了哲理性,适合表达人生感悟。

### 句子 5
**原句:** "The accident of meeting Tom felt like fate."

**分析:** "the accident of..."将偶然的相遇名词化。"felt like"表示主观感受。"fate"升华了偶然到必然的哲学思考。

**亮点:** 将"accident"(意外)与"fate"(命运)对比,创造了意义的张力。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点 1: 动名词作主语

**原句:** "Moving home felt very strange after living with her mother for so long."

**聚焦:** 动名词 (V-ing) 作主语

**用法:** 动名词可以作主语,表示一般性的动作或经验。比名词更动态,比不定式更强调经验性。常用于表达感受和评价。

**可替换句:** "To move home felt very strange after she had lived with her mother for so long."

**区别:** 不定式版本更强调未来或特定的行为,动名词版本更强调已发生的经验。

### 焦点 2: 明喻的使用

**原句:** "Increasingly, I felt trapped, like cattle in a pen."

**聚焦:** "like" 引导的明喻

**用法:** "like + 名词"创造明喻,使抽象感受具象化。常放在句尾,作为补充说明。有效的明喻能唤起读者的感官体验。

**可替换句:** "Increasingly, I felt trapped, as if I were cattle in a pen."

**区别:** "as if"引导方式状语从句,更正式;"like"直接比较,更口语化、简洁。

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **Why** did Sarah leave her previous job?
2. **Who** was Tom, and what did he used to do?
3. **What** was Sarah's first independent story idea about?
4. **How** did Tom's philosophy about possibility influence Sarah?
5. **What if** Sarah hadn't explored the building—how might her experience have been different?

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. The building's ___________ (elegant/tough) design impressed everyone.
2. There's a ___________ (possibility/instance) that we'll finish early today.
3. ___________ (Increasingly/Remaining), people are working from ___________ (home/stair).
4. The ___________ (journalist/cattle) interviewed the mayor about the new policy.
5. His ___________ (wound/envelope) took weeks to heal, but he paid close ___________ (attention/grade) to recovery.

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

Write a short paragraph (80-100 words) about a time when you moved to a new place or started something new. How did unexpected connections help you? Try to use at least 5 words from this group's vocabulary list.

**提示词:** home, behind, possibility, attention, tough

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion)

> **"Are chance encounters truly 'accidents,' or do we create our own opportunities through openness and curiosity?"**

Think about:
- How did Sarah's openness to exploring lead to meeting Tom?
- What role does curiosity play in building community?
- Can we prepare ourselves to recognize meaningful "accidents" when they happen?

3. 注意如何用短句创造节奏和强调重点
4. 观察哲理性表达的构建方法

**词汇记忆技巧 (Memory Tips):**

- **elegant** 联想:e-(出) + leg(选择) + ant = 精选出来的 = 优雅的
- **possibility** 联想:possible(可能的) + ity(名词后缀) = 可能性
- **journalist** 联想:journal(日报) + ist(人) = 写日报的人 = 记者
- **increasingly** 联想:increase(增加) + ing + ly = 越来越...地
