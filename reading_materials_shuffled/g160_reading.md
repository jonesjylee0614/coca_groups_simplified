## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Marcus worked at the **academy** /əˈkædəmi/ (n. 学院；研究院) as a **counseling** /ˈkaʊnsəlɪŋ/ (n. 咨询；辅导) director. His job was to **hear** /hɪr/ (v. 听到；听说) students' problems and help them find solutions. One morning, he reviewed an **application** /ˌæplɪˈkeɪʃn/ (n. 申请；应用) from a student named Wei. The **format** /ˈfɔːrmæt/ (n. 格式；版式) was perfect—everything typed on the correct **page** /peɪdʒ/ (n. 页；页面), nothing **aside** /əˈsaɪd/ (adv. 在旁边；另外). But Marcus sensed something **inappropriate** /ˌɪnəˈproʊpriət/ (adj. 不适当的；不合适的) in the **tone** /toʊn/ (n. 语气；音调).

"**Dude** /duːd/ (n. 伙计；家伙)," his colleague said, reading over his shoulder, "this kid needs **more** /mɔːr/ (det./adv. 更多的) than paperwork. Listen to what he's **actually** /ˈæktʃuəli/ (adv. 实际上) saying."

Marcus read the **application** again. Wei had described his **conduct** /ˈkɑːndʌkt/ (n. 行为；品行) as "always correct" and claimed he was "never **ignorant** /ˈɪɡnərənt/ (adj. 无知的；愚昧的)." But the way he wrote suggested **noise** /nɔɪz/ (n. 噪音；干扰) in his mind—confusion hiding behind perfect words. **Apparently** /əˈpærəntli/ (adv. 显然；似乎), Wei believed that appearing perfect would solve his problems.

Marcus decided to **offer** /ˈɔːfər/ (v. 提供；出价) Wei a meeting. When the young man arrived, he sat **actively** /ˈæktɪvli/ (adv. 积极地；活跃地) forward, ready to **impress** /ɪmˈpres/ (v. 使印象深刻). But his hands shook. "**Thank** /θæŋk/ (v. 感谢) you for seeing me," Wei said **softly** /ˈsɔːftli/ (adv. 温柔地；轻声地).

"I want to **hear** your story," Marcus said. "Not what you think I want to **hear**, but what's real. Tell me about your **past** /pæst/ (n./adj. 过去；过去的)."

Wei looked at the floor. "In the **past**, my **uncle** /ˈʌŋkl/ (n. 叔叔；伯伯) always said I should be the best. He kept an **inventory** /ˈɪnvəntɔːri/ (n. 清单；存货) of my mistakes in a **container** /kənˈteɪnər/ (n. 容器；集装箱)—literally, a box where he wrote down everything I did wrong. Every **sound** /saʊnd/ (n. 声音) I made, every wrong answer. He thought this would make me better, but it just made me afraid."

Marcus listened. The room felt **square** /skwer/ (adj./n. 正方形的；广场) and solid, a safe space. "That must have been hard. **Apparently**, you learned to hide your real feelings."

"Yes. I thought if I could just be perfect, I'd earn his approval. But **conducting** /kənˈdʌktɪŋ/ (v. 进行) my life like that—always checking, always worried—it's exhausting. I came to this **academy** hoping to find a different way."

Marcus smiled. "You've **already** /ɔːlˈredi/ (adv. 已经) found it. By coming **here** /hɪr/ (adv. 这里), by being honest. You're not **ignorant**—you're learning. And that takes **more** courage than pretending to know everything."

Wei's eyes filled with tears. "I was worried my **application** would be rejected. The **format** seemed so important. I thought if one **page** was wrong, I'd lose my chance."

"The **format** matters less than the **content** /ˈkɑːntent/ (n. 内容；满足)," Marcus said. "What matters is that you're **actively** working to understand yourself. That's not **inappropriate**—that's **exactly** /ɪɡˈzæktli/ (adv. 确切地) what growth looks like."

As Wei left, Marcus thought about the **fame** /feɪm/ (n. 名声；名望) some counselors sought. They wanted to be known for dramatic rescues. But the real work was quieter—just listening, offering space, letting people find their own strength. The **sound** of Wei's footsteps fading down the hall was the **sound** of someone walking toward healing.

马库斯决定为魏提供一次会面。当这个年轻人到达时，他积极地向前坐着，准备给人留下深刻印象。但他的手在颤抖。"感谢你见我，"魏轻声说。

"我想听你的故事，"马库斯说。"不是你认为我想听的，而是真实的。告诉我你的过去。"

魏看着地板。"在过去，我叔叔总是说我应该是最好的。他把我的错误列在一个容器里——字面意思是，一个盒子，他在里面写下我做错的一切。我发出的每一个声音，每一个错误的答案。他认为这会让我变得更好，但它只让我害怕。"

"是的。我以为如果我能完美，我就能赢得他的认可。但像那样进行我的生活——总是检查，总是担心——太累了。我来这所学院希望找到不同的方式。"

马库斯笑了。"你已经找到了。通过来到这里，通过诚实。你不是无知的——你在学习。这比假装知道一切需要更多的勇气。"

魏的眼睛充满了泪水。"我担心我的申请会被拒绝。格式似乎很重要。我以为如果有一页错了，我就会失去机会。"

"格式不如内容重要，"马库斯说。"重要的是你正在积极地努力理解自己。这不是不适当的——这正是成长的样子。"

当魏离开时，马库斯想起了一些咨询师寻求的名声。他们想因戏剧性的救援而闻名。但真正的工作更安静——只是倾听，提供空间，让人们找到自己的力量。魏的脚步声在走廊里消失的声音是某人走向治愈的声音。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "Listen to what he's actually saying."

**分析:** "what he's actually saying" 是名词性从句，作动词 "listen to" 的宾语。"actually" 作为副词强调真实意图与表面文字的区别。这种"听其言外之意"的表达在咨询和沟通情境中很常见。

**亮点:** 通过 "actually" 的强调，暗示了表面与实质的对比，展现了专业咨询师的洞察力。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "Apparently, Wei believed that appearing perfect would solve his problems."

**分析:** "apparently" 作为句首副词，表示"显然、看起来"，带有推测的语气。"that" 引导宾语从句，说明 Wei 相信的内容。动名词 "appearing perfect" 作从句的主语。

**亮点:** "apparently" 的使用让陈述更客观、更委婉，避免了武断的判断，符合咨询情境中的专业表达。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "I want to hear your story—not what you think I want to hear, but what's real."

**分析:** 破折号引出对 "your story" 的具体解释。"not...but..." 结构对比两种不同的内容。两个 "what" 从句形成平行结构，前者是假设的虚假内容，后者是期望的真实内容。

**亮点:** 这种直接而温和的表达方式，既明确了期待，又给了对方安全感，是优秀咨询技巧的语言体现。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "The format matters less than the content."

**分析:** "matter" 作动词表示"重要"。"less than" 表示比较，说明前者不如后者重要。整句简洁有力地传达了价值判断。

**亮点:** 这种简单的比较句式，通过对比清晰地传达了核心观点，适合用于总结和建议。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: 名词性从句作宾语

**原句:** "I want to hear what's real."

**聚焦:** "what's real" 作宾语

**用法说明:**
"what" 引导的名词性从句在句中作 "hear" 的宾语。"what" 相当于 "the thing that"，既连接从句又在从句中充当主语。这种用法比使用 "the thing that" 更简洁、更地道。

**what 从句的功能:**
- 作主语: What you said is true.
- 作宾语: I heard what you said.
- 作表语: This is what I mean.

**可替换句:** "I want to hear the truth" 或 "I want to hear what is really happening."

---

### 聚焦点 2: 副词 apparently 的使用

**原句:** "Apparently, Wei believed that appearing perfect would solve his problems."

**聚焦:** "apparently" 表示推测和外表

**用法说明:**
"apparently" 意为"显然、看起来"，用于表达基于观察或证据的推测。它通常放在句首（用逗号隔开）或动词前，使陈述更客观、更委婉。类似的副词还有 evidently, seemingly, presumably。

**语气对比:**
- 确定: Wei believed... (直接陈述)
- 推测: Apparently, Wei believed... (基于观察的推测)
- 更委婉: It appears that Wei believed...

**可替换句:** "It seemed that Wei believed appearing perfect would solve his problems."
