## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

"**Um** /ʌm/ (interj. 嗯)," Rachel hesitated at the **committee** /kəˈmɪti/ (n. 委员会) meeting, "I know this seems like a small **issue** /ˈɪʃuː/ (n. 问题;议题), but I can't **deny** /dɪˈnaɪ/ (v. 否认;拒绝) how important it is to our community." She stood before the town council, her hands gripping a folder that contained her **offering** /ˈɔːfərɪŋ/ (n. 提供物;祭品)—a plan to restore the old **ridge** /rɪdʒ/ (n. 山脊;屋脊) trail that connected the **eastern** /ˈiːstərn/ (adj. 东方的) part of town to the **Israeli** /ɪzˈreɪli/ (adj. 以色列的; n. 以色列人) heritage center.

The center had become a **mark** /mɑːrk/ (n. 标志; v. 标记) of **identity** /aɪˈdentəti/ (n. 身份;特性) for many families. Rachel, whose family had lived here for generations, understood every **aspect** /ˈæspekt/ (n. 方面;层面) of why this mattered. The **series** /ˈsɪriːz/ (n. 系列;连续) of events following **Thanksgiving** /ˌθæŋksˈɡɪvɪŋ/ (n. 感恩节) had shown her that the community was divided—not by **terrorism** /ˈterərɪzəm/ (n. 恐怖主义) or violence, but by forgetting their shared history.

"We need to **undertake** /ˌʌndərˈteɪk/ (v. 承担;从事) this project," Rachel continued. "The trail restoration is not just about recreation. It's about the **process** /ˈprɑːses/ (n. 过程; v. 处理) of reconnecting people." The **Republican** /rɪˈpʌblɪkən/ (adj./n. 共和党的;共和党人) mayor, Mr. Thompson, leaned back in his chair, **concerned** /kənˈsɜːrnd/ (adj. 担心的;有关的).

"This **initiative** /ɪˈnɪʃətɪv/ (n. 倡议;主动性) seems **inevitable** /ɪnˈevɪtəbl/ (adj. 不可避免的)," he said, "but the cost is significant. And frankly, some see it as a waste when we have more pressing matters—like the **pipeline** /ˈpaɪplaɪn/ (n. 管道;输油管) repairs after last winter."

Rachel took a breath. "I understand your **attempt** /əˈtempt/ (n./v. 尝试;企图) to prioritize, Mr. Mayor. But consider this: when water doesn't **boil** /bɔɪl/ (v. 沸腾;煮), you don't abandon the pot. You adjust the heat. This project will **totally** /ˈtoʊtəli/ (adv. 完全地;彻底地) change how our **eastern** neighborhoods feel connected."

The committee members exchanged glances. One woman, wearing a simple white **cap** /kæp/ (n. 帽子;盖子), spoke up: "My children walk that **ridge** every day to school. The path is dangerous now. This isn't just about history—it's about safety."

Rachel felt hope rising. The mayor nodded slowly. "Very well. We'll **issue** /ˈɪʃuː/ (v. 发布;颁发) a statement supporting this **initiative** tomorrow. But Rachel, you'll need to lead this. Can you **undertake** that responsibility?"

"Yes," Rachel said without hesitation. "**Um**, actually, I've already started gathering volunteers. We're ready to begin the **process** immediately after approval."

As she left the building, Rachel looked toward the **eastern** sky. The old **ridge** trail would soon be restored, and with it, a piece of their shared **identity**. Sometimes change comes not from grand gestures but from one person **concerned** enough to speak up.

当她离开大楼时,瑞秋望向东方的天空。古老的山脊小径很快就会被修复,随之而来的,还有他们共同身份的一部分。有时候改变不是来自宏大的姿态,而是来自一个足够关心而愿意站出来说话的人。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "I know this seems like a small issue, but I can't deny how important it is to our community."

**分析:** "seems like" 表示"看起来像","can't deny" 是双重否定强调肯定。"how important it is" 是感叹句式作宾语从句,强调程度。

**亮点:** 通过"看似...但实际..."的对比结构,强化了说话者的观点,是说服性表达的有效方式。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "The series of events following Thanksgiving had shown her that the community was divided—not by terrorism or violence, but by forgetting their shared history."

**分析:** "following Thanksgiving" 是现在分词短语作后置定语。破折号后用 "not...but..." 结构明确指出分裂的真正原因。

**亮点:** 破折号的使用制造了戏剧性的停顿,引出意想不到但更深刻的真相。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "When water doesn't boil, you don't abandon the pot. You adjust the heat."

**分析:** 这是一个生动的类比(analogy)。用日常生活中煮水的经验来比喻解决问题的态度。短句的使用使道理更清晰有力。

**亮点:** 具体生活化的比喻让抽象道理变得易懂,是优秀演讲和说服性写作的常用技巧。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "Sometimes change comes not from grand gestures but from one person concerned enough to speak up."

**分析:** "not...but..." 对比结构,"concerned enough to..." 表示"足够...以至于..."。这是一个哲理性总结句。

**亮点:** 简洁有力的对比和总结,给读者留下深刻印象,适合作为文章结尾。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: "not...but..." 对比结构

**原句:** "The community was divided—not by terrorism or violence, but by forgetting their shared history."

**聚焦:** "not...but..." 表示"不是...而是..."

**用法说明:** 
这个结构用于否定前者、肯定后者,强调真正的原因、方式或对象。在说服性文本和论述中非常常用,能有效引导读者思维。

**结构分析:**
- not by A (否定的原因)
- but by B (真正的原因)

**类似表达:**
- "not A but B" (不是A而是B)
- "B rather than A" (是B而不是A)

**可替换句:** "The community was divided by forgetting their shared history, not by terrorism or violence." (意思相同,但强调效果较弱)

---

### 聚焦点 2: 现在分词作后置定语

**原句:** "The series of events following Thanksgiving had shown her..."

**聚焦:** "following Thanksgiving" 作后置定语修饰 "events"

**用法说明:**
现在分词短语可以作后置定语,相当于一个定语从句。这里 "following" = "that followed",但分词形式更简洁。

**对比:**
- 分词: "events following Thanksgiving" (简洁)
- 定语从句: "events that followed Thanksgiving" (完整但略显冗长)

**可替换句:** "The series of events that took place after Thanksgiving had shown her..." (更口语化)
