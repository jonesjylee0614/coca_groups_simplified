## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Mrs. Chen was a **smart** /smɑːrt/ (adj. 聪明的) teacher who loved **Halloween** /ˌhæloʊˈiːn/ (n. 万圣节). Every year, she tried to **facilitate** /fəˈsɪlɪteɪt/ (v. 促进;使便利) a memorable party for her students. This year's **upcoming** /ˈʌpkʌmɪŋ/ (adj. 即将到来的) celebration would be special—she planned to teach science through fun.

On the morning of October 31st, students rushed through the **hallway** /ˈhɔːlweɪ/ (n. 走廊), excited to **attend** /əˈtend/ (v. 参加) the party. The classroom was decorated with fake spider webs and **glass** /ɡlæs/ (n. 玻璃) jars filled with red liquid that looked like **blood** /blʌd/ (n. 血液) but was actually juice.

"Today, we'll learn about an interesting **phenomenon** /fəˈnɑːmənɑːn/ (n. 现象)," Mrs. Chen announced. "Does anyone know what **carbon** /ˈkɑːrbən/ (n. 碳) dioxide does?" A few hands went up. She smiled. Her **aim** /eɪm/ (n. 目标) was to make learning fun, not scary.

"Let me **count** /kaʊnt/ (v. 数;计算) the volunteers," she said, looking around. "One, two, three... great!" She handed each student a balloon. "When you breathe out, you create carbon dioxide. Watch what happens when we collect it in these balloons."

The experiment was **popular** /ˈpɑːpjələr/ (adj. 受欢迎的). Kids laughed as they filled balloons with their breath. "This is cool!" shouted Mark, whose **sibling** /ˈsɪblɪŋ/ (n. 兄弟姐妹) was in the class too. The twins loved science, though they didn't always agree—**either** /ˈiːðər/ (adv. 也不;或者) one could be stubborn at times.

Mrs. Chen then showed them a video about **mortality** /mɔːrˈtæləti/ (n. 死亡率) rates during historical plagues. It tied to Halloween's history. "People used to believe that wearing masks could protect them at the **border** /ˈbɔːrdər/ (n. 边界) between life and death," she explained. The students listened, fascinated.

"Can we **observe** /əbˈzɜːrv/ (v. 观察) more experiments?" asked Emma. Mrs. Chen nodded. She pulled out dry ice from a cooler, creating fog that filled the room. "This looks like a haunted **house** /haʊs/ (n. 房子)!" someone exclaimed.

Later, Mrs. Chen **surprised** /sərˈpraɪzd/ (v. 使惊讶) everyone with another lesson. She talked about how some people help the **homeless** /ˈhoʊmləs/ (adj. 无家可归的) during cold months, and how small acts of kindness **count** /kaʊnt/ (v. 重要). "Science isn't just experiments," she said. "It's about understanding the world to make it better."

As the party ended, she showed them a **glass** /ɡlæs/ beaker with layers of colored liquids. "This is density at work," she explained. "Just like in life, different things can exist together without mixing if we respect their differences." The students nodded thoughtfully.

The school principal, an old woman about to **retire** /rɪˈtaɪr/ (v. 退休), stopped by. "Your class is amazing," she told Mrs. Chen. "You **facilitate** /fəˈsɪlɪteɪt/ learning in ways I've never seen. No wonder you're so **popular** /ˈpɑːpjələr/."

That evening, Mrs. Chen reflected on the day. She had taken a small **venture** /ˈventʃər/ (n. 冒险;企业) in mixing education with celebration. It had worked. She didn't have much **finance** /ˈfaɪnæns/ (n. 财政;金融) or fancy equipment, but she had creativity. Sometimes, she thought, the best lessons come from seeing the world with fresh eyes—even if it's through Halloween decorations.

Outside, leaves fell near her **house** /haʊs/, marking the change of seasons. She smiled, already planning next year's **surprise** /sərˈpraɪz/ (n. 惊喜). Teaching, she decided, was its own kind of magic—a **bit** /bɪt/ (n. 一点) of science, a **bit** of heart, and endless wonder.

派对结束时,她给他们看了一个装有彩色液体层的玻璃烧杯。"这是密度在起作用,"她解释道。"就像在生活中,如果我们尊重彼此的差异,不同的事物可以在一起存在而不混合。"学生们若有所思地点头。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "Every year, she tried to facilitate a memorable party for her students."

**分析:** 这句使用了不定式 "to facilitate" 作宾语,表示尝试做的具体内容。"Facilitate" 比简单的 "make" 或 "organize" 更正式,强调促进和使事情变得容易的过程。形容词 "memorable" 前置修饰 party,说明活动的品质。

**亮点:** 使用更高级的动词 facilitate 而非简单动词,体现了教师的专业性和用心。

---

### 句子 2
**原句:** "Her aim was to make learning fun, not scary."

**分析:** 这是一个主系表结构,表语由不定式短语 "to make learning fun" 充当。"Not scary" 通过否定对比,强调了教学目标的积极性。"Make + 宾语 + 形容词"是常用句型,表示"使...变得..."。

**亮点:** 通过肯定+否定的对比结构,清晰表达了教学理念,简洁有力。

---

### 句子 3
**原句:** "Just like in life, different things can exist together without mixing if we respect their differences."

**分析:** 这句以类比开头 "Just like in life",将科学实验与人生哲理联系起来。主句使用情态动词 "can",表示可能性。条件状语从句 "if we respect..." 说明了和谐共存的条件。

**亮点:** 从具体的科学现象升华到人生哲理,体现了深度教学——不仅传授知识,更启发思考。

---

### 句子 4
**原句:** "Teaching, she decided, was its own kind of magic—a bit of science, a bit of heart, and endless wonder."

**分析:** 插入语 "she decided" 放在主语和谓语之间,增加了叙述的亲切感。破折号后的内容解释了 "magic" 的具体构成,使用三个并列成分,最后一个 "endless wonder" 没有 "a bit of",形成递进效果。

**亮点:** 通过诗意的语言和排比结构,总结了教学的本质,既有理性又有感性,令人印象深刻。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦点 1: 不定式作宾语

**原句:** "Every year, she tried to facilitate a memorable party for her students."

**聚焦:** To facilitate 不定式作宾语

**用法说明:**
某些动词后面必须接不定式(to + 动词原形)作宾语,"try" 就是其中之一。"Try to do" 表示"尝试做某事",强调努力的过程。常见的后接不定式的动词还有: want, hope, decide, plan, learn, refuse 等。

**对比:**
- Try to do: 尝试做(强调努力)
- Try doing: 尝试(用某种方法)(强调方法)

**可替换句:** "Every year, she attempted to create a memorable party for her students." (使用同义动词 attempt)

---

### 聚焦点 2: 条件状语从句

**原句:** "Different things can exist together without mixing if we respect their differences."

**聚焦:** If 引导的条件状语从句

**用法说明:**
If 引导的条件状语从句表示"如果...就..."的条件关系。在主句中使用情态动词 "can" 表示在某种条件下的可能性。这种句型常用于表达因果关系和提出建议。注意条件从句可以放在主句前或后,位置灵活。

**语序对比:**
- 从句后置: Different things can exist together if we respect their differences.
- 从句前置: If we respect their differences, different things can exist together.

**可替换句:** "When we respect their differences, different things can exist together without mixing." (使用 when 表示时间/条件)
