## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

The **tropical** /ˈtrɑːpɪkl/ (adj. 热带的) rain fell softly on the small **market** /ˈmɑːrkɪt/ (n. 市场) **base** /beɪs/ (n. 基地；基础) where Rosa had been **working** /ˈwɜːrkɪŋ/ (v./adj. 工作；运转的) for three years. She had come here seeking **change** /tʃeɪndʒ/ (n./v. 改变), but life had settled into something **calm** /kɑːm/ (adj. 平静的). Each **monthly** purchase at the little shops felt the same. Yet Rosa was **convinced** /kənˈvɪnst/ (adj. 确信的) something was about to shift.

"The **good** /ɡʊd/ (adj. 好的) news is," said Marco, the shop owner, "we're going to **change** how we sell things." He explained that a new **settlement** /ˈsetlmənt/ (n. 定居点；解决) of farmers **off** /ɔːf/ (prep./adv. 离开) the main road wanted to bring fresh food directly to the **market**. Rosa's **flexibility** /ˌfleksəˈbɪləti/ (n. 灵活性) would be important. Could she help organize it?

Rosa felt her **limitations** /ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃnz/ (n. 限制) keenly. She was not a **receiver** /rɪˈsiːvər/ (n. 接收者；听筒) of grand ideas, just someone **working** hard each day. But something in Marco's words sparked hope. "I'm **perfectly** /ˈpɜːrfɪktli/ (adv. 完美地) able to try," she said, though her voice was **calm** and measured.

The next week felt like **lightning** /ˈlaɪtnɪŋ/ (n. 闪电) had struck. Change was **likely** /ˈlaɪkli/ (adj./adv. 可能的) coming faster than anyone expected. The farmers arrived with vegetables Rosa had never seen before, and **currently** /ˈkɜːrəntli/ (adv. 目前) the **market** buzzed with energy. Children ran between stalls, and an old man played a **cartoon** /kɑːrˈtuːn/ (n. 卡通；漫画) on his phone for anyone who would watch. The **depth** /depθ/ (n. 深度) of community feeling surprised everyone.

Yet not everyone was happy. One vendor, still bitter from past wrongs, spoke of **revenge** /rɪˈvendʒ/ (n./v. 复仇). "Why should we **beg** /beɡ/ (v. 乞求) for **personnel** /ˌpɜːrsəˈnel/ (n. 全体人员) to support this?" he demanded. Rosa listened **perfectly** still, then spoke. "This isn't about **revenge** or proving we're better. It's about building something **good** together." Her words, simple but **literally** /ˈlɪtərəli/ (adv. 字面上；确实地) true, shifted the mood.

By the end of the month, the new system was **working** **perfectly**. Rosa realized that **change** required **flexibility**, yes, but also the courage to **beg** for nothing and give everything. The **tropical** rain still fell, but now it felt like a **kiss** /kɪs/ (n./v. 亲吻) from the sky, washing away old **limitations** and bringing new life. The **market** was **convinced** it could thrive, and Rosa had learned that even in the **depth** of routine, transformation is always **likely**.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

热带雨轻柔地落在小市场基地上，罗莎已在这里工作了三年。她来这里寻求改变，但生活已经安定下来，变得平静。在小商店的每月采购都感觉一样。然而，罗莎确信有什么即将转变。

"好消息是，"店主马可说，"我们要改变销售方式。"他解释说，一个离开主干道的农民新定居点想要直接向市场提供新鲜食物。罗莎的灵活性将很重要。她能帮忙组织吗？

罗莎敏锐地感受到自己的局限。她不是宏大想法的接收者，只是每天努力工作的人。但马可话中的某些东西点燃了希望。"我完全有能力尝试，"她说，尽管她的声音平静而有分寸。

接下来的一周就像被闪电击中了一样。变化可能比任何人预期的都要快。农民们带来了罗莎从未见过的蔬菜，目前市场充满了活力。孩子们在摊位间奔跑，一位老人在手机上为愿意看的人播放卡通。社区情感的深度让每个人都感到惊讶。

然而，并非每个人都高兴。一位摊贩因过去的冤屈仍心存怨恨，谈到了复仇。"我们为什么要乞求人员支持这个？"他质问道。罗莎完全静止地听着，然后开口说话。"这不是关于复仇或证明我们更好。这是关于一起建立一些好的东西。"她的话语简单但确实真实，改变了气氛。

到月底，新系统运转得很完美。罗莎意识到，改变需要灵活性，是的，但也需要勇气去不乞求任何东西而付出一切。热带雨仍在下，但现在感觉像来自天空的亲吻，洗去旧的限制，带来新生命。市场确信它能繁荣，罗莎明白了，即使在日常生活的深处，转变总是可能的。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "She had come here seeking change, but life had settled into something calm."

**分析：** "had come...seeking"使用了过去完成时+现在分词的结构，"seeking"表示伴随的目的。"settle into"是固定短语，意为"安定下来，习惯于"。

**亮点：** "something calm"用不定代词"something"+形容词的结构，给"calm"增添了一种模糊、难以名状的质感，比直接说"a calm life"更有文学性。

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "Rosa felt her limitations keenly."

**分析：** 这是一个简洁而有力的句子。"feel + 名词 + 副词"结构中，副词"keenly"（敏锐地、强烈地）修饰动词"felt"，强调感受的强度。

**亮点：** "keenly"这个词选择精妙——它既表示"清楚地意识到"，又暗含"痛苦地感受到"，一词双关，体现了Rosa内心的矛盾。

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "Her words, simple but literally true, shifted the mood."

**分析：** 这句使用了同位语结构。"simple but literally true"作为插入语，对"her words"进行补充说明。"shift the mood"是常用搭配，表示"改变气氛"。

**亮点：** "simple but literally true"的对比很有力量——暗示有时候最简单的真理反而最有说服力。"literally"在此强调"真正地、确确实实地"，不是夸张。

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "The tropical rain still fell, but now it felt like a kiss from the sky, washing away old limitations and bringing new life."

**分析：** 这是全文的升华句。主句后接"but"转折，再用"felt like"引出比喻，最后两个现在分词短语（washing, bringing）作伴随状语，层层递进。

**亮点：** 
- 比喻："rain"比作"kiss"，将自然现象人格化，赋予温柔的情感
- 对比："washing away old"与"bringing new"形成对称，呼应全文"change"主题
- 节奏：句子从简单到复杂，从具体到抽象，营造出诗意的结尾

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦1：过去完成时表示更早的过去

**原句：** "She had come here seeking change, but life had settled into something calm."

**聚焦：** 过去完成时（had + p.p.）表示在故事主时间线之前发生的事情

**时间关系：**
- 罗莎来到这里（had come）→ 更早的过去
- 生活安定下来（had settled）→ 早于故事开始的过去
- 故事现在（Rosa was convinced）→ 过去时

**用法：** 过去完成时用于回溯更早的背景信息，为当前故事情境提供铺垫。这里说明Rosa到来和生活安定都是在故事主要事件之前就完成的状态。

**可替换句：** "She came here seeking change, but life settled into something calm."（一般过去时，失去时间层次感）

---

### 聚焦2：现在分词作结果状语

**原句：** "The tropical rain still fell, but now it felt like a kiss from the sky, washing away old limitations and bringing new life."

**聚焦：** 现在分词短语（washing...and bringing...）作结果状语

**用法：** 
- 现在分词短语可以表示主句动作带来的自然结果或伴随情况
- 这里"washing away"和"bringing"不是目的，而是"felt like a kiss"这个感受所带来的效果
- 两个分词用"and"并列，形成对称结构

**特点：**
- 主语一致：washing和bringing的逻辑主语都是"rain"
- 时间同步：与主句动作同时发生
- 因果关系：雨像亲吻 → 洗去限制、带来新生

**可替换句：** "The tropical rain still fell like a kiss from the sky. It washed away old limitations and brought new life."（分成两句，但失去了流畅性和一气呵成的诗意）

## Part 6. 延伸练习 (Practice & Reflection)

### 6.1 理解题 (Comprehension)

1. **Who** is Rosa, and why did she come to the tropical market base?
   - Rosa is someone who came seeking change but had settled into a calm, routine life working at the market for three years.

2. **Why** was Rosa's flexibility important for the new market plan?
   - The new settlement of farmers wanted to sell directly at the market, and organizing this required someone flexible who could adapt to change.

3. **What** made one vendor angry about the changes?
   - The vendor was bitter from past wrongs and saw the changes as something they shouldn't have to "beg" for support about.

4. **What if** Rosa had not spoken up to address the vendor's concerns?
   - The negative mood might have spread, potentially undermining the new market system before it could succeed.

5. **How** does Rosa's perspective on change evolve throughout the story?
   - She starts feeling limited and uncertain, but grows to understand that change requires both flexibility and the courage to give without expecting anything in return.

   - **Answer:** likely

   - **Answer:** personnel

   - **Answer:** flexibility

   - **Answer:** convinced

   - **Answer:** currently

### 6.3 写作任务 (Writing Prompt)

Write a short paragraph (80-100 words) about a time when your community came together to create positive change. Use at least 5 words from this group's vocabulary list.

**参考示例：**
Our neighborhood was calm until someone suggested creating a community garden. Currently, the empty lot was just gathering trash, but change seemed unlikely. We had to beg the city for permission, which tested our flexibility. When lightning storms destroyed our first plants, we stayed convinced in our mission. The depth of our commitment surprised even ourselves. Now the market sells our vegetables, and literally everyone feels proud. What started as a simple purchase of seeds became something perfectly good for all of us.

### 6.4 讨论问题 (Discussion)

**Question:** Rosa says, "This isn't about revenge or proving we're better. It's about building something good together." In your experience, what helps communities move beyond past conflicts to work together? What barriers make this difficult?

**思考提示：**
- Think about the difference between competition and collaboration
- Consider how past hurts can limit future possibilities
- Reflect on what "building something good together" requires from individuals
- Discuss the role of leadership (like Rosa's calm words) in shifting group dynamics
- Consider whether flexibility means compromising values or finding creative solutions
