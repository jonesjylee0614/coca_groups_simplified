## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

In a small village where **nature** /ˈneɪtʃər/ (n. 自然) met **standard** /ˈstændərd/ (n./adj. 标准) city life, Marcus ran a simple restaurant. His **base** /beɪs/ (n. 基地；基础) menu included **potato** /pəˈteɪtoʊ/ (n. 土豆) dishes and fresh bread—nothing fancy, but people came for the honest food. "This place is **pretty** /ˈprɪti/ (adj./adv. 漂亮的；相当) **good** /ɡʊd/ (adj. 好的)," visitors would say. Marcus believed in doing things the **right** /raɪt/ (adj./adv. 正确的) way, even when it cost more.

One day, the **server** /ˈsɜːrvər/ (n. 服务员；服务器) who worked nights disappeared. Money was missing, and witnesses said they saw someone **break** /breɪk/ (v. 打破；破坏) into the safe. The **penalty** /ˈpenəlti/ (n. 惩罚；罚金) for theft was severe, and Marcus faced a choice: call the police or try to understand what happened. His mother had always taught him to **raise** /reɪz/ (v. 举起；提高) questions before raising **accusations** /ˌækjuˈzeɪʃnz/ (n. 指控).

Marcus discovered the **server** had a family emergency. Medical bills had formed a **chain** /tʃeɪn/ (n. 链条；连锁) of **debt** /det/ (n. 债务) that seemed impossible to **break**. The young man hadn't meant to **trick** /trɪk/ (n./v. 诡计；欺骗) anyone—he planned to **permit** /pərˈmɪt/ (v./n. 允许；许可证) himself just one loan and return it. But **nature** has its own logic: one wrong choice **differs** /ˈdɪfərz/ (v. 不同) from a **career** /kəˈrɪr/ (n. 职业) of crime, yet **all** /ɔːl/ (det./pron. 所有的) wrong choices create consequences.

Marcus faced his own dilemma. He had worked hard his whole life for **profit** /ˈprɑːfɪt/ (n./v. 利润；获益). Should he **decrease** /dɪˈkriːs/ (v./n. 减少) his **standard** of ethics to protect his business? Or should he **praise** /preɪz/ (v./n. 赞扬) the **better** /ˈbetər/ (adj./adv. 更好的) **nature** in people and offer another chance? A **speech** /spiːtʃ/ (n. 演讲) he heard once came to mind: "True **utility** /juːˈtɪləti/ (n. 实用；公用事业) comes from building people up, not tearing them down."

In the end, Marcus made an **object** /ˈɑːbdʒekt/ (n./v. 物体；反对) lesson of the situation. He called the **server** back and said, "I will **permit** you to work **off** /ɔːf/ (prep./adv. 离开；关闭) your **debt** here. But you must promise: **no** /noʊ/ (det. 没有) more **tricks**, no lies. Every **ounce** /aʊns/ (n. 盎司；少量) of **profit** you make through honest work will **raise** your dignity." The young man, tears in his eyes, agreed.

Months passed. The **server** worked harder than anyone Marcus had ever seen. He learned to **cook** /kʊk/ (v. 烹饪), to manage, to lead. People noticed his transformation and offered **praise**. Eventually, he saved enough to **break** free from **debt** and start fresh. Marcus learned something too: **wealthy** /ˈwelθi/ (adj. 富有的) people measure **profit** in money, but true wealth comes from giving second chances. The **chain** of kindness **differs** from the **chain** of punishment—one builds, the other destroys. In the end, **all** choices create **chains**. We must choose which **chain** to forge.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

在一个自然与标准城市生活交汇的小村庄里，马库斯经营着一家简单的餐厅。他的基础菜单包括土豆菜肴和新鲜面包——没什么花哨的，但人们因为诚实的食物而来。"这地方相当好，"游客们会说。马库斯相信用正确的方式做事，即使成本更高。

有一天，晚上工作的服务员失踪了。钱不见了，目击者说他们看到有人闯入保险箱。盗窃的惩罚很严厉，马库斯面临一个选择：报警还是试图理解发生了什么。他的母亲总是教导他，在提出指控之前要先提出问题。

马库斯发现服务员有家庭紧急情况。医疗账单形成了一条看似不可能打破的债务链条。这个年轻人并不想欺骗任何人——他计划只允许自己借一次钱然后归还。但自然有自己的逻辑：一个错误的选择不同于犯罪生涯，但所有错误的选择都会产生后果。

马库斯面临自己的困境。他一生都在努力工作赚取利润。他应该降低自己的道德标准来保护生意吗？还是应该赞扬人们更好的本性，给予另一次机会？他想起曾经听过的一次演讲："真正的效用来自建设人们，而不是摧毁他们。"

最后，马库斯把这种情况作为一个实物教训。他叫回了服务员，说："我允许你在这里通过工作偿还你的债务。但你必须承诺：不再有诡计，不再有谎言。你通过诚实工作赚取的每一盎司利润都会提升你的尊严。"年轻人眼含泪水，同意了。

几个月过去了。服务员比马库斯见过的任何人都更努力地工作。他学会了烹饪、管理、领导。人们注意到他的转变并给予赞扬。最终，他攒够了钱摆脱债务，重新开始。马库斯也学到了一些东西：富有的人用金钱衡量利润，但真正的财富来自给予第二次机会。善意之链不同于惩罚之链——一个建设，另一个摧毁。最终，所有选择都会创造链条。我们必须选择锻造哪一条链条。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子1
**原句：** "His mother had always taught him to raise questions before raising accusations."

**分析：** 这句使用平行结构"raise questions...raising accusations"，通过重复动词"raise"但搭配不同宾语，创造对比效果。"before"引导时间关系，强调顺序的重要性。

**亮点：** 
- 平行结构增强节奏感和记忆性
- "raise questions" vs. "raise accusations"揭示了智慧的处理方式：先理解，后判断
- 过去完成时"had taught"表明这是长期的教导，深深影响了Marcus的价值观

---

### 句子2
**原句：** "One wrong choice differs from a career of crime, yet all wrong choices create consequences."

**分析：** 这是一个包含转折的复合句。"differ from"表示"不同于"，"yet"引出转折，"all wrong choices"是泛指。

**亮点：** 
- 哲理性强：区分了一次错误与持续犯罪，但又指出所有错误都有后果
- "yet"连接两个看似矛盾的观点，展现思考的深度
- 为Marcus后续的决定提供了道德基础：人会犯错，但不意味着他们就是罪犯

---

### 句子3
**原句：** "Every ounce of profit you make through honest work will raise your dignity."

**分析：** "every ounce of"强调"每一点"，"through honest work"是方式状语，说明profit的来源。"raise your dignity"将抽象概念具体化。

**亮点：** 
- "ounce"（盎司）通常用于重量，这里比喻微小的数量，强调积少成多
- "through honest work"与前文的"trick"形成对比
- "raise dignity"巧妙呼应前文的"raise questions/accusations"，现在raise的是尊严

---

### 句子4
**原句：** "The chain of kindness differs from the chain of punishment—one builds, the other destroys."

**分析：** 这是全文的核心比喻句。两个"chain"形成平行对比，破折号后的补充说明用最简洁的语言揭示本质区别。

**亮点：** 
- "chain"的双重比喻：债务之链、因果之链
- 平行结构："chain of kindness" vs. "chain of punishment"
- "one builds, the other destroys"极简对比，力量强大
- 结构对称：名词对名词（kindness/punishment），动词对动词（builds/destroys）

## Part 5. 语法聚焦 (Grammar Focus)

### 聚焦1：虚拟语气在疑问句中的应用

**原句：** "Should he decrease his standard of ethics to protect his business?"

**聚焦：** Should在疑问句中表达道德困境或建议请求

**用法说明：**
"Should"在疑问句中有多重功能：

1. **征求建议：** "Should I call her?" (我应该给她打电话吗?)
2. **表达不确定：** "Should this be happening?" (这应该发生吗?)
3. **道德困境：** "Should he sacrifice his values?" (他应该牺牲价值观吗?)

**原句分析：**
Marcus的问题不是简单的行动选择，而是道德层面的深刻困境。"Should"暗示：
- 这个决定有道德维度
- 没有明显的"正确"答案
- 需要深思熟虑

**对比：**
- **Can** he decrease his standard? = 能力问题（他有能力这么做吗？）
- **Will** he decrease his standard? = 意愿/预测（他会这么做吗？）
- **Should** he decrease his standard? = 道德问题（他应该这么做吗？）

---

### 聚焦2：Work off的用法

**原句：** "I will permit you to work off your debt here."

**聚焦：** Phrasal verb "work off" 的含义和用法

**含义：** 通过工作来偿还、消除或减轻某物

**常见搭配：**
1. **Work off debt** = 通过工作偿还债务
   - Example: He worked off his debt by doing extra shifts.

2. **Work off stress/anger** = 通过活动释放压力/愤怒
   - Example: She goes jogging to work off stress.

3. **Work off calories** = 通过运动消耗卡路里
   - Example: I need to work off that big lunch!

**语法结构：**
- Work off + 名词（不可分离）
- 强调通过努力"消除"或"偿还"某物

**同义表达：**
- Pay off (debt) = 还清（更直接）
- Burn off (calories) = 消耗
- Release (stress) = 释放

**为什么用work off：**
原句中"work off"不仅表示偿债，还暗含着：
- 通过劳动获得尊严
- 债务不是一次性清除，而是逐渐"work off"
- 强调过程而非结果

## Part 6. 延伸练习 (Practice & Reflection)

### 6.1 理解题 (Comprehension)

1. **Who** is Marcus, and what kind of business does he run?
   - Marcus runs a simple restaurant with a base menu including potato dishes and fresh bread, emphasizing honest food.

2. **Why** did the server steal money from the restaurant?
   - He had a family emergency with overwhelming medical bills that created a chain of debt he couldn't break.

3. **What** choice does Marcus face when he discovers the theft?
   - He must decide whether to call the police and impose the penalty, or to try to understand what happened and offer another chance.

4. **What if** Marcus had immediately called the police?
   - The server would likely have faced severe penalties and had no chance for redemption or transformation.

5. **How** does the story illustrate that "all choices create chains"?
   - It shows how one wrong choice creates consequences (negative chain), but choosing kindness can create a chain of positive transformation and redemption.

### 6.3 写作任务 (Writing Prompt)

Write a short paragraph (80-100 words) about a time when someone showed you mercy or gave you a second chance, or when you did this for someone else. Use at least 5 words from this group's vocabulary list.

**参考示例：**
When I was starting my career, I made a pretty big mistake that cost my employer profit. The penalty could have been termination, but my manager chose a better path. She permitted me to learn from the error rather than punishing me. Her speech about growth through mistakes helped raise my confidence. The difference between her approach and standard punishment was enormous. I learned that nature gives us chances to grow, and we should offer the same to others. This chain of kindness differs from harsh judgment—one builds character, the other breaks spirit.

### 6.4 讨论问题 (Discussion)

**Question:** Marcus learns that "wealthy people measure profit in money, but true wealth comes from giving second chances." Do you agree that there are forms of wealth beyond money? What are they, and how do they differ from financial wealth?

**思考提示：**
- Consider different types of "wealth": relationships, reputation, knowledge, peace of mind
- Think about whether financial wealth and other forms of wealth are互斥 (mutually exclusive) or can coexist
- Reflect on the concept of "investing" in people versus investing in assets
- Discuss whether second chances are always deserved or if there should be limits
- Consider how Marcus's decision affects not just the server but the entire community
- Think about the "utility" (usefulness) of mercy versus strict justice in society
