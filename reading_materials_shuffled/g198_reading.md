## Part 1. Story (英文原文)

Maya walked into the library, her **hand** /hænd/ (n. 手；v. 递给) clutching a folder of **photography** /fəˈtɑːɡrəfi/ (n. 摄影；摄影术) samples. She had an **advance** /ədˈvæns/ (n. 前进；预付；v. 前进) appointment with the city's cultural **panel** /ˈpænl/ (n. 专家组；面板), hoping they would fund her documentary project. The **criteria** /kraɪˈtɪriə/ (n. 标准；准则) for selection were strict, and she felt her palms sweating.

"This should be **fun** /fʌn/ (n. 乐趣；adj. 有趣的)," her friend Jake had joked before she left. But Maya knew it wasn't just **fun** /fʌn/ (n. 乐趣)—it was serious. She wanted to document the **secular** /ˈsekjələr/ (adj. 世俗的；非宗教的) traditions of immigrant communities, showing how culture survived **differently** /ˈdɪfrəntli/ (adv. 不同地) in new lands.

The **spokesman** /ˈspoʊksmən/ (n. 发言人) for the panel, Mr. Chen, greeted her warmly. "Welcome," he said. "**Please** /pliːz/ (v. 请；使高兴) have a seat." He gestured to a chair. Maya sat down, noticing a water **bottle** /ˈbɑːtl/ (n. 瓶子) on the table. "Would you like a **drink** /drɪŋk/ (n. 饮料；v. 喝)?" he offered. She declined politely.

Mr. Chen flipped through her portfolio. "These images are striking," he said. "The **demonstration** /ˌdemənˈstreɪʃn/ (n. 示范；游行) of emotion in each **photo** /ˈfoʊtoʊ/ (n. 照片) is powerful." He **paused** /pɔːz/ (v. 暂停；n. 暂停), studying a picture of an elderly woman cooking. "What's your **vice** /vaɪs/ (n. 恶习；缺点) as a photographer? What do you struggle with?"

Maya thought carefully. "Sometimes I **borrow** /ˈbɑːroʊ/ (v. 借；借用) too heavily from documentary styles I admire. I want to find my **own** /oʊn/ (adj. 自己的) voice." Mr. Chen nodded approvingly. "That's **necessarily** /ˌnesəˈserəli/ (adv. 必然地；必定) part of growth. Authenticity comes with time."

Another panel member, Dr. Kim, spoke up. "Your project focuses on **data** /ˈdeɪtə/ (n. 数据；资料)—interviews, oral histories. But will there be narrative?" Maya explained her vision: weaving together stories with images, letting subjects speak for themselves. "The **trade** /treɪd/ (n. 贸易；行业；v. 交易)-off," she said, "is that I sacrifice some artistic control for authentic voices."

"Interesting," Dr. Kim murmured. "And you plan to work with former **inmate** /ˈɪnmeɪt/ (n. 囚犯；同住者)-s who are **sexually** /ˈsekʃuəli/ (adv. 性方面地；两性之间) diverse communities? That's **check** /tʃek/ (v. 检查；核对；n. 支票) bold." Maya nodded. "Many have been marginalized. Their stories matter."

The panel seemed impressed. Mr. Chen mentioned that the project could even feature at the upcoming **Olympic** /əˈlɪmpɪk/ (adj. 奥林匹克的) cultural showcase—a **diplomatic** /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/ (adj. 外交的；圆滑的) gesture of inclusion. Maya's heart raced. That would give her work incredible visibility.

As the meeting concluded, Dr. Kim smiled. "We'll **check** /tʃek/ (v. 检查；核对) your references and review the **chemical** /ˈkemɪkl/ (adj. 化学的；n. 化学制品)... sorry, I meant financial breakdown." Everyone laughed at the slip. "We'll be in touch soon. This isn't **necessarily** /ˌnesəˈserəli/ (adv. 必然地) a rejection or approval yet—we need to follow procedure."

Maya left feeling hopeful. Outside, she found Jake waiting with coffee. "How did it go?" he asked. "**Please** /pliːz/ (v. 请) tell me you got it!" Maya smiled. "Not yet. But they liked my work. They asked good questions, which means they're thinking **differently** /ˈdɪfrəntli/ (adv. 不同地) about what documentary can be."

Jake handed her the coffee. "You know what? That **demonstration** /ˌdemənˈstreɪʃn/ (n. 示范) you gave—showing your work with such confidence—that alone proves you're ready." Maya took a sip. The **drink** /drɪŋk/ (n. 饮料) was sweet, much like the possibility of success. She had done her best. The rest was up to the **panel** /ˈpænl/ (n. 专家组).

Walking home, Maya thought about the **criteria** /kraɪˈtɪriə/ (n. 标准) she held for **hers** /hɜːrz/ (pron. 她的). Success wasn't just funding; it was staying true to her vision. And in that moment, she realized she'd already won something valuable: **her** /hɜːr/ (pron. 她的) **own** /oʊn/ (adj. 自己的) sense of purpose.

## Part 2. 故事翻译 (中文)

玛雅走进图书馆，手里紧握着一个装有摄影样本的文件夹。她与城市文化专家组有一个提前预约，希望他们能资助她的纪录片项目。评选标准很严格，她感到手心出汗。

"这应该很有趣，"她的朋友杰克在她离开前开玩笑说。但玛雅知道这不仅仅是有趣——这很严肃。她想记录移民社区的世俗传统，展示文化如何在新土地上以不同方式生存。

专家组的发言人陈先生热情地迎接她。"欢迎，"他说。"请坐。"他指着一把椅子。玛雅坐下，注意到桌上有一个水瓶。"你想喝点什么吗？"他提议。她礼貌地拒绝了。

陈先生翻阅她的作品集。"这些图像很震撼，"他说。"每张照片中情感的展示都很有力。"他停顿了一下，研究着一张老妇人做饭的照片。"作为摄影师，你的缺点是什么？你在哪方面挣扎？"

玛雅仔细思考。"有时我过度借鉴我欣赏的纪录片风格。我想找到自己的声音。"陈先生赞许地点点头。"这必然是成长的一部分。真实性需要时间。"

另一位专家组成员金博士说话了。"你的项目关注数据——访谈、口述历史。但会有叙事吗？"玛雅解释了她的愿景：将故事与图像交织在一起，让受访者为自己说话。"权衡，"她说，"是我牺牲一些艺术控制以换取真实的声音。"

"有意思，"金博士喃喃道。"你计划与曾经的囚犯以及性方面多元的社区合作？这很大胆。"玛雅点点头。"许多人被边缘化了。他们的故事很重要。"

专家组似乎印象深刻。陈先生提到该项目甚至可以在即将到来的奥林匹克文化展示中展出——这是一个包容的外交姿态。玛雅的心跳加速。那会给她的作品带来难以置信的可见度。

会议结束时，金博士微笑了。"我们会核查你的推荐信并审查化学...抱歉，我是说财务明细。"每个人都对这个口误笑了。"我们很快会联系你。这不一定是拒绝或批准——我们需要遵循程序。"

玛雅离开时充满希望。外面，她发现杰克拿着咖啡在等。"怎么样？"他问。"请告诉我你得到了！"玛雅微笑。"还没有。但他们喜欢我的作品。他们问了很好的问题，这意味着他们对纪录片的可能性有了不同的思考。"

杰克把咖啡递给她。"你知道吗？你的那个展示——如此自信地展示你的作品——这本身就证明你已经准备好了。"玛雅抿了一口。这杯饮料很甜，就像成功的可能性一样。她已经尽力了。剩下的就看专家组了。

走回家的路上，玛雅想起了她对自己持有的标准。成功不仅仅是资助；而是忠于自己的愿景。在那一刻，她意识到她已经赢得了有价值的东西：她自己的目标感。

## Part 4. 重点句讲解

### 句子 1
**原句:** "She wanted to document the secular traditions of immigrant communities, showing how culture survived differently in new lands."

**分析:** "want to do"表示意愿。现在分词"showing"作伴随状语，说明记录的方式或目的。"how"引导宾语从句，说明生存的方式。

**亮点:** 通过分词短语连接两个相关的动作，使句子流畅而富有层次。

### 句子 2
**原句:** "What's your vice as a photographer? What do you struggle with?"

**分析:** 两个疑问句，第二个是对第一个的补充解释。"as a photographer"介词短语说明身份。第二句用"struggle with"使问题更具体、更容易理解。

**亮点:** 用两个问题逐步深入，先抽象后具体，展现了有技巧的提问方式。

### 句子 3
**原句:** "Authenticity comes with time."

**分析:** 简单句，使用"come with"表示"伴随...而来"。抽象名词"authenticity"作主语，使表达富有哲理性。

**亮点:** 简短有力的格言式表达，适合用于总结和启发。

### 句子 4
**原句:** "The trade-off is that I sacrifice some artistic control for authentic voices."

**分析:** "trade-off"表示"权衡；交换"。"that"引导表语从句。"sacrifice A for B"表示"牺牲A以换取B"。

**亮点:** 用"trade-off"精准表达了艺术创作中常见的两难选择。

### 句子 5
**原句:** "Success wasn't just funding; it was staying true to her vision."

**分析:** 分号连接两个对比的判断句。"not just... it was..."结构强调真正的重点。动名词"staying"作表语。

**亮点:** 通过对比重新定义"成功"，升华了主题，展现角色的成长。

## Part 5. 语法聚焦（Grammar Focus）

### 焦点 1: 现在分词作伴随状语

**原句:** "She wanted to document the secular traditions of immigrant communities, showing how culture survived differently in new lands."

**聚焦:** 现在分词 (showing) 作伴随状语

**用法:** 现在分词短语可以作伴随状语，说明主要动作的方式、结果或伴随情况。它与主句主语保持逻辑上的主动关系。

**可替换句:** "She wanted to document the secular traditions of immigrant communities and show how culture survived differently in new lands."

**区别:** 并列结构"and show"更平直，分词短语更强调"showing"是"document"的方式或目的。

### 焦点 2: "not just... it was..." 强调结构

**原句:** "Success wasn't just funding; it was staying true to her vision."

**聚焦:** 否定+真正重点的强调结构

**用法:** 先否定表面或次要的内容，然后用"it was"强调真正的重点。这种结构常用于表达深层认识或价值观的转变。

**可替换句:** "Success was more than just funding; it was about staying true to her vision."

**区别:** "more than"版本较温和，原句的否定式更有力，对比更鲜明。

## Part 6. 延伸练习（Practice & Reflection）

### 6.1 理解题（Comprehension）

1. **What** was Maya's documentary project about?
2. **Why** did Mr. Chen ask about Maya's "vice" as a photographer?
3. **What** "trade-off" did Maya mention in her approach?
4. **How** did Maya redefine success by the end of the story?
5. **What if** Maya had compromised her vision to please the panel—would that have been a better strategy?

### 6.2 词汇练习（Vocabulary Practice）

1. The company **spokesman** /ˈspoʊksmən/ announced the new policy.
   - 公司发言人宣布了新政策。

2. Can I ___________ (borrow/trade) your camera for the weekend?
3. The ___________ (panel/demonstration) of experts will review all applications.
4. She gave a ___________ (diplomatic/chemical) answer to avoid offending anyone.
5. We need to ___________ (check/pause) the ___________ (data/fun) before making a decision.
6. ___________ (Necessarily/Differently), everyone approaches problems ___________ (differently/sexually).

### 6.3 写作任务（Writing Prompt）

Write a short paragraph (80-100 words) about pursuing a creative goal or passion. What challenges did you face, and what did you learn? Try to use at least 5 words from this group's vocabulary list.

**提示词:** fun, demonstration, criteria, borrow, advance

### 6.4 讨论问题（Discussion)

> **"In creative work, is it better to follow established styles and criteria, or to risk failure by pursuing your unique vision?"**

Think about:
- How did Maya balance learning from others with finding her own voice?
- What role does authenticity play in artistic success?
- Can you think of artists or creators who succeeded by being different?

**学习建议 (Study Tips):**

1. 学习现在分词作伴随状语，让句子更紧凑自然

3. 注意抽象名词(authenticity, criteria等)在哲理性表达中的使用
4. 观察如何通过对话推动情节和展现角色性格
