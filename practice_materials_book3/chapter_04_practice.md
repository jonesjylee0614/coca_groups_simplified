## Part 5: Grammar Focus

### Grammar Point 1: Modal Verbs for Necessity and Obligation

**Explanation**: We use modal verbs (must, have to, need to, should) to express different levels of necessity and obligation.

**Examples from the story**:
- "We **must** fix this quickly." (strong necessity)
- "You **need to** think about scalability." (necessary)
- "We **should** establish clearer processes." (advisable)
- "They **had to** work from coffee shops." (past obligation)

**Practice**:
Complete with the correct modal verb:

1. The team ________ (must/might) expand their office space soon.
2. Jake ________ (should/could) have designed for scalability from the start.
3. During construction, they ________ (had to/might) work from various locations.
4. Emily thinks they ________ (need to/may) establish formal processes.
5. Dr. Chen said they ________ (must/should) maintain quality before expanding.

### Grammar Point 2: Present Perfect for Recent Events and Their Impact

**Explanation**: We use present perfect (has/have + past participle) to talk about recent events that have present relevance.

**Examples from the story**:
- "Five schools **have contacted** us."
- "The system **has crashed** again."
- "We **have lost** three hours of grading work."
- "Competition **has started** to notice us."

**Practice**:
Complete with present perfect or past simple:

1. The office ________ (become) too small after they ________ (hire) two new people.
2. Marcus ________ (show) humility since the crisis ________ (begin).
3. We ________ (face) many challenges, but we ________ (overcome) them together.
4. Jake ________ (learn) an important lesson when the database ________ (crash).
5. The team ________ (grow) stronger after they ________ (survive) their first crisis.

### Grammar Point 3: Gerunds and Infinitives After Verbs

**Explanation**: Some verbs are followed by gerunds (-ing), some by infinitives (to + verb), and some by either with different meanings.

**Examples from the story**:
- "They decided **to hire** both." (infinitive)
- "They began **hiring** in February." (gerund)
- "They struggled **to maintain** quality." (infinitive)
- "They finished **building** the new facility." (gerund)

**Practice**:
Complete with the correct form:

1. They need ________ (expand) carefully.
2. Marcus suggested ________ (redesign) the interface.
3. Jake admitted ________ (make) a mistake.
4. The team struggled ________ (balance) speed with quality.
5. They finished ________ (move) into the new facility.
